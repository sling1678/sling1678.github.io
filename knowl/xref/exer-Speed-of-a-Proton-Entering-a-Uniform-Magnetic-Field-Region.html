Exercise 1. Speed of a Proton Entering a Uniform Magnetic Field Region.

A proton enters in the region between the poles of a horse-show magnet perpendicularly to the magnetic field direction. The trajectory of the protonâ€™s path is then fitted to be a circular arc of radius \(4\,\text{cm}\text{.}\) Suppose the magnetic field is uniform with magnitude \(0.025\, \text{T}\text{.}\) Find the speed of the proton. Data: \(m_p = 1.67 \times 10^{-27}\,\text{kg}\text{.}\)
Answer.
\(9.58\times 10^{4}\,\text{m/s}.\)
Solution.
Using the acceleration of the uniform circular motion to get acceleration we get the force to be
\begin{equation*} F = m_p\frac{v^2}{R}. \end{equation*}
This will equal
\begin{equation*} Q v B \sin\,90^\circ = Q v B. \end{equation*}
Therefore,
\begin{equation*} m_p\frac{v^2}{R} = Q v B. \end{equation*}
Solving for \(v\)
\begin{equation*} v = \frac{Q B R}{m_p} = 9.58\times 10^{4}\,\text{m/s}. \end{equation*}
