Exercise 2. Solving Capacitor Charging Differential Equation.

In a certain \(RC\) circuit the charge on the capacitor is found to build according to the following rate in charge per unit time. Here \(t\) is in msec.
\begin{equation*} \frac{dQ}{dt} = -2Q + 3. \end{equation*}
The capacitor plates initially contain \(\pm 5 \mu C\) charges. (a) What is the value of time constant of the circuit? (b) Find charges on the capacitor plates at \(t = 1.2\ ms\text{.}\) (c) Set up a circuit with resistor(s), capacitor(s), and voltage source(s) with appropriate values so that your proposed circuit obeys the equation given above. Ans: (a) \(\tau= 0.5\, \text{ms}\) , (b) \(6.36\ \mu\text{C}\text{.}\)
Solution 1. a
The given equation is an equation for charging a capacitor with a resistor in series with it.
\begin{equation*} R\:\dfrac{dq}{dt} = -\dfrac{1}{C}\:q + V. \end{equation*}
Therefore, we have
\begin{equation*} \tau = RC = \dfrac{1}{2}\:\textrm{ms}. \end{equation*}
Solution 2. b
Since there is some charge on the capacitor at \(t = 0\text{,}\) the solution will be modified a little bit with a charge \(5\: \mu\textrm{C}\) added.
\begin{equation*} Q(t) = C V \left( 1 - e^{-t\tau} \right) + 5\: \mu\textrm{C} \end{equation*}
The charge on the capacitors at \(t\) = 1.2 ms is then given by
\begin{align*} Q(t) \amp = RC\ \dfrac{V}{R} \left( 1 - e^{-t\tau} \right) + 5 \\ \amp = 0.5\:\textrm{ms}\times 3\:\mu\textrm{C} \left( 1 - e^{-t\tau} \right) + 5\: \mu\textrm{C} = 6.36\: \mu\textrm{C}. \end{align*}
Solution 3. c
All circuits with \(RC = 0.5\,\text{ms}\) and \(V/R = 3\, \mu\text{C/ms}\) will obey the given equation. We can pick a value of \(C\) to be concrete, which will completely fix \(R\) and \(V\text{.}\) Let \(C = 1\, \mu\text{F}\text{.}\) Then \(R = 0.5\,\text{k}\Omega\) and \(V = 1.5\, \text{V}\text{.}\)
