Example 45.30. Magnifying Power and F/# of a Reflecting Telescope.

(a) A reflecting telescope has a concave mirror with opening diameter of \(24\text{ inches}\) and focal length \(10\text{ feet}\text{.}\) What is the F/# of the telescope?
(b) The image in the reflector in (a) is to be viewed through a number of eyepieces of focal lengths \(4\text{ mm}\text{,}\) \(10\text{ mm}\text{,}\) \(25\text{ mm}\text{,}\) and \(35\text{ mm}\) are avilable. Find the magnifications in each case.
Answer.
(a) \(5\text{,}\) (b) \(762,\ 304.8,\ 121.9,\ 87.1\text{.}\)
Solution 1. (a)
The F/# is obtained from the ratio of focal length and the diameter of the aperture.
\begin{equation*} F/\# = \frac{f}{D} = \frac{10\times 12\text{ inches}}{ 24\text{ inches}} = 5. \end{equation*}
Solution 2. (b)
The magnification is obtained from the ratio of focal lengths.
\begin{equation*} |M| = \frac{f_\text{o}}{f_\text{e}}. \end{equation*}
We need to make sure the two are in the same unit so that the unit cancels out. Recall that \(1\text{ in} = 25.4\text{ mm}\text{.}\) Therefore, we get
\begin{align*} \amp |M|_4 = \frac{10\times 12 \times 25.4}{4} = 762, \\ \amp |M|_{10} = |M|_4 \times \frac{4}{10} = 304.8, \\ \amp |M|_{25} = |M|_4 \times \frac{4}{25} = 121.9,\\ \amp |M|_{35} = |M|_4 \times \frac{4}{35} = 87.1, \end{align*}
