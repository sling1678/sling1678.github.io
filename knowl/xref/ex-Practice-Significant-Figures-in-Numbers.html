Exercise 1. Practice Significant Figures in Numbers.

Evaluate the following to the correct number of significant figures/digits. (a) \(2.52+1.008\text{,}\) (b) \(3.01 \times 5\text{,}\) (c) \(2.51/ \pi\text{,}\) (d) \(\pi(1.5)^2\text{,}\) (e) \(3.5^{1/3}\text{,}\) (f) \(\sqrt{5.34}\text{,}\) (g) \(15.5\times(1.1)^{1/4}\text{,}\) (h) \(\pi^2\) (Trick question!).
Hint.
Use a calculator and round off to the lowest significant number in the expression.
Answer.
(a) 3.53, (b) 15.1, (c) 0.799,(d) 7.1,(e) 1.5,(f) 2.31,(g)\(1.6 \times 10^1\text{,}\) (h)\(9.86960440108935...\text{.}\)
Solution 1. (a)
(a)
\begin{equation*} 2.52 + 1.008 = 3.528 \rightarrow 3.53. \end{equation*}
I rounded to three digits since 2.52 has the lower precision of the two numbers being added.

(b)
\begin{equation*} 3.01 \times 5 = 15.05 \rightarrow 15.1. \end{equation*}

I rounded to three digits since 2.51 has the lower precision of the two numbers being multiplied. Number \(\pi\) is assumed to be exact.
Solution 4. (d)
(d)
\begin{equation*} \pi(1.5)^2 = \pi\times (1.5)^2 = 7.06858 \rightarrow 7.1. \end{equation*}
I rounded to two digits since 1.5 has the lower precision of the two numbers being multiplied. Number \(\pi\) is assumed to be exact.

(e)
