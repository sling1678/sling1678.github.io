Exercise 1. Induced EMF in a Metal Bar Moving in an External Magentic Field.

A metal bar of length \(25\, \text{cm}\) is placed perpendicular to a uniform magnetic field of strength \(3\, \text{T}\text{.}\) (a) Determine the induced EMF between the ends of the rod when it is not moving. (b) Determine the EMF when the rod if moving perpendicular to its length and magnetic field with a speed of \(50\, \text{cm/s}\text{.}\)
Answer.
(b) \(0.375 \ \textrm{V}\text{.}\)
Solution 1. a
Zero induced EMF since, no moving charge in the rod and hence no magnetic force to deflect them towards the ends of the metal bar.
Solution 2. b
The electron in the metal bar moving to the right experiences a magnetic force pointed up. Therefore electrons accumulate at the top and equal amount of positive charge develops at the bottom since the bar is electricically neutral. This polarization of the bar creates an electric field $vec E$ in the bar. The electrons will stop moving up the bar when the electric force balances the magnetic force. Let $v$ be the magnitude of the horizontal velocity of the rod and $B$ the magnetic field pointed out-of-page. Then the balancing of electric force to magnetic force on an electron in the bar gives
\begin{equation*} e\:E = e\:v\:B\ \ \Longrightarrow\ \ E = v\:B. \end{equation*}
The induce EMF set up between the ends of the rod can be obtained by the line integral of the electric field.
\begin{equation*} V = \int_0^L\:E\:dl = v\:B\:L = 0.5\:\textrm{m/s}\times 3\:\textrm{T}\times 0.25\:\textrm{m} = 0.375\:\textrm{V}. \end{equation*}
