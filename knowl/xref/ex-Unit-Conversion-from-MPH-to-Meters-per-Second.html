Exercise 2. Unit Conversion from MPH to Meters per Second.
A car travels at a speed of 65.0 miles per hour (mph). What is the speed in meters/second?
Hint.
Convert both units.
Answer.
\(29.1\,\text{m/s}.\)
Solution.
\begin{align*} 65.0\ \frac{\text{mi}}{\text{h}}\ \amp = 65.0\ \frac{1609.344\text{m}}{3600\text{s}}\\ \amp = 29.0576\,\frac{\text{m}}{\text{s}}\ \text{(from calculator)}\\ \amp = 29.1\,\text{m/s}. \ \text{[Rounding off to three digits.]} \end{align*}
Note that I rounded off the final number to three digits - I did that because the least precise number was 65.0 which has three significant digits.
