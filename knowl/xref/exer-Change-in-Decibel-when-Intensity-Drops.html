Exercise 1. Change in Decibel when Intensity Drops.

The intensity of sound drops by half. What is the change in dB?
Answer.
\(-3\,\text{dB}\text{.}\)
Solution.
We are given that \(I_2 = \frac{1}{2}I_1\text{,}\) and we are to find \(I_2\,\text{(dB)} - I_1\,\text{(dB)}\text{.}\) We will use the original formula relating the intensity to intensity in dB.
\begin{align*} \amp I_1 (\text{dB}) = 10 \log_{10}\left( \frac{I_1}{I_0}\right)\\ \amp _2 (\text{dB}) = 10 \log_{10}\left( \frac{I_2}{I_0}\right) \end{align*}
Therefore,
\begin{align*} I_2 (\text{dB}) - I_1 (\text{dB}) \amp = 10 \log_{10}\left( \frac{I_2}{I_0}\right) - 10 \log_{10}\left( \frac{I_1}{I_0}\right)\\ \amp = 10 \log_{10}\left( \frac{I_2}{I_1}\right)\\ \amp = 10 \log_{10}\left( \frac{1}{2}\right) = - 10 \log_{10}(2) = -3.0 \end{align*}
The intensity in dB decreases by 3 dB when the intensity becomes half in the units \(\text{W/m}^2\) regardless of the value of the reference intensity.
