Example 48.15. Intensity of Light in a Particular Direction behind a Single Slit.

A single slit of width \(3.0\, \mu\text{m}\) is illuminated by a sodium yellow light of wavelength \(589\, \text{nm}\text{.}\) Find the intensity at a \(15^{\circ}\) angle to the axis in terms of the intensity of the central maximum which can be taken to be \(1\) unit.
Answer.
\(0.04\)
Solution.
The intensity in the diffraction pattern of a single slit is given by
\begin{equation*} I(\theta) = I(0)\:\left( \dfrac{\sin\:\beta(\theta)}{\beta(\theta)}\right)^2, \end{equation*}
where \(\beta(\theta) = \pi\:b\:\sin\:\theta/\lambda\text{.}\) Let us find \(\beta\) for the given angle.
\begin{equation*} \beta(15^{\circ}) = \dfrac{\pi \times 3.0\: \mu\text{m}\:\sin\:15^{\circ}}{0.589\:\mu\text{m}}=4.14\:\text{rad}. \end{equation*}
Therefore, our answer will be
\begin{equation*} \dfrac{I(\theta)}{I(0)} = \left( \dfrac{\sin\: (4.14\:\text{rad} )}{4.14\:\text{rad}}\right)^2 = 0.04. \end{equation*}
