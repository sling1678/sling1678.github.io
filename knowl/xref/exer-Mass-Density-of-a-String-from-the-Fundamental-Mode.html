Exercise 2. Mass Density of a String from the Fundamental Mode.

A 2-m long string is under a tension of 300 N. It is found that the frequency of its fundamental tone of vibration is 250 Hz. Find the mass per unit length of the string.
Solution.
We have discussed above that the fundamental mode has wavelength equal to 2 times the length \(L\) and the speed of the wave is given by \(\sqrt{T/\mu}\text{.}\) Therefore, the frequency \(f_0\) of the mode is related to \(\mu\) as follows.
\begin{equation*} f_0 = \frac{v}{\lambda_0} = \frac{1}{2L} \sqrt{T/\mu}. \end{equation*}
Therefore,
\begin{equation*} \mu = \frac{T}{4 L^2 f_0^2} = \frac{300\ \text{N}}{4 (2\ \text{m})^2 (250\ \text{Hz})^2} =3.0\times 10^{-4}\ \text{kg/m}. \end{equation*}
