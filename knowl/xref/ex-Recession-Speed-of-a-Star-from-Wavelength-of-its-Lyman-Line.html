Checkpoint 49.46. Recession Speed of a Star from Wavelength of its Lyman Line.

A prominent emission of light from excited hydrogen atom occurs in the Lyman series line \(\text{L}_\alpha\text{.}\) Light corresponding to the \(\text{L}_\alpha\) line has a wavelength of 121.6 nm when the emitting atom has a low speed and considered to be at rest. The \(\text{L}_\alpha\) line in the light from some star is observed to have wavelength of 486.4 nm. Suppose the star is directly moving away from Earth, what is the speed of the star with respect to Earth?
Hint.
Use Doppler effect.
Answer.
\(2.647 \times 10^8\text{ m/s}\text{.}\)
Solution.
The shift in wavelength is due to Doppler efect.
\begin{equation*} \lambda = \lambda_0\: \sqrt{\dfrac{1+V/c}{1- V/c}}, \end{equation*}
Letâ€™s call \(x=V/c\) and \(z=\lambda/\lambda_0\) and solve for \(x\) in
\begin{equation*} \dfrac{1+x}{1- x} = z^2. \end{equation*}
This gives
\begin{equation*} x = \frac{z^2 - 1}{z^2 + 1}. \end{equation*}
Here
\begin{equation*} z = \frac{486.4\text{ nm}}{121.6\text{ nm}} = 4. \end{equation*}
Hence,
\begin{equation*} x = \frac{4^2 - 1}{4^2 + 1} = \frac{15}{17}. \end{equation*}
Therefore, speed of star with respect to Earth is
\begin{equation*} V = \frac{15}{17}\, c = 2.647 \times 10^8\text{ m/s}. \end{equation*}
