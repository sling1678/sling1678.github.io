Exercise 5. A Bead on a Rotating Ring not Sliding.

A bead of mass \(m\) slides on a ring of radius \(R\) meter without any friction. When the ring rotates about a vertical axis at a rate of \(\omega\) radians per sec, the bead is found to be stable in a circular motion of non-zero radius. Find the radius of the circle for the motion of the bead when it is not sliding in the ring in terms of the given quantities and g.
Solution.
The bead moves in a circle of radius \(r\) as shown in Figure 6.126. The radius of the circle in which the bead moves and the radius \(R\) of the ring are related as
\begin{equation*} r = R \sin\theta. \end{equation*}
The free-body-diagram of the bead shown in Figure 6.127 gives rise to the following equations of motion.
\begin{align*} \amp F_N\sin\theta = m \frac{v^2}{R} = m \omega^2 r. \\ \amp F_N\cos\theta - mg = 0. \end{align*}
Eliminating \(F_N\) from these equations we obtain
\begin{equation*} \tan\theta = \frac{\omega^2 r}{g}. \end{equation*}
Therefore,
\begin{equation*} \tan\theta = \frac{\omega^2 R}{g} \sin\theta. \end{equation*}
Cancelling \(\sin\theta\) gives
\begin{equation*} \cos\theta = \frac{g}{\omega^2 R}. \end{equation*}
Now, the radius of the circle of motion will be
\begin{align*} r \amp = R\sin\theta = R \sqrt{1-\cos^2\theta}\\ \amp = R\sqrt{1 - (g/\omega^2R)^2} \end{align*}
