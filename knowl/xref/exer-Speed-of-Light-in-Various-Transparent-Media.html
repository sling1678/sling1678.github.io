Exercise 1. Speed of Light in Various Transparent Media.

Determine the speed of the yellow light through water, Plexiglas and flint glass.
Answer.
\(2.25 \times 10^8\, \text{m/s}\text{,}\) \(1.99 \times 10^8\,\text{m/s}\text{,}\) \(1.90 \times 10^8\, \text{m/s}\text{.}\)
Solution.
The speed of light in a transparent material is obtained by dividing the speed in vacuum by the refractive index of the material according to \(v = \dfrac{c}{n}\text{.}\)
\begin{align*} \amp \textrm{In water: } v = \dfrac{ 3\times 10^{8}\:\textrm{m/s}}{4/3} = 2.25\times 10^{8}\:\textrm{m/s}.\\ \amp \textrm{In plexiglas: } v = \dfrac{ 3\times 10^{8}\:\textrm{m/s}}{1.51 } = 1.99 \times 10^{8}\:\textrm{m/s}.\\ \amp \textrm{In flint glass: } v = \dfrac{ 3\times 10^{8}\:\textrm{m/s}}{1.58 } = 1.90 \times 10^{8}\:\textrm{m/s}. \end{align*}
