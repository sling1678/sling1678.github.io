Exercise 4. Heat Transfer Through Two Rods Connected in Parallel Between Two Heat Baths.

Two heat baths at temperatures \(580\)K and \(380\)K are connected by steel and copper rods parallel to each other, each of length \(50\) cm and diameter \(4\) cm. The rods are insulated so that heat can flow only along their lengths. Find the power flux through each rod and the net power flux
Answer.
\(172,000 \textrm{W/m}^2\text{.}\)
Solution.
There are two channels for heat flow between the two baths. Therefore, the net flux for the heat flow will be
\begin{equation*} \Phi = \Phi_1 + \Phi_2, \end{equation*}
where
\begin{align*} \amp \Phi_1 = k_1 \frac{T_1-T_2}{L }\\ \amp \Phi_2 = k_2 \frac{T_1-T_2}{L } \end{align*}
Putting in the given numerical values we find
\begin{equation*} \Phi = (50 + 380)\frac{\textrm{W}}{\textrm{m.K}}\times\frac{200\ \textrm{K}}{0.5\ \textrm{m}} = 172\frac{\textrm{kW}}{\textrm{m}^2}. \end{equation*}
