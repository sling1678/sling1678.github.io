Example 14.38. Beats Between Sirens of two Police Cars.

Two police cars are producing sirens primarily at \(2000\,\text{Hz}\) when they are not moving. You hear a beat when one of the cars is standing still while the other car is moving towards you at a speed of \(40\, \text{m/s}\text{.}\) Determine the beat frequency. Use the speed of sound in air to be \(343\, \text{m/s}\text{.}\)
Answer.
\(265\, \text{Hz}\text{.}\)
Solution.
Just by using the formula for beats, we get
\begin{equation*} f_{\text{beat}} = \left| 2000\ \text{Hz} - \left( \frac{343}{343-40}\right) 2000\ \text{Hz} \right| = 265 \ \text{Hz}. \end{equation*}
