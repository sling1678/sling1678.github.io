Example 10.30. Radius of Gyration of a Sphere About an Axis Tangent to the Edge.

What is the radius of gyration of a solid sphere of mass \(M\) and radius \(R\) about an axis that is tangent to its surface? Assume the sphere to be of unform density.
Answer.
\(\sqrt{\frac{7}{5}}\, R\text{.}\)
Solution.
Recall the definition of radius of gyration. Itâ€™s the distance from the axis, usually denoted by \(R_G\text{,}\) such that moment of inertia about the axis is \(I = M R_G^2\text{.}\)
We will start with the previously known fact that moment of inertia of a sphere about an axis passing through the center is
\begin{equation*} I_0 = \frac{2}{5}MR^2. \end{equation*}
Now, we want moment of inertia about an axis \(A\) that is parallel to the axis through the CM but is at a distance \(R\) away. Hence, moment of inertia about \(A\) will be
\begin{equation*} I_A = I_0 + MR^2 = \frac{7}{5} MR^2. \end{equation*}
Now, to get radius of gyration, we equate this to \(MR_G^2\text{.}\) This gives us
\begin{equation*} R_G = \sqrt{\frac{7}{5}}\, R. \end{equation*}
