Exercise 9. Resistance of a Rectangular Slab in Three Different Directions.

A rectangular slab of copper has dimensions as shown in the figure. Find resistance in the three directions.
Answer.
\(1.13\times 10^{-1}\,\Omega\text{,}\) \(6.38\times 10^{-4}\,\Omega\text{,}\) \(2.83\times 10^{-4}\,\Omega\text{.}\)
Solution 1. a
The resistance formula tells us that resistance is proportional to the length and inversely proportional to the cross-sectional area. Both length and cross-sectional area depend upon the direction of the current. Hence, we have the following values of the resistance depending on the direction in which we pass the current even when the material is homogeneous and the resistivity is independent of direction.
\begin{equation*} R = 1.7\times 10^{-8}\:\Omega.\textrm{m}\times \dfrac{40\times 10^{-6}\:\textrm{m}}{(2\times 10^{-6}\:\textrm{m})(3\times 10^{-6}\:\textrm{m})} = 1.13\times 10^{-1}\:\Omega\text{.} \end{equation*}
Solution 2. b
\begin{equation*} R = 1.7\times 10^{-8}\:\Omega.\textrm{m}\times \dfrac{3\times 10^{-6}\:\textrm{m}}{(2\times 10^{-6}\:\textrm{m})(40\times 10^{-6}\:\textrm{m})} = 6.38\times 10^{-4}\:\Omega\text{.} \end{equation*}
Solution 3. c
\begin{equation*} R = 1.7\times 10^{-8}\:\Omega.\textrm{m}\times \dfrac{2\times 10^{-6}\:\textrm{m}}{(3\times 10^{-6}\:\textrm{m})(40\times 10^{-6}\:\textrm{m})} = 2.83\times 10^{-4}\:\Omega\text{.} \end{equation*}
