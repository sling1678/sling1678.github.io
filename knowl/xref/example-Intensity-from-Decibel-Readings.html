Example 14.26. Intensity from Decibel Readings.

Two speakers differ by \(3\,\text{dB}\text{.}\) How loud is the sound of one speaker compared to the other in terms of intensities?
Solution.
Solution No. 1: Let intensities of the two sounds be \(I_1\) and \(I_2\text{.}\) Then, we have
\begin{align*} \amp \text{dB}_1 = 10\,\log_{10} \left( \frac{I_1}{I_0} \right) \\ \amp \text{dB}_2 = 10\,\log_{10} \left( \frac{I_2}{I_0} \right) \end{align*}
We are given that \(\text{dB}_1 - \text{dB}_2 = 3\text{.}\) Therefore subtracting we get
\begin{align*} 3 \amp = 10\,\log_{10} \left( \frac{I_1}{I_0} \right) - 10\,\log_{10} \left( \frac{I_2}{I_0} \right) \\ \amp = 10\,\log_{10} \left( \frac{I_1}{I_2} \right) \end{align*}
Hence,
\begin{equation*} I_1 = 10^{0.3}\, I_2 \approx 2 \times I_2. \end{equation*}
Solution No. 2: Since we have two signals, we compare them directly to write the final equation.
\begin{equation*} 10\,\log_{10} \left( \frac{I_1}{I_2} \right) = 3\,\text{dB}. \end{equation*}
Then use the final step of Solution no. 1.
