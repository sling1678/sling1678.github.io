Exercise 2. Primary and Secondary of a Power Adapter for Driving Speakers.

Many electronic devices work on \(12\text{-V}\) DC. To get \(12\text{-V}\) DC from a \(120\text{-V}\) AC line (i.e. \(V_{\text{rms}} = 120 \text{V}\)), first the voltage is dropped to \(12\text{-V}\) AC (i.e. \(V_{\text{rms}} = 12 \text{V}\)) by using a transformer, and then the \(12\text{-V}\) AC is converted to \(12\text{-V}\) DC by using diodes in a rectifier circuit. Consider a speaker of resistance \(8\,\Omega\) connected to the \(12\text{-V}\) DC output of the rectifier.
(a) What must be the ratio of turns in the primary to secondary of the transformer?
(b) How much power is delivered to the device?
(c) Assuming no power loss anywhere else, what is the rms-current in the secondary?
Hint.
Use tranaformer equations.
Answer.
(a) \(10:1\text{,}\) (b) \(18\ \text{W}\text{,}\) (c) \(1.5\ \text{A}\text{.}\)
Solution 1. (a)
We need a step-down transformer. The ratio of primary to secondary turns will be 10:1 since we need one-tenth the voltage at the output.
Solution 2. (b)
The power in the \(8\:\Omega\) resistor will be \(\dfrac{V_{\text{rms}}^2}{R} = \dfrac{ (12\:\text{V})^2}{8\:\Omega} = 18\:\text{W}\text{.}\)
Solution 3. (c)
\(I = P/V = 18\:\text{W}/12\:\text{V} = 1.5\:\text{A}\text{.}\)
