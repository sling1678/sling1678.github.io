Example 7.75. Two Balls Stick Together Upon Collision.

Two balls A and B of mass $0.30$ kg and $0.40$ kg are moving towards each other with speeds $10$ m/s and $8$ m/s respectively. Upon collision, they stick together, and then move as one object after the collision. Find the speed and direction in which the pair moves.
Answer.
\(28.6\, \text{mm/s}\) opposite to A.
Solution.
Let the motion of the two balls intially be on the \(x\)-axis, with positive \(x\)-axis direction being the direction of ball Aâ€™s motion before the collision. Then, total momentum before the collision has only \(x\)-component.
\begin{equation*} p_{\text{tot},x}^\text{bef} = 0.3\times 10 + 0.4\times (-8) = -0.02\text{ kg.m/s}. \end{equation*}
This tells us that total momentum vector before the collision is (suppressing the units.)
\begin{equation*} \vec p_\text{bef} = -0.02\hat u_x. \end{equation*}
Since the two balls get stuck and move together, their final velocity must e same and mass will be the sum of the two masses. Let their velocity vector be denoted by \(\vec v\text{.}\) Hence, we must have
\begin{equation*} \vec p_\text{aft} = 0.7\,\vec v. \end{equation*}
Equating the the momenta we get
\begin{equation*} 0.7\,\vec v = -0.02\hat u_x. \end{equation*}
Hence, velocity after the collision is \(\vec v =-0.02/0.7 \hat u_x = -0.0286 \hat u_x\text{.}\) This give speed to be \(28.6\, \text{mm/s}\) and the direction of velocity is towards the negative \(x\)-axis, i.e., opposite to the initial direction of A.
