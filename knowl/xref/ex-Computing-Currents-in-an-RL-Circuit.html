Exercise 1. Computing Currents in an RL Circuit.

The switch in the circuit given in Figure 39.30 is closed at \(t = 0\text{.}\) Determine current through each resistor as \(t \rightarrow \infty\text{.}\)
Hint.
When steady state is reached, you can ignore the inductance.
Answer.
\(I = 0.257\:\text{A}\text{,}\) \(I_1 = 0.171\:\text{A}\text{,}\) \(I_2 = 0.086\:\text{A}\)
Solution.
With time the circuit approaches steady state. In the steady state, the current does not change, which means that there would be no self-inductance as \(t\rightarrow \infty\text{.}\)
If there is no self-inductance, we can ignore the presence of inductor in the circuit. The circuit simplifies to the resistors only as shown in Figure 39.31.
The equivalent circuit when steady state has reached can be readily solved for currents from techniques you have learned before. The current through the battery is simple the voltage of the battery divided by the equivalent resistance of the circuit.
The equivalent resistance is
\begin{equation*} R_{eq} = 20\,\Omega + \frac{40\,\Omega\times 80\,\Omega}{40\,\Omega+ 80\,\Omega} = 46.7\,\Omega. \end{equation*}
Therefore, current through the battery is
\begin{equation*} I = \frac{12\:\text{V}}{46.7\:\Omega} = 0.257\:\text{A}. \end{equation*}
Since a parallel circuit is a current divider, currents in the two branches are
\begin{align*} \amp I_1 =\left( \frac{80\,\Omega}{40\,\Omega+ 80\,\Omega}\right) 0.257\:\text{A} = 0.171\:\text{A}\\ \amp I_2 =\left( \frac{40\,\Omega}{40\,\Omega+ 80\,\Omega}\right) 0.257\:\text{A} = 0.086\:\text{A} \end{align*}
