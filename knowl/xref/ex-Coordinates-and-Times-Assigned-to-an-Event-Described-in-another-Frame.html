Checkpoint 49.28. Coordinates and Times Assigned to an Event Described in another Frame.

A train arrives at a location \(( x = x_0, y = 0, z = 0)\) at time \(t=10\text{ sec}\) in a frame S at rest with respect to the train station. A spaceship is passing by with velocity \((3,000\text{ m/s},0,0)\) with respect to train station. What will be the coordinates and time the pilot will assign to the event of the train arrival?
Hint.
Use Lorentz transformation.
Answer.
\(\left( 1 + \frac{1}{2}\times 10^{-10} \right) \times 10\text{ sec}\text{,}\) \(-\left( 1 + \frac{1}{2}\times 10^{-10} \right) \times 3\times 10^{4}\text{ m}\text{.}\)
Solution.
Let us denote Pilot’s frame by \(\text{S}^\prime\text{.}\) Then, this problem corresponds to Figure 49.24. Suppose frame S and \(\text{S}^\prime\) are coincident with zero reading of time in each frame. Denoting event in \(\text{S}^\prime\) by \((t', x', y', z')\) for event (0,0,0,0) in frame S, we have
\begin{align*} \amp t' = \dfrac{t - V x/c^2}{\sqrt{1 - V^2/c^2}} = \dfrac{t}{\sqrt{1 - V^2/c^2}} \\ \amp x' = \dfrac{x - Vt}{\sqrt{1 - V^2/c^2}} = \dfrac{- Vt}{\sqrt{1 - V^2/c^2}} \end{align*}
Since relative motion is only along common \(x\)-axis, no change will occur in \(y\) and \(z\) coordinates. To compute numrical value, we first compute \(\gamma\text{.}\)
\begin{equation*} \gamma = \dfrac{1}{\sqrt{1 - V^2/c^2}} = \dfrac{1}{\sqrt{1 -10^{-10}}} \approx 1 + \frac{1}{2}\times 10^{-10}. \end{equation*}
Therefore,
\begin{align*} \amp t' = \left( 1 + \frac{1}{2}\times 10^{-10} \right) \times 10\text{ sec}. \\ \amp x' = -\left( 1 + \frac{1}{2}\times 10^{-10} \right) \times 3\times 10^{4}\text{ m}. \end{align*}
