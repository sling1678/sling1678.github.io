Exercise 3. Compute Various Properties of an Electromagnetic Plane Wave Given its Magnetic Field.

The magnetic field of an electromagnetic wave traveling in vacuum is given by the following wave function.
\begin{equation*} \vec B = \hat u_y (5\times 10^{-7}\: \textrm{T})\cos\left(5\times 10^{10} x + \omega\: t\right), \end{equation*}
where \(\omega\) is the angular frequency in rad/sec, \(x\) in m, \(t\) in sec.
(a) Amplitude, (b) Frequency, (c) Wavelength, (d) Phase constant, (e) the direction of the travel, (f) the associated electric field wave, (g) the Poynting vector of the electromagnetic wave, (h) the intensity of the electromagnetic wave.
Solution.
(a) Amplitude, \(B_0= 5\times 10^{-7}\: \textrm{T}\text{.}\)
(b) Frequency, \(f = \dfrac{\omega}{2\pi} = \dfrac{c/k}{2\pi}\) \(=\) \(\dfrac{3\times10^{8}\:\textrm{m/s}}{2\pi\:5\times 10^{10}\textrm{m}^{-1}}\) \(=\) \(9.54\times 10^{-4}\:\textrm{Hz}\text{.}\)
(c) Wavelength, \(\lambda = \dfrac{2\pi}{k} = \dfrac{2\pi}{5\times 10^{10}\textrm{m}^{-1}} = 1.26\times 10^{-10}\:\textrm{m}\text{.}\)
(d) Phase constant, \(\phi = 0\text{.}\)
(e) The direction of the travel is towards the negative \(x\)-axis.
(f) The associated electric field wave.
\begin{equation*} \vec E = -\hat u_z E_0\:\cos\left(5\times 10^{10} x + \omega\: t\right), \end{equation*}
where
\begin{equation*} E_0 = 5\times 10^{-7}\: \textrm{T}\times 3\times 10^8\:\dfrac{\textrm{m}}{\textrm{s}} = 150\:\dfrac{\textrm{N}}{\textrm{C}},\ \ \omega = 0.006\:\dfrac{\textrm{rad}}{\textrm{sec}}. \end{equation*}
(g) The Poynting vector of the electromagnetic wave.
\begin{equation*} \vec S = \dfrac{1}{\mu_0}\: \vec E\times \vec B = -\hat u_x\:\dfrac{1}{\mu_0}\: c\: E_0^2\:\cos^{2}\left(5\times 10^{10} x + \omega\: t\right). \end{equation*}
(h) The intensity of the electromagnetic wave is the time-average of the power flux \(S\) given by the magnitude of \(\vec S\text{.}\) We find
\begin{equation*} I = \dfrac{1}{2}\:c\:\epsilon_0\:E_0^2 = 29.8\:\textrm{W/m}^2.. \end{equation*}
