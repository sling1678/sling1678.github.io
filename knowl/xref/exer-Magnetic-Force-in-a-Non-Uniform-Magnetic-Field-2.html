Exercise 8. Magnetic Force in a Non-Uniform Magnetic Field 2.

In the previous problem, ExerciseÂ 35.8.5.7, find the magnetic force if the magnetic field is \(B_x = c\text{,}\) \(B_y = a x\text{,}\) and \(B_z = b x\text{,}\) where \(a\text{,}\) \(b\) and \(c\) are constants.
Answer.
Magnitude \(\frac{IL^2}{2}\sqrt{a^2+b^2}\text{.}\)
Solution.
Now, it is best to work in the vector notation since we need to work out the cross product.
\begin{align*} d\vec F \amp = (I\:dx\:\hat u_x) \times (a\:x\: \hat u_y + b\: x\: hat u_z),\ \ (\textrm{since }\hat u_x \times \hat u_x = 0) \\ \amp = (-I b x dx) \hat u_y + (I a x dx ) \hat u_z. \end{align*}
Therefore, we obtain the following for the components of the net force.
\begin{equation*} F_y = - \dfrac{I b L^2}{2},\ \ F_y = \dfrac{I a L^2}{2}. \end{equation*}
The magnitude and direction of the net force can be readily obtained from these components.
