Example 6.32. Weight of a Dumb-bell-Shaped Object.

The object shown in the figure has two large masses, each of \(200\text{ kg}\text{,}\) with a relatively light bent bar, whose mass may be ignored.
(a) What is the weight of the dumb-bell?
(b) What does the weight of the dumb-bell act? Show by drawing an arrow with the tail at the point you think it acts.
Answer.
(a) \(3924\text{ N} \text{,}\) (b) See solution.
Solution 1. a
\begin{equation*} mg = 2\times 200\times 9.81 = 3924\text{ kg.m/s}^2 = 3924\text{ N}. \end{equation*}
