Exercise 1. Pressure from a Stream of Particles Striking on a Wall and Sticking to the Wall.

A stream of particles of mass \(m\) strikes a wall of area \(A\) perpendicularly with speed \(v\text{,}\) sticks to it and comes to rest. If there are \(n\) particles per unit volume, what is the pressure exerted on the wall? (Note the collision with the wall is not elastic.)
Hint.
Study Example 27.4.
Answer.
\(m n v^2 \text{.}\)
Solution.
If the particles stick to the wall, then the imparted impulse in the Example 27.4 will have \(v'=0\text{.}\) The pressure will now be given by
\begin{equation*} p = \frac{F_x}{A} = m n v^2. \end{equation*}
