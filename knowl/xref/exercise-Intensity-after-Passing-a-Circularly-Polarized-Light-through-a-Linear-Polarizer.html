Exercise 5. Intensity after Passing a Circularly Polarized Light through a Linear Polarizer.

A circularly polarized light has the following electric field.
\begin{equation*} \vec E = E_0 \left[ \hat u_x \cos\left( 2\pi f t - \frac{2\pi}{\lambda} z \right) + \hat u_y \sin\left( 2\pi f t - \frac{2\pi}{\lambda} z \right) \right] \end{equation*}
The wave is passed through a linear polarizer whose polarizing axis is pointed \(45^\circ\) counterclockwise to the \(x\) axis. Find the intensity of the emergent wave in terms of the intensity of the incident wave.
Hint.
Note dot product with a unit vector is a good way to find the componet in some direction.
Answer.
\(\frac{ c\:\epsilon_0\:E_0^2}{2}\)
Solution.
Let \(\hat u\) be a unit vector in the direciton of the polarizer. Here
\begin{equation*} \hat u =\frac{\hat u_x + \hat u_y}{\sqrt{2}}. \end{equation*}
The dot product of \(\vec E\) with this unit vector we get
\begin{equation*} \vec E \cdot \hat u = \frac{E_0}{\sqrt{2}} \left[ \cos\left( 2\pi f t - \frac{2\pi}{\lambda} z \right) + \sin\left( 2\pi f t - \frac{2\pi}{\lambda} z \right) \right]. \end{equation*}
The time average of square of this will be
\begin{equation*} \langle ( \vec E \cdot \hat u )^2 \rangle = \frac{E_0^2}{2}. \end{equation*}
The intensity will be
\begin{equation*} I = c\:\epsilon_0\langle ( \vec E \cdot \hat u )^2 \rangle = \frac{ c\:\epsilon_0\:E_0^2}{2}. \end{equation*}
