Example 24.25. Change in Internal Energy and Temperature in a Joule-Thomson Throttling Process.

In a throttling process, \(2.5\) moles of Argon is expanded from a high pressure \(30\) atm and volume \(2\) L to \(1\) atm and volume \(70\) L. (a) What is the change in the internal energy? (b) Interpret your answer and determine whether the gas will cool off or heat up.
Solution 1. a
The enthalpy is conserved in the Joule-Thompson throttle.
\begin{equation*} U_1 + p_1V_1 = U_2 + p_2V_2. \end{equation*}
Therefore, the change in the internal energy of the gas will be
\begin{align*} U_2 - U_1 \amp = p_1V_1 - p_2V_2 = 30\ \textrm{atm} \times 2\ \textrm{L} - 1\ \textrm{atm} \times 70\ \textrm{L}\\ \amp = - 10 \ \textrm{L . atm} = - 1013\ \textrm{J}. \end{align*}
Solution 2. b
Since the gas loses internal energy which is a measure of the motion of the molecules the gas molecules will slow down and hence the gas will cool off.
