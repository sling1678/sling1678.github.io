Exercise 5. Magnetic Field of Current in a Finite Wire by Biot Savart Law Ex2.

Find magnetic field at an arbitrary point P by a straight wire section AB of length \(L\) in a circuit carrying a steady current \(I\) as shown in Figure 36.26. (Ignore the contribution of other parts of the circuit.)
Answer.
Magnitude: \(\frac{\mu_0 I}{4\pi} \left[ \frac{L-h}{\sqrt{s^2+(L-h)^2}} +\frac{h}{\sqrt{s^2+h^2}} \right]\text{,}\) Direction: into-the-page.
Solution.
This is same problem as Exercise 36.1.3.4 above if we pick the origin at the same horizontal level as P and perform the integration from \(y=-h\) to \(y = L-h\) rather than from \(y=0\) to \(y=L\text{.}\)
The Biot-Savart integral from the wire AB will be
\begin{align*} \vec B \amp = \dfrac{\mu_0}{4\pi}\:\int_{-h}^{L-h}\:\dfrac{I\:dy\:\hat u_y \times \left( s\:\hat u_x - y\: \hat u_y \right)}{\left(s^2 + y^2 \right)^{3/2}}\ \ \ \textrm{(from the current in AB)} \\ \amp = - \hat u_z\: \dfrac{\mu_o\: I}{4\pi}\:\left[ \dfrac{L-h}{s\sqrt{s^2 + (L-h)^2}} + \dfrac{h}{s\sqrt{s^2 + h^2}} \right]. \end{align*}
