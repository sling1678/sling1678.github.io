Exercise 1. Recoil Force on the Gun by Exiting Bullet.

A gun of mass \(1.5\) kg fires bullets of mass \(100\) grams each. The bullets come out at a speed of \(400\) m/s. If each firing lasts \(200\) msec, what is the average recoil force on the gun? The recoil force is the force applied by the exiting bullet on the gun.
Hint.
Find \(J\) on bullet by \(\Delta p\) of bullet.
Answer.
\(200\text{ N}\text{,}\) towards gun.
Solution.
The change in momentum of the bullet is as a result of impulse by the gun. By third law, the impulse of the recoil force on the gun has the same magnitude, although in the opposite drection. Thus, from the magnitude of the change in the momentum of the bullet, we get the magnitude of the impulse of the recoil force.
\begin{equation*} J = 0.1\times 400 - 0 = 40\text{ N.s}. \end{equation*}
The firing process lasts \(\Delta t = 0.2\text{ s}\text{.}\) Therefore, the average recoil force has the magnitude give by
\begin{equation*} F_\text{av} = \dfrac{J}{\Delta t} = 200\text{ N}. \end{equation*}
The direction of the recoil force on the gun is obviously towards the gun.
