Exercise 8. Tipping Angle of a Box on an Incline.

Figure 16.13 shows a rectangular box of cross-sectional dimensions \(a\times b\) on an inclined surface whose angle of incline \(\theta\) can be varied. If \(\mu_s \gt \tan\theta\text{,}\) the box will not slide. Even when box would not slide, at some angle \(\theta_c\text{,}\) box will tip over. Find this angle.
Hint.
Torque about bottom right corner will not be balanced if angle is larget than the critical angle.
Answer.
\(\tan^{-1}\left( b/a\right)\text{.}\)
Solution.
Figure 16.14 shows force diagrams on the box when the incline is (a) at critical angle and (b) at an angle greater than the critical angle. At the critial angle, the weight vector is pointed towards the bottom right corner of the box. At higher angles, weight vector is to the right of the bottom right corner, which makes balancing of torque of the three forces impossible.
From the triangle in Figure 16.14(a), we see that
\begin{equation*} \tan\theta_c = \frac{b}{a}. \end{equation*}
Therefore,
\begin{equation*} \theta_c = \tan^{-1}\left( b/a\right). \end{equation*}
