Exercise 1. Velocity and Acceleration of a Projectile from Position as Function of Time.

The position of a projectile in the air is monitored with respect to a reference point in a Cartesian coordinate system. The \(x\)-axis of the Cartesian coordinate system is in the horizontal direction and the \(y\)-axis vertically up. The \(z\)-coordinate of the projectile does not change with time. The data for the \(x\) and \(y\)-coordinates are plotted and the following functions fit the \(x\) vs \(t\) and \(y\) vs \(t\) curves: \(x(t) = 40 t\) and \(y(t) = 60 t - 5 t^2\text{.}\) (a) Find the instantaneous velocity of the projectile at the following instants: (i) \(t=0\text{,}\) (ii) \(t=4\ \textrm{sec}\text{,}\) (iii) \(t=8\ \textrm{sec}\text{,}\) and (iv) \(t=10\ sec\text{.}\) (b) Find the instantaneous acceleration of the projectile at the following instants: (i) \(t=0\text{,}\) (ii) \(t=4\ \textrm{sec}\text{,}\) (iii) \(t=8\ \textrm{sec}\text{,}\) and (iv) \(t=10\ \textrm{sec}\text{.}\)
Solution 1. a
Calculating the derivatives we find
\begin{align*} \amp v_x = 40;\ v_y = 60 - 10 t;\ v_z = 0\ \ \Longrightarrow \vec v = 40\ \hat u_x + (60 - 10 t)\ \hat u_y\ \ [\textrm{m/s}]\\ \amp a_x = 0;\ a_y = - 10 ;\ v_z = 0\ \ \Longrightarrow \vec a = - 10 \ \hat u_y\ \ [\textrm{m/s}^2] \end{align*}
Plug in the values of \(t\) for the components of the instantaneous velocity and acceleration at particular instants. The magnitudes and directions of the velocity and acceleration can be obtained in the usual way.
(i) \(v_x(0) = 40\ \textrm{m/s}\text{,}\) \(v_y(0) = 60\ \textrm{m/s}\text{,}\)\(\ v_z(0) = 0\text{,}\)
\begin{equation*} v(0) = \sqrt{(40\ \textrm{m/s})^2 + (60\ \textrm{m/s})^2} = 72\ \textrm{m/s}, \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \theta = \tan^{-1}\left(\frac{60\ \textrm{m/s}}{40\ \textrm{m/s}} \right)=56^{\circ}. \end{equation*}
(ii) \(v_x(4) = 40\ \textrm{m/s}\text{,}\) \(v_y(4) = 20\ \textrm{m/s}\text{,}\) \(v_z(4) = 0\text{,}\) \(v(4) = 45\ \textrm{m/s}\text{,}\) \(\theta = 27^{\circ}\text{.}\)
(iii) \(v_x(8) = 40\ \textrm{m/s}\text{,}\) \(v_y(8) = -20\ \textrm{m/s}\text{,}\) \(v_z(8) = 0\text{,}\) \(v(8) = 45\ \textrm{m/s}\text{,}\) \(\theta = 333^{\circ}\text{.}\)
(iv) \(v_x(10) = 40\ \textrm{m/s}\text{,}\) \(v_y(10) = -40\ \textrm{m/s}\text{,}\) \(v_z(10) = 0\text{,}\) \(v(8) = 72\ \textrm{m/s}\text{,}\) \(\theta = 315^{\circ}\text{.}\)
Solution 2. b
The instantaneous accelerations are computed in a similar way as (a).
