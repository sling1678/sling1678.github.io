Exercise 2. Resolving Power Required in a Fabry Perot Instrument for Separating Sodium Yellow Doublet.

Sodium yellow doublet consists of wavlengths \(0.5889950\,\mu\text{m}\) and \(0.5895924\,\mu\text{m}\text{.}\) These lines are to be separated by a Fabry-Perot interferometer that has air as its medium and length of cavity is \(1.0\text{ cm}\text{.}\) What is the minimum resolving power and finesse of a Fabry-Perot interferometer used to resolve these lines?
Hint.
Use definition of resolving power.
Answer.
\(982,\ 0.03\text{.}\)
Solution.
Resolving power we need is
\begin{equation*} \frac{\lambda_\text{av}}{|\Delta\lambda|} = \frac{0.5889950 + 0.5895924}{ 2(0.5895924 - 0.5889950 )} = 982. \end{equation*}
The finesse can be obtained by equating this to \({\cal{F}} \times 2nd/\lambda_\text{av}\text{.}\)
\begin{equation*} {\cal{F}} = 982 \times \frac{(0.5889950 + 0.5895924)\times 10^{-6}/2 }{ 2 \times 1.0\times 1.0\times 10^{-2}} = 0.03. \end{equation*}
