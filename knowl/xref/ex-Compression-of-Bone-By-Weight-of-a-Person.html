Exercise 2. Compression of Bone By Weight of a Person.

Suppose a \(100\text{-kg}\) person supports all her weight on one leg. Assuming the weight is supported by the long bone of the thigh (called femur) and modeling the bone as a solid cylinder of diameter \(5\text{ cm}\) and length \(30\text{ cm}\text{.}\) Data: Youngâ€™s modulus of cortical bone = \(250 \text{ MPa}\text{.}\)
By how much is the length of the bone compressed?
Hint.
Convert everything in SI units and then use the definitions.
Answer.
\(0.6\text{ mm}\)
Solution.
We first compile the numbers we need in the SI units.
\begin{align*} \amp F = m g = 100\times 9.81 = 981\text{ N}, \\ \amp A = \dfrac{\pi d^2}{4} = 1.96\times 10^{-3}\text{ m}^2,\\ \amp Y = 250 \text{ MPa} = 2.50\times 10^{8}\text{ Pa},\\ \amp L = 0.30\text{ m}. \end{align*}
Therefore
\begin{align*} \Delta L\amp = \dfrac{L}{Y} \dfrac{F}{A} \\ \amp = \dfrac{0.30}{2.50\times 10^{8}}\ \dfrac{981}{1.96\times 10^{-3}} = 6.0\times 10^{-4}\text{ m}. \end{align*}
The shrinkage is \(0.6\text{ mm}\text{.}\)
