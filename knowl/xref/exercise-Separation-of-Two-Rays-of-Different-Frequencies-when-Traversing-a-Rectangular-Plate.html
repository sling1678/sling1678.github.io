Exercise 5. Separation of Two Rays of Different Frequencies when Traversing a Rectangular Plate.

A ray of light consisting of two wavelengths of light (\(700\text{ nm}\) and \(400\text{ nm}\) in air) is incident at \(30^\circ\) to the normal on a rectangular flint glass plate of width \(5\text{ cm}\text{,}\) and emerges on the other side as shown in Figure 43.25 of Exercise 43.7.3.4. The refractive indices of flint glass for \(700\text{-nm}\) and \(400\text{-nm}\) light are \(1.62\) and \(1.67\) respectively.
Find (a) direction(s) of the emergent ray, and (b) the lateral displacement(s) d from the original direction. (c) Using a protractor and a ruler, draw as accurately as you can the rays you found in exercise above.
Hint.
Find displacements of each ray separately as in Exercise 43.7.3.4.
Answer.
\(d_{\text{R}} = 1.09\text{ cm}\text{,}\) \(d_{\text{B}} = 1.14\text{ cm}\text{.}\)
Solution.
Refer to the figures in the Exercise 43.7.3.4. There will be one such ray for each color. Let \(\theta = \theta_{\text{R}}\) for the red light of wavelength \(700\text{ nm}\) and \(\theta = \theta_{\text{B}}\) for the blue light of wavelength \(400\text{ nm}\text{.}\) These angles in glass will be
\begin{align*} \amp \theta_{\text{R}} = \sin^{-1}\left( \dfrac{1\times \sin\:30^{\circ}}{1.62}\right) = 18^{\circ}.\\ \amp \theta_{\text{B}} = \sin^{-1}\left( \dfrac{1\times \sin\:30^{\circ}}{1.67}\right) = 17.4^{\circ}. \end{align*}
The displacemets of the two rays when they exit on the other side would be \(d = d_{\text{R}}\) and \(d = d_{\text{B}}\) given by
\begin{align*} \amp d_{\text{R}} = (5\:\text{cm})\:\left( \tan\:30^{\circ} - \tan\:18^{\circ}\right)\:\cos\:30^{\circ} = 1.09\:\text{cm}.\\ \amp d_{\text{B}} = (5\:\text{cm})\:\left( \tan\:30^{\circ} - \tan\:17.4^{\circ}\right)\:\cos\:30^{\circ} = 1.14\:\text{cm}. \end{align*}
