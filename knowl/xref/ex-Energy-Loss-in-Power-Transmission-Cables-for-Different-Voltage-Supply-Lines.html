Exercise 3. Energy Loss in Power Transmission Cables for Different Voltage Supply Lines.

Consider a power plant located \(2.5\text{ km}\) outside a town delivering \(50\text{ MW}\) of power to the town. The transmission lines are made of aluminum cables of \(7\text{ cm}^2\) cross-section area. Find the loss of power in the transmission lines if it is transmitted at (a) \(200\text{ kV}\) (rms) and (b) \(120\text{ V}\) (rms).
Data: resistivity of Al, \(\rho = 2.82\times 10^{-8}\:\Omega.\text{m}\)
Hint.
(a) Find \(R\) first. Use Av. Power = \(I_\text{rms}^2R\text{.}\)
Answer.
(a) \(12.6\: \text{MW}\text{,}\) (b) all.
Solution 1. (a)
The resistivity of Al from the table we have \(\rho = 2.82\times 10^{-8}\:\Omega.\text{m}\text{.}\) Using this value we find the resistance in the 2.5-km transmission line, which will consist of 2.5-km one way and 2.5-km back. Therefore, the reistance will be that of 5 km long wire. The area of cross-section of the wire is \(A = 7\times 10^{-4}\:\text{m}^2\text{.}\)
\begin{align*} R \amp = \rho\:\dfrac{L}{A}\\ \amp = 2.82\times 10^{-8}\:\Omega.\text{m}\times \dfrac{5,000\:\text{m}}{7\times 10^{-4}\:\text{m}^2}\\ \amp = 2014\:\Omega. \end{align*}
When \(50\, \text{MW}\) of power is supplied at \(200\,\text{kV}\) (rms) the current in the wire will be, \(I_{\text{rms}}=50\times 10^6\:\text{W}/200\times 10^3\:\text{V} = 250\:\text{A}\text{.}\) Therefore the power dissipated in the supply line will be
\begin{equation*} P = I_{\text{rms}}^2\: R = 12.6\: \text{MW}. \end{equation*}
Solution 2. (b)
When \(50\, \text{MW}\) of power is supplied at \(200\,\text{kV}\) (rms) the current in the wire will be, \(I_{\text{rms}}=50\times 10^6\:\text{W}/120\:\text{V} = 417\:\text{kA}\text{.}\) Therefore the power dissipated in the supply line will be
\begin{equation*} P = I_{\text{rms}}^2\: R > 50\: \text{MW}. \end{equation*}
That is, all power will be dissipated and nothing will be supplied.
