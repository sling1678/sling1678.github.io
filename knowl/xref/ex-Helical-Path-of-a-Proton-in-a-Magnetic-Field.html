Exercise 2. Helical Path of a Proton in a Magnetic Field.

A proton enters a uniform magnetic field region with a velocity of \(6 \times 10^5 \text{ m/s}\) at an angle of \(80^{\circ}\) with the direction of the magnetic field. Find the pitch of the helical path if magnetic field has a magnitude of \(2\text{ T}\text{.}\)
Hint.
Use helical path equations.
Answer.
\(3.4\text{ mm}\text{.}\)
Solution.
To find the pitch we need the cyclotron frequency \(\Omega\) and \(z\)-component of initial velocity, where \(z\) is the axis of the helix.
\begin{equation*} \Omega = \dfrac{QB}{m} = \dfrac{1.60\times 10^{-19}\text{ C}\times 2\text{ T} }{ 1.67 \times 10^{-27}\text{ kg} } = 1.92\times 10^{8}\text{ rad/s}. \end{equation*}
The component of velocity along axis is
\begin{equation*} v_z = v\cos\,\theta = 6 \times 10^5\text{ m/s}\times \cos\, 80^{\circ} = 1.04\times 10^{5}\text{ m/s}. \end{equation*}
Therefore, pitch along axis is
\begin{equation*} \lambda = \dfrac{2\pi v_z}{\Omega} = 0.0034\text{ m} = 3.4\text{ mm}. \end{equation*}
