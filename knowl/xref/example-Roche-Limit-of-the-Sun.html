Example 12.50. Roche Limit of the Sun.

The density of Sun and Earth are \(\rho_\odot = 1.41\,\text{g/cm}^3\) and \(\rho_\text{E} = 5.51\,\text{g/cm}^3\text{.}\) What is the Roche radius in units of radius of the sun?
Solution.
Let us denote radius of the sun by the symbol \(R_\odot\text{.}\) Using Eq. (12.65), we get
\begin{equation*} L_\odot = 2.52 \left( \frac{1.41}{5.51}\right)^{1/3}\,R_\odot = 1.6\,R_\odot. \end{equation*}
