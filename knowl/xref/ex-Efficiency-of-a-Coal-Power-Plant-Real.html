Exercise 2. Efficiency of a Coal Power Plant.

A coal power plant consumes \(100,000\text{ kg}\) of coal per hour and produces \(500\text{ MW}\) of power. What is the efficiency of the power plant?
Data: heat of combustion of coal is 30 MJ/kg.
Hint.
Answer.
\(0.6\) or \(60\%\text{.}\)
Solution.
From the given data we find that energy used per sec is
\begin{equation*} \frac{ 100,000\text{ kg} \times 30\text{ MJ/kg} }{3600\text{ s}} = 833.33\text{ MJ/s}. \end{equation*}
The energy produced per sec is \(500\text{ MW} = 500\text{ MJ/s}\text{.}\) Therefore, efficiency of the power plant is
\begin{equation*} \frac{500\text{ MJ/s}}{833.33\text{ MJ/s}} = 0.60. \end{equation*}
