Exercise 1. Comparing Magnetic Field of Current in Wire to that of Earth.

Evaluate magnetic field of a \(5\text{-A}\) current in a very long straight wire at the following distance from the wire: (a) \(1\, \text{cm}\text{,}\) (b) \(10\, \text{cm}\text{,}\) and (c) \(100\, \text{cm}\text{.}\) Compare the strengths of magnetic fields to that of magnetic field of Earth, whose magnitude is approximately \(1/2\) Gauss.
Solution.
The formula for current in infinitely lone wire can be used to find the magnetic field values here. The calculations for the magnitudes give the following results.
  1. \begin{align*} B \amp = \frac{\mu_0}{2\pi }\frac{I}{r} = 2\frac{\textrm{T.m}}{\textrm{A}}\times \frac{5\ \textrm{A}}{0.01\ \textrm{m}}\\ \amp = 10^{-4} \textrm{T} = 1\ \textrm{G} \end{align*}
  2. \(B = 0.1\ \textrm{G}\text{.}\)
  3. \(B = 0.01\ \textrm{G}\text{.}\)
At \(1\, \text{cm}\) distance, the magnetic field by the \(5\, \text{A}\) current in the wire is twice the Earth’s magnetic field. At \(10\, \text{cm}\text{,}\) B of the current is \(1/5\)-th of the Earth’s magnetic field, and at \(100\, \text{cm}\) from the wire it is \(1/50\)-th of the Earth’s magnetic field.
