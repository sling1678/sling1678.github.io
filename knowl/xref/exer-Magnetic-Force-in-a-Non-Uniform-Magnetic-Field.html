Exercise 7. Magnetic Force in a Non-Uniform Magnetic Field.

In a region a non-uniform magnetic field exists such that \(B_x = 0\text{,}\) \(B_y =0\text{,}\) and \(B_z = a x\text{,}\) where \(a\) is a constant. At some time \(t\text{,}\) a wire of length \(L\) is carrying a current \(I\) is located along the \(x\)-axis from origin to \(x=L\text{.}\) Find the magnetic force on the wire at this instant in time.
Answer.
Magnitude \(IaL^2/2\text{.}\)
Solution.
The magnetic force on an element between \(x\) and \(x+dx\) will have the following magnitude
\begin{equation*} dF = I\: a\: x\: dx. \end{equation*}
Integrating this from \(x=0\) to \(x=L\) gives \(F = \frac{1}{2}\: I a L^2\text{.}\) By using the right-hand rule, the direction of the force will be in the negative \(y\)-direction.
