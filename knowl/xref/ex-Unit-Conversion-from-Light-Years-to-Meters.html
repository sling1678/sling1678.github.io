Exercise 3. Unit Conversion from Light Years to Meters.
In astronomy, a common unit of distance is a light-year (ly), which is equal to the distance that light travels in one year, which is taken to be 365.25 days. How many meters are one ly?
Hint.
Use \(d = v\,t\text{.}\)
Answer.
\(9,460,730,472,580,800\, \text{m}.\)
Solution.
\begin{align*} d \amp = v\, t \\ \amp = 299792458\, \text{m/s}\times 365.25\times24\times3600\,\text{s}\\ \amp = 9,460,730,472,580,800\, \text{m}. \end{align*}
