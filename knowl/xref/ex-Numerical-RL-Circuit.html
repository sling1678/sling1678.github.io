Exercise 2. Numerical RL-Circuit.

A \(200\text{-}\Omega\) resistor is connected to a \(2\text{-mH}\) inductor and a \(10\text{-V}\) source in series. The voltage source is turned on at \(t=0\text{,}\) and the current starts to build.
(a) Determine the value of steady current.
(b) What is the voltage drop across the inductor when steady current flows through it?
(c) What is the voltage drop across the resistor at steady state?
(d) Determine the time it will take to build to \(50\%\) of the steady current in the circuit.
(e) Determine the time it will take to build \((1-1/e)\times100\%\) of the steady current.
(f) Determine the time it will take to build to \(90\%\) of the steady current.
Hint.
USe solution of series RL-circuit.
Answer.
(a) \(5.0\times 10^{-2}\, \text{A}\text{,}\) (b) \(0\text{,}\) (c) \(10\,\text{V}\text{,}\) (d) \(6.93\:\mu\text{s}\text{,}\) (e) \(10\:\mu\text{s} \text{,}\) (f) \(23\:\mu\text{s}\text{.}\)
Solution 1. a
As \(t\) becomes large we reach a steady current in the circuit given by voltage of the source and the net resistance.
\begin{equation*} I_{\text{max}} = \dfrac{V}{R} = 5.0\times 10^{-2}\:\text{A}. \end{equation*}
Solution 2. b
When current is steady, its derivative with time is zero. Hence, no voltage will drop across the inductor, assuming the inductor has only negligible resistance.
\begin{equation*} V_L = 0. \end{equation*}
Solution 3. c
Whe steady current is flowing, the circuit is like a DC circuit with resitor only. Hence, all voltage of the source must drop across the resistor.
\begin{equation*} V_R = 10\text{ V}. \end{equation*}
Solution 4. d
The time constant of an \(RL\) circuit is \(\tau = \mathcal{L}/R = 10^5\text{ s}\text{.}\) Therefore, current in the \(RL\) circuit rises according to the following
\begin{align*} I(t) \amp = I_{\text{max}}\left(1 - e^{-t/\tau} \right)\\ \amp = 0.05\:\text{A}\:\left(1 - e^{-10^5 t} \right). \end{align*}
Therefore the time to get to \(50\%\) of the max will be given by \(t\) in the following equation.
\begin{equation*} \dfrac{I}{I_{\text{max}}} = 0.5 = 1 - e^{-10^5 t}. \end{equation*}
You can solve this for \(t\) by rearranging and taking natural log of both side.
\begin{equation*} \ln(e^{-10^5 t}) = \ln(1-0.5). \end{equation*}
This gives
\begin{equation*} t = \frac{\ln(2)}{10^{5}} = \frac{0.693}{10^5} = 6.93\times 10^{-6}\,\text{sec}. \end{equation*}
Hence, \(t=6.93\:\mu\text{s}\text{.}\)
Solution 5. e
\begin{equation*} I_{\text{max}}\left(1 - e^{-1} \right) = I_{\text{max}}\left(1 - e^{-10^5t} \right) \text{ gives } \end{equation*}
This gives \(t= 10\,\mu\text{s}.\)
Solution 6. f
Now, time \(t\) will satisfy the following condition.
\begin{equation*} 0.9\:I_{\text{max}}\left(1 - e^{-1} \right) = I_{\text{max}}\left(1 - e^{-10^5 t} \right) \end{equation*}
This gives
\begin{equation*} t= 23\, \mu\text{s}. \end{equation*}
