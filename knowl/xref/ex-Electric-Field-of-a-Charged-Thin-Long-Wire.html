Example 30.24. Electric Field of a Charged Thin Long Wire.

A thin straight wire has a uniform linear charge density \(\lambda_0\) (SI units: \(\text{C/m}\)). Find the electric field at a distance \(d\) from the wire.
Answer.
Magnitude: \(E = \dfrac{1}{2\pi\epsilon_0}\, \dfrac{\lambda_0}{d}\text{,}\) and direction away from the wire if \(\lambda_0\gt 0\) and towards the wire if \(\lambda_0\lt 0\text{.}\)
Solution.
The given charge density has cylindrical symmetry. Therefore, electric field at a distance \(d\) from the wire will have the magnitude
\begin{equation*} E = \dfrac{1}{2\pi\epsilon_0}\, \dfrac{q_\text{enc}/L}{d}, \end{equation*}
where \(q_\text{enc}\) are the charges on the wire in length \(L\text{.}\)
\begin{equation*} q_\text{enc} = \lambda_0 L. \end{equation*}
Therefore,
\begin{equation*} E = \dfrac{1}{2\pi\epsilon_0}\, \dfrac{\lambda_0}{d}. \end{equation*}
The direction of the field is away from the wire if \(\lambda_0\gt 0\) and towards the wire if \(\lambda_0\lt 0\text{.}\)
