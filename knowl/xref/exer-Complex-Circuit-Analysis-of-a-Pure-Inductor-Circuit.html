Exercise 1. Complex Circuit Analysis of a Pure Inductor Circuit.

In the circuit shown in FigureÂ 41.49, find: (a) the current in the circuit, (b) the phase angle between the current in the circuit and the EMF, and (c) average power. Assume \(R = 0\) in the circuit.
Answer.
(a) \((225\, \text{mA}) \cos(\omega t - \pi/2)\text{.}\)
Solution 1. a
Here, the complex impedance is \(Z = i \omega L\text{.}\) We write the source voltage as \(V= V_0\: e^{i\omega t}\text{,}\) where \(V_0 = 170\) V and \(\omega = 120\pi\,\text{sec}^{-1}\text{.}\) The current is obtained from the real part of \(I = V/Z\text{.}\)
\begin{equation*} (1)\ \ I = Re\left[\dfrac{V_0\: e^{i\omega t}}{i \omega L} \right] = \dfrac{V_0}{\omega L}\:\cos\left(\omega t - \dfrac{\pi}{2}\right) = I_0 \:\cos\left(\omega t - \dfrac{\pi}{2}\right). \end{equation*}
Putting in the numbers we find \(I_0 = \dfrac{V_0}{\omega L} = 225\) mA.
Solution 2. b
The phase angle is \(\pi/2\) with the EMF leading the current as in (1).
Solution 3. c
Since there are no dissipative elements in the circuit average power will be zero.
