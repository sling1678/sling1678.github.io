Exercise 2. Heat Loss Through a Window.

Windows are a source of heat loss in the winter from a heated room. Consider a plane glass window of height 1 m, width 50 cm and thickness 3 mm. The temperature inside the house is maintained at \(27^{\circ}\)C. What is the rate of loss of energy by the heat conduction through the window on a day when the temperature outside is \(-5^{\circ}\)C and the air is still? Use conductivity of glass pane, \(k = 0.9\) J/s.m.K. (Note if the air is not still, there will be greater loss of heat not accounted for by the conduction alone.)
Answer.
\(4.8\) kW.
Solution.
Referring to FigureÂ 23.9, the rate of flow of heat due to conduction is given by
\begin{equation*} \frac{1}{A}\left| \frac{Q}{\Delta t}\right| = k \frac{|\Delta T|}{w}, \end{equation*}
where \(\Delta T = T_1-T_2\) and \(k\) the thermal conductivity. Since the temperature in this formula is the difference in the temperatures it will have the same value in Celsius and Kelvin scales.
Therefore, we do not need to convert the values of the temperatures given in the problem.
\begin{align*} \left| \frac{Q}{\Delta t}\right| \amp = k \frac{|\Delta T|}{w}A \\ \amp = 0.9\frac{\textrm{J}}{\textrm{s.m.K}} \frac{32\ \textrm{K}}{0.003\ \textrm{m}}\times 0.5\ \textrm{m}^2\\ \amp = 4.8\ \textrm{kJ/s} = 1000\ \textrm{cal/s}. \end{align*}
