Example 14.35. Variation of Intensity in Interference.

Two coherent \(3\text{-mm}\) wavelength sources separated by \(5\text{-mm}\) shine towards a screen as shown. If the intensity of individual source at the screen is \(20\text{ W/m}^2\text{,}\) what is the intensity at points P and Q marked on the screen?
Answer.
\(I_P = 39.3\text{ W/m}^2\text{,}\) \(I_Q = 0.095\text{ W/m}^2\text{.}\)
Solution.
First we find angle \(\theta\) to points P and Q. From the given geometry, we get
\begin{align*} \amp \theta_P = \tan^{-1}(2.5/10) = 0.245\text{ rad},\\ \amp \theta_Q = \tan^{-1}(3.5/10) = 0.337\text{ rad}. \end{align*}
Now, we use these values in Eq. (14.79) to find intensity expected at those points. We need rto make sure we change the unit of wavelength to \(\text{cm}\) so that all length units in the argument of cosine cancel out.
\begin{align*} I_P \amp = 2I_0\left[ 1+ \cos \left( \frac{2\pi d}{\lambda} \sin\theta \right) \right]\\ \amp = 2\times 20 \left[ 1+ \cos \left( \frac{2\pi \times 5}{0.3} \sin(0.245 \text{ rad})\right) \right]\\ \amp = 40(1+0.96) = 39.3\text{ W/m}^2.\\ I_Q \amp = 2\times 20 \left[ 1+ \cos \left( \frac{2\pi \times 5}{0.3} \sin(0.337 \text{ rad})\right) \right]\\ \amp = 40(1-0.9976) = 0.095\text{ W/m}^2. \end{align*}
