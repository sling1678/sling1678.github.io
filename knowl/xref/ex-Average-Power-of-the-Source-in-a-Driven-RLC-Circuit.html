Example 40.29. Average Power of the Source in a Driven RLC Circuit.

For each circuit in ExampleÂ 40.26, find the average power delivered to the circuit if the peak voltage of the source of frequency \(600\text{ Hz}\) is \(10\text{ V}\text{.}\)
Answer.
(a) \(0.8\text{ W}\text{,}\) (b) \(2.7\text{ W}\text{,}\) (c) \(0.011\text{ W}\text{,}\) and (d) \(0.14\text{ W}\text{.}\)
Solution.
Average power is related to the peak current and resistance only: \(P_{\textrm{ave}} = \frac{1}{2}RI_0^2\text{,}\) where \(I_0\) is the peak current. The peak current for each circuit has been worked out in the last exercise. Now, using this formula we obtain the following values for the average power: (a) \(0.8\text{ W}\text{,}\) (b) \(2.7\text{ W}\text{,}\) (c) \(0.011\text{ W}\text{,}\) and (d) \(0.14\text{ W}\text{.}\)
