Exercise 2. Rate of Flow of Electrons in a Current-Carrying Copper Wire.

In a thin wire of copper \(20\text{ mA}\) of current is flowing. How many electrons are passing through through the wire every minute?
Hint.
Convert charge into number of electrons.
Answer.
\(7.5\times 10^{18}\text{.}\)
Solution.
We use the definition of current of \(1\text{ A}\) as \(1\text{ C/s}\) and convert the charge into number of electrons. Hence,
\begin{align*} I \amp = 20\times 10^{-3}\:\dfrac{\text{C}}{\text{s}}\times \dfrac{1\:\text{e}}{1.6\times 10^{-19}\:\text{C}}\times \dfrac{60\:\text{s}}{1\:\text{min}} \\ \amp = 7.5\times 10^{18}\:\dfrac{\text{e}}{\text{min}}. \end{align*}
