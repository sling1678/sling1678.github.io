Example 43.3. Number of Photons in a Light Source.

How many photons are emitted in \(3\text{ sec}\) by a light source of power \(2\text{ W}\) if the wavelength of the light in vacuum is \(0.45\,\mu\text{m}\text{?}\)
Answer.
\(1.36 \times 10^{19}\text{.}\)
Solution.
Energy in one photon, denoted by \(E_\gamma\text{,}\) of light of the given wavelength is
\begin{align*} E_\gamma \amp = h f = h\:\dfrac{c}{\lambda}\\ \amp = 6.63\times 10^{-34}\:\text{J.s}\times \dfrac{3\times 10^{8}\:\text{m/s}}{450\times 10^{-9}\:\text{m}} \\ \amp = 4.42\times 10^{-19}\:\text{J}. \end{align*}
Therefore, the number of photons passing in \(3\text{ sec}\) will be
\begin{equation*} \dfrac{2\:\text{W}}{ 4.42\times 10^{-19}\:\text{J}}\times 3\:\text{s} = 1.36\times 10^{19}. \end{equation*}
