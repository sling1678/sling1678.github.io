Exercise 2. Period of Same Pendulum at Different Locations on Earth.

The period of a plane pendulum varies from place to place. At place A, the period is \(2.84\text{ sec}\text{.}\) At place B, the period is \(2.89\text{ sec}\text{.}\) What is the ratio of the acceleration due to gravity, \(g_A/g_B\text{?}\)
Hint.
Use \(T\propto 1/\sqrt{g}\text{.}\)
Answer.
\(1.05\text{.}\)
Solution.
Since only thing different may be the acceleration due to gravity, we use the proportionality \(T\propto 1/\sqrt{g}\) to obtain
\begin{equation*} \dfrac{T_A}{T_B} = \sqrt{\dfrac{g_B}{g_A}}. \end{equation*}
From this we get
\begin{equation*} \dfrac{g_A}{g_B} = \left( \dfrac{T_B}{T_A} \right)^2 = \left( \dfrac{2.89}{2.82} \right)^2 = 1.05. \end{equation*}
That is, \(g\) is about 5% higher at A than at B.
