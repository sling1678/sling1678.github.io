Exercise 1. Normal Modes of Vibrations of a Guitar String.

A guitar string of length 78 cm and mass per unit length of 30 grams per meter is under a tension of 200 N. (a) Find the frequency of the fundamental mode of vibration. (b) Find the frequencies of the first, second and third overtones. (c) Find the distance between the nodes of the second overtone. (d) Find the distance between nodes of the third overtone.
Solution 1. a
In these questions it is easier to first work out the wavelength of the standing wave from the pictures of the modes on the string. From the wavelength and the speed of the waves on the string we then calculate the frequency of the modes.
In the fundamental of a string tied to the two ends, we know from the text that half a wave fits on the length \(L\) of the string. This gives the wavelength \(\lambda_0\) of the mode to be \(2L\text{.}\)
\begin{equation*} \lambda_0 = 2 L = 2\times 78\ \text{cm} = 156\ \text{cm}. \end{equation*}
The speed of the wave on a string depends on the tension \(T\) and linear density \(\mu\text{.}\)
\begin{equation*} v = \sqrt{\frac{T}{\mu}} = \sqrt{\frac{200\ \text{N}}{0.03\ \text{kg/m}}} = 81.6\ \text{m/s}. \end{equation*}
The frequency of the fundamental will be
\begin{equation*} f_0 = \frac{v}{\lambda_0} = \frac{81.6\ \text{m/s}}{1.56\ \text{m}} = 52.3 \ \text{Hz}. \end{equation*}
Solution 2. b
The frequencies of the first, second and third overtones will be \(2f_0\text{,}\) \(3f_0\) and \(4f_0\text{.}\)
Solution 3. c
In the second overtone 1.5 times a wavelength fits in the string. That is, the nodes are separated by 1/3 of \(L\text{,}\) or 26 cm.
Solution 4. d
In the third overtone 2 times the wavelength fits in the string. Therefore, the distance between the nodes will be \(L/4\) or 19.5 cm.
