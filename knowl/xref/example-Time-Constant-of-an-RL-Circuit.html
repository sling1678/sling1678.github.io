Example 39.21. Time Constant of an \(RL\) Circuit.

Self-inductace of an electric motor is \(10\,\text{H}\text{.}\) Th motor is connected in a circuit of net resistance \(200.0\,\Omega\text{.}\) What is the time constant of this circuit?
Solution.
From the formula \(\tau = \mathcal{L}/R\) we get
\begin{equation*} \tau = \frac{10\,\text{H}}{200.0\,\Omega} = \frac{1}{20}\,\text{sec}. \end{equation*}
