Example 4.48. Acceleration from Slopes of Velocity Components.

The following figure shows plots of the \(x \) and \(y \) components of velocity, \(v_x \) and \(v_y \text{.}\) Find the acceleration in the component form at the following instants: (a) \(t = 1\text{ s}\text{,}\) (b) \(t = 3\text{ s}\text{,}\) (c) \(t = 5\text{ s}\text{,}\) (d) \(t = 7\text{ s}\text{,}\) (e) \(t = 9\text{ s}\text{.}\)
Answer.
(a) \((0, 1\text{ m/s}^2)\text{,}\) (b) \((0, -2\text{ m/s}^2)\text{,}\) (c) \((-1\text{ m/s}^2, 1\text{ m/s}^2)\text{,}\) (d) \((-1\text{ m/s}^2, 1\text{ m/s}^2)\text{,}\) (e) \((0, 0)\text{.}\)
Solution.
We need to find slopes at the indicated instants by \(a_x = \dfrac{\Delta v_x}{\Delta t}\) and similarly for \(a_y\text{.}\)
\begin{align*} \amp \text{(a)}\ \ a_x = 0,\ \ a_y = 1\text{ m/s}^2,\\ \amp \text{(b)}\ \ a_x = 0,\ \ a_y = -2\text{ m/s}^2,\\ \amp \text{(c)}\ \ a_x = -1\text{ m/s}^2,\ \ a_y = 1\text{ m/s}^2,\\ \amp \text{(d)}\ \ a_x = -1\text{ m/s}^2,\ \ a_y = 1\text{ m/s}^2,\\ \amp \text{(d)}\ \ a_x = 0,\ \ a_y = 0, \end{align*}
