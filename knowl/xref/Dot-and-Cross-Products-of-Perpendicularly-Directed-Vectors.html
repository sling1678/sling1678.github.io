Exercise 6. Dot and Cross Products of Perpendicularly Directed Vectors.

Consider a force vector \(\vec F \) that has a magnitude \(25.0\text{ N}\) in the direction of East and a displacement vector \(\vec r \) that has a magnitude \(4.0\text{ m}\) in the direction of North. For stating directions in space, let \(x \) axis be pointed towards East, \(y \) axis be pointed towards North, and \(z \) axis be pointed Up. Find (a) \(\vec r \cdot \vec F \text{,}\) and (b) \(\vec r \times \vec F \text{.}\)
Hint.
Use definitions.
Answer.
(a) \(0 \text{,}\) (b) (\(100.0\text{ N.m} \text{,}\) in the negative \(z \) axis direction).
Solution.
Here the angle between the two vectors are \(90^{\circ} \text{.}\) Therefore, we have
\begin{equation*} \cos\,\theta = 0,\ \ \sin\, \theta = 1. \end{equation*}
Therefore, the dot product will give \(\vec r \cdot \vec F = 0\text{,}\) and the magnitude of the cross product will be \(100.0 \text{ N.m}\text{.}\) Using the right-hand rule, you can deduce the direction to be into-the-page direcion, which is the direction of the negative \(z \) axis.
