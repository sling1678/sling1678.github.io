Exercise 8. Magnetic Field from Current in a Square by Biot-Savart Law.

Find magnetic field a point P a height \(h\) above the center of a square loop of side \(a\) carrying a current \(I\text{.}\)
Answer.
Magnitude: \(\frac{\mu_0 I a}{\pi}\frac{1}{\left( h^2 + a^2/4\right)\sqrt{h^2+a^2/2}}\text{,}\) Direction: into-the-page.
Solution.
Let us place the square loop in the \(xy\)-plane so that point P is on the \(z\)-axis as shown in FigureÂ 36.31. We will work out the magnetic field of one side and use the symmetry to find the final \(\vec B\) at P.
The magnetic field from currents in AB and CD will add up so that their \(z\)-components add and the horizontal components cancel out. Similarly for the contributions from currents in AD and BC. From symmetry, the magnitudes of magnetic field from each wire is same. Hence, we need only find the \(z\)-component of magnetic field from AB and multiply it by four. From Biot-Savart law, we find the following.
\begin{align*} \left( B_z\right)_{\textrm{from AB}} \amp = \dfrac{\mu_0 }{8\pi}\:\int_{-a/2}^{a/2}\: \dfrac{I \dfrac{a}{2} dx}{[h^2+(a^2/4)+x^2]^{3/2}}\\ \amp = \dfrac{\mu_0 I a^2}{8\pi}\: \dfrac{1}{(h^2 + a^2/4)\sqrt{ h^2+(a^2/2)}}. \end{align*}
Hence, the net magnetic field at P is
\begin{equation*} B_z = \dfrac{\mu_0 I a^2}{2\pi}\: \dfrac{1}{(h^2 + a^2/4)\sqrt{ h^2+(a^2/2)}}. \end{equation*}
