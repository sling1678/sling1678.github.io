Exercise 4. Power Adapter for Neon Signs.

Neon signs require \(12\text{-kV}\) for their operation. A transformer is to be used to change the voltage from \(220\text{-V}\) (rms) AC to \(12\text{-kV}\) (rms) AC.
(a) What must the ratio of turns in the secondary to the turns in the primary?
(b) What is the maximum rms current the neon lamps can draw if the fuse in the primary goes off at \(0.5\text{ A}\text{?}\)
(c) How much power is used by the neon sign when it is drawing the maximum current allowed by the fuse in the primary?
Hint.
Use trasformer equations.
Answer.
(a) \(600:11\text{,}\) (b) \(9.17\:\text{mA}\text{,}\) (c) \(110\:\text{W}\text{.}\)
Solution 1. (a)
\(N_s:N_p = V_s:V_p = 12000:220 = 600:11\text{.}\)
Solution 2. (b)
Balancing the power in the primary to the power in the secondary we get \(V_p \times I_p = V_s \times I_s\text{.}\) Therefore,
\begin{equation*} I_s = \dfrac{V_p}{V_s}\: I_p = \dfrac{11}{600}\times 0.5 = 9.17\:\text{mA}. \end{equation*}
Solution 3. (c)
\(P = I_{\text{rms}}V_{\text{rms}} = 110\:\text{W}\text{.}\)
