Exercise 2. Force on Sperical Object by Two Spherical Objects.

Find the net gravitational force on a 2-kg steel spherical ball by two other spherical balls with masses 20-kg and 30-kg in the configuration shown in Figure 12.12. Give both the magnitude and the direction.
Answer.
\(1.53 \times 10^{-7}\) N, \(2.80\circ\) below horizontal in third quadrant.
Solution.
The forces \(\vec F_1\) and \(\vec F_2\) from the 20-kg and 30-kg balls will add vectorially. Let us work out the magnitudes first. The distances between the 2-kg ball and these balls are both equal to
\begin{equation*} r = \sqrt{0.2^2 + 0.05^2} = 0.21\ \textrm{m}. \end{equation*}
The magnitudes of the two forces are
\begin{align*} \amp F_1= G_N\frac{20\times 2}{0.21^2} = 6.05\times 10^{-8} \textrm{N}.\\ \amp F_2 =9.07\times 10^{-8} \textrm{N}. \end{align*}
Let the \(x\)-axis be horizontal to the right and the vertical axis pointed up. Then, the \(x\)- and \(y\)-components of the net force will be
\begin{align*} \amp F_x=-\frac{0.2}{0.21} \times (6.05 +9.07) 10^{-8} = 1.44\times 10^{-7} \textrm{N}.\\ \amp F_y =-\frac{0.1}{0.21} \times (9.07-6.05)\times 10^{-8}= -1.43\times 10^{-8} \textrm{N}. \end{align*}
From these you can find the magnitude and the angle for the direction of the net force in the \(xy\)-plane in which the centers of the three masses are located. The magnitude would be \(1.45\times 10^{-7} \textrm{N}\) and the angle theta below the horizontal direction would be \(tan^{-1}(1.43/14.4) = 5.67^{\circ}\) as shown in Figure 12.13.
