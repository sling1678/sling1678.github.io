Example 12.49. Roche Limit of Earth to Breakup a two Spherical Balls held together by Gravitation.

Two spherical balls of mass \(1\,\text{kg}\) each are floating in outer space by bound together at a center-to-center distance of \(10.0\,\text{cm}\) from each other by gravitational force. They are gradually drifting towards Earth. At what distance from the Earth will they start to break apart, i.e., what is the Roche limit of Earth in this situation?
Solution.
In Eq. (12.64), we have \(2a = 10\,\text{cm}\text{,}\) which gives \(a=5.0\,\text{cm}\text{.}\) Therefore,
\begin{align*} d \amp = a \left( \right)^{1/3} = \left(5.0\times 10^{-2}\,\text{m} \right)\times \left( \frac{16\times 6\times 10^{24}\,\text{kg}}{1.0\,\text{kg}} \right)^{1/3} \\ \amp = 2.28\times 10^{7}\,\text{m}. \end{align*}
Compare this to the radius of Earth, which is \(6.4\times 10^{6}\,\text{m}\text{.}\)
