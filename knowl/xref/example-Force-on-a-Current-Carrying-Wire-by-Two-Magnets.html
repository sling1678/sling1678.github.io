Example 35.39. Force on a Current Carrying Wire by Two Magnets.

A straight wire passed between two magnets as shown. Assume that there is no magnetic field outside the space between the poles, and it has uniform magnitude of \(B_1\) and \(B_2\) between the poles. Find the force on the wire when a current \(I\) passes through the wire.
Solution.
The forces by both magnets are pointed up, so they will add. Hence the net magnetic force on the wire has the magnitude of \(I B_1 a + I B_2 b\text{.}\)
