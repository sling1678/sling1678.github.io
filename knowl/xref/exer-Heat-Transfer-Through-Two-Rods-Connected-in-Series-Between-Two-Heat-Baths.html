Exercise 3. Heat Transfer Through Two Rods Connected in Series Between Two Heat Baths.

Two heat baths at temperatures 580 K and 380 K are connected by steel and copper rods attached end to end, each of length 50 cm and diameter 4 cm. The rods are insulated so that heat can flow only along its length. (a) What is the temperature at the point where the rods are joined? (b) Find the power flux.
Answer.
(a) 403 K, (b) \(17700\ \textrm{W/m}^2\text{.}\)
Solution.
Let \(T\) be the temperature at the joint. Denote the quantities for the steel and the copper by the subscripts 1 and 2 respectively. The flow of heat to the joint must equal the flow away from the joint since no energy accumulates at the joint. Therefore,
\begin{equation*} k_1 \frac{T_1-T}{L_1} = k_2 \frac{T-T_2}{L_2}. \end{equation*}
Since \(L_1 = L_2\text{,}\) the lengths cancel out. Solving for \(T\) we find
\begin{equation*} T = \frac{k_1 T_1 + k_2 T_2}{k_1 + k_2}. \end{equation*}
Now, we put in the numbers to obtain \(T = 403\) K. Using this \(T\) we can find the heat flus \(\Phi\) using either flux in the steel or the copper part.
\begin{equation*} \Phi = k_1 \frac{T_1-T}{L_1} = 50\frac{\textrm{J}}{\textrm{s.m.K}} \frac{(580-403)\ \textrm{K}}{0.5\ \textrm{m}} = 17.7\ \frac{\textrm{kW}}{\textrm{m}^2}. \end{equation*}
