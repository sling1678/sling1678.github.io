Exercise 5. Width of Transmittance Peaks of a Fabry-Perot Interferometer.

Show the width in phase difference \(\Delta_{12}\) at half the height of a peak of \(T\equiv I_t/I_i\) in the assumption of narrow peaks is
\begin{equation*} \frac{4}{\sqrt{F}}\text{ or } \frac{2(1-R)}{\sqrt{R}}. \end{equation*}
Hint.
Set \(T=\frac{1}{2}\) for \(\Delta_{12}=2\pi m \pm \frac{w}{2}\) where \(w\) is the full width.
Answer.
Already in the problem statement.
Solution.
Recall that peaks of \(T\) occur at \(\Delta_{12}=2\pi m\) with \(m\) an integer. Let \(\Delta_{12}\) at half height around peak \(\Delta_{12}=2m\pi\) for some \(m\) be
\begin{equation*} \Delta_{12} = 2\pi m \pm \frac{w}{2}, \end{equation*}
where \(w\) is the full width. Setting \(T\) at these values to \(1/2\) we get the following condition.
\begin{equation*} \frac{1}{2} = \frac{1}{1+ F\sin^2\left(\pi m \pm \frac{w}{4} \right)}. \end{equation*}
Rearraning we get
\begin{equation*} 1+ F\sin^2\left(\pi m \pm \frac{w}{4} \right) = 2. \end{equation*}
Now, we use cosine double angle formula and expanding the resulting cosine to change this to
\begin{equation*} 1+\frac{F}{2} - \frac{F}{2}\cos(w/2) = 2. \end{equation*}
Solving these we get
\begin{equation*} \cos(w/2) = 1 - \frac{2}{F}. \end{equation*}
Since peaks in Fabry-Perot are usually ver narrow, we can expand cosine in Taylor series and keep only upto square term.
\begin{equation*} 1-\frac{1}{2} (w/2)^2 = 1 - \frac{2}{F}. \end{equation*}
Therefore,
\begin{equation*} w = \frac{4}{\sqrt{F}}. \end{equation*}
Replacing \(F\) by \(4R/(1-R)^2\text{,}\) we get
\begin{equation*} w = \frac{2(1-R)}{\sqrt{R}}. \end{equation*}
