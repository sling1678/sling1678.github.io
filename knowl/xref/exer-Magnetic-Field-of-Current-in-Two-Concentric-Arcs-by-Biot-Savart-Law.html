Exercise 7. Magnetic Field of Current in Two Concentric Arcs by Biot-Savart Law.

Find the magnetic field at the common center of the arcs by the current in wire as shown in FigureÂ 36.29.
Answer.
Magnitude: \(\frac{\mu_0 I\theta}{4\pi}\left| \frac{1}{R_1} - \frac{1}{R_2} \right|\text{,}\) Direction: into-the-page.
Solution.
First note that the current in the wires joining the two arcs do not generate any magnetic field at P since the \(Id\vec l\) vector of a current element on these segments is collinear with the vector to the field point, i.e., the center of the arcs. That collinearily gives the cross product in Biot-Savart Law zero.
Further note that magnetic field of current in the inner arc is opposite to that of the outer arc. Hence, their magnetic fields will subtract at the center of the arcs. Moreover, as inner arc is closer to point P, its contribution will dominate over that of the outer arc.
Let the direction pointing in the page be positive. Recall for center of arc we set \(z=0\) in Eq. (36.8) to get magnetic field from current element \(I\,dl\) on a circular arc of radius \(R\) to be
\begin{equation*} dB = \frac{\mu_0}{4\pi}\, \frac{I\,dl}{R^2} = \frac{\mu_0}{4\pi}\, \frac{I\,R\,d\theta}{R^2} = \frac{\mu_0\,I}{4\,\pi\,R}\,d\theta. \end{equation*}
Therefore, magnetic field from an arc that subtends an angle \(\theta\) at the center will be
\begin{equation*} B = \frac{\mu_0\,I}{4\,\pi\,R}\,\theta. \end{equation*}
Hence, the net magnetic field from the current in the two arcs will be
\begin{equation*} B = \dfrac{\mu_0 I\:\theta}{4\pi}\:\left( \dfrac{1}{R_1} - \dfrac{1}{R_2} \right).\ \ \ \textrm{(into-the-page)} \end{equation*}
