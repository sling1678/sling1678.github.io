Exercise 1. A Box Sliding Down an Incline with Constant Acceleration.

Starting from rest a box slides down an incline at a constant acceleration of \(2\, \text{m/s}^2\) pointed down the incline (not vertically down). (a) Determine the distance traveled on the incline in \(1.5\ \text{sec}\text{.}\) (b) Find its speed at \(t = 1.5\ \text{sec}\text{.}\) (c) Find the average velocity between \(t = 0\) and \(t =1.5\ \text{sec}\text{.}\) (d) Verify that the displacement during \(1.5\ \text{sec}\) interval from \(t = 0\) to \(t =1.5\ \text{sec}\) is equal to the product of average velocity for the interval and the time interval. (e) Will part (d) be true if the acceleration changed with time?
Solution 1. a
Let positive \(x\)-axis point down the incline. Then, we have
\begin{equation*} a_x = 2.0\text{ m/s}^2,\ \ v_{0x} =0,\ \ x_0 = 0. \end{equation*}
For brevity, drop the subscript \(x\) from all our symbols. Then, we will use the following to get \(x(1.5\text{ s})\text{.}\)
\begin{equation*} x = x_0 + v_0 t + \frac{1}{2}a t^2 = 0 + 0 + \frac{1}{2}\times 2 \times 1.5^2 = 2.25\,\text{m}. \end{equation*}
Solution 2. b
Since the motion is only along \(x\)-axis, it is sufficient to find \(v_x\) (which we will denote by \(v\)) to get the speed.
\begin{equation*} v = v_0 + a t = 0 + 2\times 1.5 = 3.0\,\text{m/s}. \end{equation*}
Therefore, speed is \(|v| = 3.0\,\text{m/s}\text{.}\)
Solution 3. c
From positions at \(t=0\) and \(t=1.5\,\text{m}\text{,}\) we can immediately get \(v_{av,x}\text{,}\) the average velocity along the \(x\)-axis.
\begin{equation*} v_{av} = \frac{2.25\,\text{m}-0}{1.5\,\text{s}-0} = 1.5\,\text{ m/s}. \end{equation*}

Solution 5. e
Yes, it will still be the case since the average velocity just depends on the displacement divided by time interval. It does not matter whether acceleration was changing or not.
