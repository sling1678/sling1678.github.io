Exercise 2. Angular Magnification by a Magnifying Glass for a Typical Eye.

(a) Find the angular magnification of an image by a mgnifying glass of focal length \(f=5\text{ cm}\text{.}\) Assume the magnifying glass is next to the eye and the near point of the eye is \(25\text{ cm}\) from the eye.
(b) How far behind the magnifying glass should the physical specimen be for clearest view?
Hint.
(a) Use Eq. (45.11). (b) Use Eq. (45.10).
Answer.
(a) \(6\text{,}\) \(4.17\text{ cm}\text{.}\)
Solution 1. (a)
(a) From Eq. (45.11), we see that
\begin{equation*} M = \frac{n_p+f}{f} = \frac{25+5}{5} = 6. \end{equation*}
Solution 2. (b)
(b) From Eq. (45.10), we see that
\begin{equation*} p \approx \frac{f\;n_p}{n_p+f} = \frac{5\times 25}{5+25} = 4.17\text{ cm}. \end{equation*}
