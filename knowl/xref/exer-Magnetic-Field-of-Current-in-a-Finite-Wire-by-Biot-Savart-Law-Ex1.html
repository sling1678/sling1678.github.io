Exercise 4. Magnetic Field of Current in a Finite Wire by Biot Savart Law Ex1.

Find the magnetic field at point P a distance \(s\) from the end of a straight wire section AB of length \(L\) in a circuit carrying a steady current \(I\) as shown in FigureÂ 36.24. (Ignore the contribution of other parts of the circuit.)
Answer.
\(\frac{\mu_0 I}{4\pi}\frac{L}{\sqrt{s^2+L^2}}\) into-the-page.
Solution.
Let point A be the origin, point P on the positive \(x\)-axis at a distance \(s\) from A, and the wire on the positive \(y\)-axis. We first write down the magnetic field expression from the elemental current \(I\,dy\) and then integrate that expression.
The Biot-Savart integral from the wire AB will be
\begin{align*} \vec B \amp = \dfrac{\mu_0}{4\pi}\:\int_0^L\:\dfrac{I\:dy\:\hat u_y \times \left( s\:\hat u_x - y\: \hat u_y \right)}{\left(s^2 + y^2 \right)^{3/2}}\ \ \ \textrm{(from the current in AB)} \\ \amp = - \hat u_z\: \dfrac{\mu_o\: I}{4\pi}\:\dfrac{L}{s\sqrt{s^2 + L^2}}. \end{align*}
