Exercise 5. Current Density in a Wire from a Potential Difference across the Wire.

An aluminum wire of diameter \(1 \text{ mm}\) and length \(50 \text{ m}\) is connected across a \(3\text{-V}\) battery. Find the current density in the wire.
Data: \(\rho_\text{Al} = 2.7\times 10^{-8}\:\Omega.\textrm{m}\text{.}\)
Hint.
Use \(J=I/A\text{.}\)
Answer.
\(2.2\times 10^{6}\:\text{A/m}^2\text{.}\)
Solution.
The current density is related to the current and area of cross-section by \(J = I/A\text{.}\) The area of cross-section \(A\) is
\begin{equation*} A = \frac{\pi}{4} D^2 = \frac{\pi}{4} 0.001^2 = 7.85\times 10^{-7}\text{ m}^2. \end{equation*}
For current, we need resistance,
\begin{align*} R \amp = \rho \frac{L}{A} \\ \amp = 2.7\times 10^{-8}\:\Omega.\textrm{m} \times \frac{ 50\text{ m}}{7.85\times 10^{-5}\text{ m}^2 } = 1.72\ \Omega. \end{align*}
Therefore, current in the wire is
\begin{equation*} I = \frac{\Delta \phi}{R} = \frac{3\text{ V}}{1.72\ \Omega} = 1.74\text{ A}. \end{equation*}
Now, we can compute the current density.
\begin{equation*} J= \frac{I}{A} = \frac{1.74\text{ A}}{7.85\times 10^{-7}\text{ m}^2} = 2.2\times 10^{6}\:\text{A/m}^2. \end{equation*}
