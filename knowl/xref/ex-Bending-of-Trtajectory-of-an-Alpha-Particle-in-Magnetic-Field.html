Exercise 1. Bending of Trajectory of an Alpha Particle in Magnetic Field.

In a region between north and south poles of a strong magnet there is a uniform magnetic field of 3 T pointed up. An alpha particle of charge \(+2\ e\) and mass equal to the sum of two protons and two neutrons enters the region from east with a speed of \(4 \times 10^6\) m/s. It makes a circular arc of 90-degrees before exiting.
(a) What is the radius of the circular arc? (b) What is the value of the cyclotron frequency?
Hint.
Use circular motion equations.
Answer.
(a) 2.78 cm, (b) \(\Omega = 1.44\times 10^8\ \text{rad/s}\) or \(f_c = 2.29\times 10^7\text{ Hz}.\text{.}\)
Solution 1. (a)
(a) The radius of the circular arc will be
\begin{align*} R \amp = \dfrac{mv}{QB} = \dfrac{4\times 1.67\times 10^{-27}\:\text{kg}\times 4\times 10^{6}\:\text{m/s}}{2\times 1.6\times 10^{-19}\:\text{C}\times 3\:\text{T}} = 2.78\:\text{cm}. \end{align*}
Solution 2. (b)
(b) The cyclotron (angular) frequency will be
\begin{align*} \Omega \amp = \dfrac{QB}{m}= \dfrac{2\times 1.6\times 10^{-19}\:\text{C} \times 3\:\text{T}}{4\times 1.67\times 10^{-27}\:\text{kg}} = 1.44\times 10^{8}\:\text{rad/s}. \end{align*}
In units of \(Hz\) this will be
\begin{equation*} f_c = \frac{\omega}{2\pi} = 2.29\times 10^7\text{ Hz}. \end{equation*}
