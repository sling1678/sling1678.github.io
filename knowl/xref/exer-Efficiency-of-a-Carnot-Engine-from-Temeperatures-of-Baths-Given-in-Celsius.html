Exercise 1. Efficiency of a Carnot Engine from Temeperatures of Baths Given in Celsius.

A Carnot engine operates in a Carnot cycle between a heat source at \(550^{\circ}\)C and a heat sink at \(20^{\circ}\)C. Find the efficiency of the Carnot engine.
Answer.
\(64.4\%\text{.}\)
Solution.
Recall that the temperatures in the Carnot efficiency formula are in the Absolute or Kelvin scale. Since the difference in temperature is same in Celsius and Kelvin scales, we only need to change the quantity that stands alone and not in the difference.
\begin{equation*} \eta_{\textrm{Carnot}} = \frac{T_H-T_C}{T_H} = \frac{550-20}{550+273.15} = \frac{530}{823.15} = 0.644\ \ \textrm{or}\ \ 64.4\%. \end{equation*}
