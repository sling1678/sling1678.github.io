Exercise 2. A Parallel Circuit of Three Resistors in Disguise.

Find current through each resistor in the circuit given in Figure 34.40.
Hint.
Think of potential difference across each resistor.
Answer.
\(\frac{1}{10}\text{ A},\ \frac{1}{20}\text{ A},\ \frac{1}{30}\text{ A}\)
Solution.
As before, we will choose the negative terminal of the battery as zero potential reference. Then, the positive terminal of battery is at 10 volts. We see from the diagram in Figure 34.41 that the voltage situation across each of the resistors is same: Each resistor is actually connected across the two terminals of the battery.
The given circuit is identical with the three resistors connected parallel to the battery. Hence the currents are
\begin{align*} I_1 \amp = \frac{10\text{ V}} {100\ \Omega} = \frac{1}{10}\text{ A}.\\ I_2 \amp = \frac{10\text{ V}}{200\ \Omega} = \frac{1}{20}\text{ A}.\\ I_3 \amp = \frac{10\text{ V}}{300\ \Omega} = \frac{1}{30}\text{ A}. \end{align*}
