Example 8.32. Potential Energy of a Skier at Different Heights with Zero Potential at the Bottom of Hill.

A skier of mass \(50\text{ kg}\) is skiing down a bumpy hill as shown in FigureÂ 8.33. The zero of the potential energy due to gravity is chosen at the bottom of the hill.
(a) What are the values of his potential energy at points A and B where \(h_A = 20\text{ m}\) and \(h_B = 30\text{ m}\text{?}\)
(b) What is the difference in the potential energies, \(U_B - U_A\text{?}\) In the next Checkpoint, you will find that this is independent of the choice of zero potential reference.
Answer.
(a) \(U_A = 9,810 \text{ J} \text{,}\) \(U_B = 14,715 \text{ J} \text{,}\) (b) \(4,905 \text{ J}\text{.}\)
Solution 1. a
We use the formula for the potential energy due to gravity.
\begin{align*} U_A \amp = mgh_A = 50\times 9.81\times 20 = 9,810 \text{ J}.\\ U_B \amp = mgh_B = 50\times 9.81\times 30 = 14,715\text{ J}. \end{align*}
Solution 2. b
The difference is
\begin{equation*} \Delta U = U_B - U_A = 4,905 \text{ J}. \end{equation*}
