Exercise 3. Tear Under Shear Stress.

A steel bolt is used to connect two steel plates. It is required that the bolt must withstand shear forces up to \(4,000\text{ N}\text{.}\) Assuming a safety factor of 5, what must be the minimum diameter of the bolt? Use breaking shear stress of steel = \(250\times 10^6\ \text{Pa}\text{.}\)
Hint.
Use stress= breaking stress/5.
Answer.
\(1.0\text{ cm}\)
Solution.
Here, the shear force divided by the area of the bolt must be one-fifth of the breaking shear stress. Let \(d \) be the diameter. Using the breaking shear stress for steel we get
\begin{equation*} \dfrac{4000\text{ N}}{\pi d^2/4} = \dfrac{250\times 10^6\text{ Pa}}{5}. \end{equation*}
Therefore, \(d = 0.01\text{ m} = 1.0\text{ cm}\text{.}\)
