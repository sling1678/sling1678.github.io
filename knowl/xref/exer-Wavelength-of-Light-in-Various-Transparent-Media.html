Exercise 2. Wavelength of Light in Various Transparent Media.

What is the wavelength in water, Plexiglas and flint glass of a light of wavelength \(589\, \text{nm}\) in vacuum?
Answer.
\(442\, \text{nm}\text{,}\) \(390\, \text{nm}\text{,}\) \(373\, \text{nm}\text{.}\)
Solution.
The wavelength of light in a transparent material is obtained by dividing the wavelength in vacuum by the refractive index of the material according to \(\lambda = \dfrac{\lambda_0}{n}\text{,}\) where \(\lambda_0\) is the wavelength in vacuum.
\begin{align*} \amp \textrm{In water: } \lambda = \dfrac{ 589\:\textrm{nm}}{4/3} = 442\:\textrm{nm}.\\ \amp \textrm{In plexiglas: } \lambda = \dfrac{ 589\:\textrm{nm}}{1.51 } = 390\:\textrm{nm}.\\ \amp \textrm{In flint glass: } \lambda = \dfrac{ 589\:\textrm{nm}}{1.58 } = 373\:\textrm{nm}. \end{align*}
