Example 47.12. Finding Wavelength from Interference Pattern in Youngâ€™s Double-Slit Experiment.

When a laser light of unknown wavelength is passed through two narrow slits separated by \(250\,\mu\text{m}\text{,}\) an interference pattern is observed on a screen \(1.5\, \text{m}\) away. The distance between the central spot and first order constructive interference is found to be \(5\, \text{mm}\text{.}\) (a) Find the wavelength of light. (b) Where would the second order constructive interference form on the screen, if at all?
Answer.
(a) \(0.5\:\mu\text{m}\text{.}\) (b) \(10\:\text{mm}\text{.}\)
Solution 1. (a)
Let \(\lambda\) be the unknown wavelength and let \(d\) stand for the separation of the slits. Then the condition for the angle \(\theta\) for a first-order constructive interference in this situation will be
\begin{equation*} d\:\sin\:\theta = \lambda. \end{equation*}
The angle \(\theta\) is also obtained from the geometry of set up. From the distance \(L\) to the screen and the distance \(y\) of the interference spot from the central spot we get
\begin{equation*} \sin\theta = \dfrac{y}{\sqrt{y^2 + L^2}} \approx \dfrac{y}{L}. \end{equation*}
Therefore,
\begin{equation*} \lambda = d \times \dfrac{y}{L}. \end{equation*}
Now, we can put in the numerical values to obtain
\begin{equation*} \lambda = 150\:\mu\text{m}\times \dfrac{0.005\:\text{m}}{1.5\:\text{m}} = 0.5\:\mu\text{m}. \end{equation*}
Solution 2. (b)
For the second order interference the condition is
\begin{equation*} d\:\sin\:\theta = 2\lambda,\ \ \sin\:\theta = \dfrac{2\lambda}{d} = \dfrac{1\:\mu\text{m}}{150\:\mu\text{m}}. \end{equation*}
Therefore, \(\theta \approx 1/150\:\text{rad}\text{.}\) Now, we multiply this by \(L= 1.5\) m to obtain the \(y\)-coordinate of this spot, \(y = \frac{1}{150}\:\text{rad} \times 1500\:\text{mm} = 10\:\text{mm}\text{.}\)
