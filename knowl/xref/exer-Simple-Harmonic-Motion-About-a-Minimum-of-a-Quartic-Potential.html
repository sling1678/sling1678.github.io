Exercise 1. Simple Harmonic Motion About a Minimum of a Quartic Potential.

The potential energy of a particle of mass \(m\) moving along \(x\)-axis is given as \(U(x)=x^2(x-1)^2\text{.}\)
  1. Plot the potential energy as a function of \(x\text{.}\) Discuss the types of motion that a particle will execute for various values of energy of the particle.
  2. Find the location of the minimum of the potential energy function.
  3. Find the angular frequency of small oscillation about the minimum of the potential.
  4. Are there any restrictions on the displacement for the particle to oscillate about the minimum? Explain.
