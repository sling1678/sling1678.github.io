Example 32.34. Capacitance of a Metal Screw and Nut Capacitor.

A steel screw and a steel nut fixed to a dry wooden board at a distance of \(1.0\text{ cm}\) from each other are connected to a \(12\text{ V}\) battery. When they are fully charged, the screw has a charge of \(+10\text{ pC}\) and the nut a charge of \(-10\text{ pC}\text{.}\) What is the capacitance of the screw/nut capacitor?
Answer.
\(\frac{5}{6}\text{ pF}\text{.}\)
Solution.
We use the fundamental capacitor formula \(Q = C\Delta \phi\) with \(\Delta \phi = 12\text{ V}\) provided by the battery. This gives the following for the capacitance.
\begin{align*} C \amp = \frac{Q}{\Delta \phi}\\ \amp = \frac{10\:\text{pC}}{12\:\text{V}} = \frac{5}{6}\: \text{pF}. \end{align*}
