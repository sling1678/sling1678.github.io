Exercise 10. Pushing a Log Over a Step.

A cylindrical log of mass \(M\) and radius \(R\) is pushed over a step of height \(a \lt R\) as shown in the Figure 16.17 with increasing amount of force \(F\text{.}\) What is the value of the force at which the log will start to move over?
Hint.
Normal from floor will be zero.
Answer.
\(\frac{\sqrt{R^2 - (R-a)^2}}{2R}\; Mg\text{.}\)
Solution.
Figure 16.18(a) shows the situation before the final value of \(F\text{.}\) As you increase \(F\text{,}\) the normal from floor decreases and at the critical value of \(F\text{,}\) as shown in (b), the normal from floor vanishes.
Figure 16.18(b) shows the lever arms of \(Mg\) and \(F\) from the tip of the step. The lever arms will be
\begin{equation*} r_{\perp w} = \sqrt{R^2 - (R-a)^2},\ \ \ r_{\perp F} = 2R. \end{equation*}
Balancing torque in the situation in Figure 16.18(b) gives
\begin{equation*} F r_{\perp F} = Mg r_{\perp w}, \end{equation*}
which gives
\begin{equation*} F = \frac{\sqrt{R^2 - (R-a)^2}}{2R}\; Mg. \end{equation*}
