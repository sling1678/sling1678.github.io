Example 35.19. Magnetic Field from Motion of an Electron in a Magnetic Field.

An electron of speed \(8.0 \times 10^6\, \text{m/s}\) moves uniformly in a circle of radius \(4\, \text{mm}\) when subjected to a uniform magnetic field perpendicular to its velocity. (a) Find the magnitude of magnetic force on the electron. (b) Find the magnetic field. Use \(m_e = 9.11\times 10^{-31}\,\text{kg}\text{.}\)
Answer.
(a) \(1.46\times 10^{-14}\,\text{N}\text{,}\) (b) \(1.14\times 10^{-2}\,\text{T}.\)
Solution 1. a
Since the motion is a uniform circular motion, we can get acceleration easily by
\begin{equation*} a = \frac{v^2}{R} = 1.6 \times 10^{16}\,\text{m/s}^2. \end{equation*}
Therefore, magnetic force has the magnitude
\begin{equation*} F_m = m_e\,a = 1.46\times 10^{-14}\,\text{N}. \end{equation*}
Solution 2. b
Since the direction between velocity and magnetic field is \(90^\circ\) the magnetic force formula gives
\begin{equation*} B = \frac{F_m}{Q v} = \frac{1.46\times 10^{-14}}{1.6\times 10^{-19}\times 8.0 \times 10^6} = 1.14\times 10^{-2}\,\text{T}. \end{equation*}
