Exercise 4. Potential Drop Across and Electric Field in a Wire Carrying Current.

A constant voltage power source across a 12-gauge Nichrome wire (radius \(0.0404 \text{ in}\)) of length \(20 \text{ m}\) produces a current of \(3 \text{ A}\text{.}\) (a) What is the voltage across the wire? (b) Find the electric field in the wire.
Data: \(\rho_\text{nichrome} = 1.0\times 10^{-6}\:\Omega.\text{m} \text{.}\)
Hint.
Electric field magnitude wil be potential drop divided by the length.
Answer.
(a) \(18.2\text{ V}\text{,}\) (b) \(0.91\text{ V/m}\text{.}\)
Solution 1. (a)
We assume same current throughout the wire. That means same current density throughout the wire. From Ohmâ€™s law, it means electric field is constant througout the wire. Therefore, we can get electric field by dividing the potental drop across the wire by the length of the wire.
To find the potential drop, we first work out the resistance.
\begin{align*} R \amp = \rho\:\frac{L}{A} \\ \amp = 1.0\times 10^{-6}\:\Omega.\text{m}\times \frac{10\:\text{m}}{\pi\: 0.0010262^2\:\text{m}^2} = 6.0\:\Omega. \end{align*}
Therefore, the voltage drop across the wire will be
\begin{equation*} \Delta \phi = I R = (3\:\text{A})(6.05\:\Omega) = 18.2\:\text{V}. \end{equation*}
Solution 2. (b)
The magnitude of the electric field in the wire will be
\begin{equation*} E = V/L = \frac{18.2\:\text{V}}{20\:\text{m}} = 0.91 \text{ V/m}. \end{equation*}
