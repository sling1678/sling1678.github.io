Example 10.51. Kinetic Energy of Rotation of a Rod.

A rod of mass \(M\) and length \(l\) is poivoted at one end as shown in FigureÂ 10.52. At a particular instant angular speed is \(\omega\text{.}\) Find kinetic energy of the rod in two ways: (a) rotation about \(P\text{,}\) (b) sum of translation of CM and rotation about the CM.
Answer.
\(\frac{1}{6}Ml^2 \omega^2\text{.}\)
Solution.
(a) For rotation about \(P\text{,}\) we need moment of inertia about \(P\text{,}\) which is \(I=\frac{1}{3}Ml^2\text{.}\) Therefore, kinetic energy will be
\begin{equation*} K = \frac{1}{2}I \omega^2 = \frac{1}{6}Ml^2 \omega^2. \end{equation*}
(b) Now, we have \(I_0 = \frac{1}{12}Ml^2\text{.}\) We also need the speed of CM. Since CM is moving in a circle of radius \(\frac{l}{2}\) with angular speed \(\omega\text{,}\) we get
\begin{equation*} V_\text{cm} = \frac{l}{2}\,\omega. \end{equation*}
Therefore, kinetic energy will be sum of CM translational energy and rotation about the CM.
\begin{align*} K \amp = \frac{1}{2}M V_\text{cm}^2 + \frac{1}{2}I_0 \omega^2 \\ \amp = \frac{1}{2}M \frac{l^2}{4}\,\omega^2 + \frac{1}{2} \frac{1}{12}Ml^2 \omega^2 \\ \amp = Ml^2 \omega^2\left( \frac{1}{8} + \frac{1}{24} \right) = \frac{1}{6}Ml^2 \omega^2. \end{align*}
