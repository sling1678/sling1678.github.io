Exercise 5. Susending a Styrofoam Ball by Radiation Pressure.

A Styrofoam spherical ball of radius 2 mm and mass 20 \(\mu\)g is to be suspended by radiation pressure in a vacuum tube in a earth-based lab. How much intensity will be needed if completely reflected by the ball?
Answer.
\(2.34 \times 10^6\, \text{W/m}^2\text{.}\)
Solution.
The upward force by the radiation pressure will equal \(F = 2P_\text{rad}\:A\) with \(A = \pi r^2\text{,}\) where the fact 2 is due to complete reflection from the surface. Equating this to \(mg\) gives
\begin{equation*} 2P_\text{rad}\:A = m g,\ \ \Longrightarrow\ \ P_\text{rad} = \dfrac{mg}{2\pi r^2}. \end{equation*}
Writing the radiation pressure in terms of the intensity we have \(P_\text{rad} = I/c\text{.}\) Hens,
\begin{equation*} I = \dfrac{mgc}{2\pi r^2}. \end{equation*}
Putting in the numerical values we obtain
\begin{equation*} I = \dfrac{20\times 10^{-6}\times 10^{-3}\:\textrm{kg}\times 9.81\:\textrm{m/s}^2\times 3\times 10^{8}\:\textrm{m/s}}{2\pi\times (0.002\:\textrm{m})^2} = 2.34\times 10^{6}\:\textrm{W/m}^2. \end{equation*}
