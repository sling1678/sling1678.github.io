Exercise 2. Relative Uncertainty of Your Height.

During the day suppose your height ranges between \(69\text{ in}\) in the morning and \(67\text{ in}\) in the evening. Letsâ€™ report this as average height \(68\text{ in}\) and uncertainty \(1 \text{ in}\text{.}\) Find relative uncertainty of your height.
Hint.
We have absolute uncertianty.
Answer.
\(1.5\%\text{.}\)
Solution.
From the average value and absolute uncertainty, we get
\begin{equation*} \frac{\Delta h}{h} = \frac{1\,\text{in}}{68\,\text{in}} = 0.0147 \rightarrow 1.5 \times 10^{-2} = 1.5\%. \end{equation*}
