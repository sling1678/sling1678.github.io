Exercise 2. Work Required for Bringing a Charge from Far to Near a Grounded Metal Plate.

A point charge is brought from infinity to a distance \(h\) from a grounded plane conductor. Find the amount of work done.
Solution.
Let the grounded plane be the \(x\)-plane and the real charge is brought on the \(z\)-axis from \(z=\infty\) to \(z=h\text{.}\) Note that the image moves as we move the real charge. Consider moving the real charge from a coordinate \(z\) to \(z-dz\text{.}\) The work by the external agent, which would apply the force in thh positive \(z\) direction to balance the electric force from the negatively charged image charge will be
\begin{equation*} dW = \dfrac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0}\: \dfrac{q^2}{(2z)^2}\: dz. \end{equation*}
Therefore, the net work done over the path from \(z=\infty\) to \(z=h\) will be
\begin{equation*} W = \dfrac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0}\: \int_{\infty}^{h}\dfrac{q^2}{(2z)^2}\: dz = - \dfrac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0}\:\dfrac{q^2}{4h}. \end{equation*}
