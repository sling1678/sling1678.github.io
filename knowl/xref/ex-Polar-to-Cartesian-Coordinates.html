Example 5.33. Polar to Cartesian Coordinates.

Convert the following polar coordinates \((r,\theta)\) into Cartesian coordinates. The angle is given in radians counterclockwise from the positive \(x\)-axis as seen from the side of the positive \(z\)-axis.
  1. \((1,0)\text{;}\)
  2. \((1,\pi/3)\text{;}\)
  3. \((1,2\pi/3)\text{;}\)
  4. \((1,3\pi/3)\text{;}\)
  5. \((1,4\pi/3)\text{;}\)
  6. \((1,5\pi/3)\text{;}\)
  7. \((1,6\pi/3)\text{;}\)
  8. \((2,29\pi/3)\text{.}\)
Answer.
See solution.
Solution.
Answers are
  1. \(x = 1.0\text{,}\) \(y=0\)
  2. \(x = 0.5\text{,}\) \(y=0.866\)
  3. \(x = -0.5\text{,}\) \(y=0.866\)
  4. \(x = -1.0\text{,}\) \(y=0\)
  5. \(x = -0.5\text{,}\) \(y=-0.866\)
  6. \(x = 0.5\text{,}\) \(y=.866\)
  7. \(x = 1.0\text{,}\) \(y=0\)
  8. \(x = -0.736\text{,}\) \(y=0.677\)
