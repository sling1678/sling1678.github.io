Exercise 3. Calculating Circulation of Magnetic Field on Various Loops Ex3.

A magnetic field exists in a region of space such that the magnitude of the field varies with distance \(r\) from the \(z\)-axis as
\begin{equation*} B = \begin{cases} B_0 \amp r\lt R\\ 0 \amp r\gt R \end{cases} \end{equation*}
where \(B_0\) is a constant, and the direction of the non-zero field is towards the positive \(z\)-axis as shown in the figure below. Find the circulation of the magnetic field for the closed loops (a)-(e). Note the loops have been drawn in different planes.
Solution 1. a
Since \(vec B\) is perpendicular to the line elements of the loop, the circulation will be zero dur to \(\vec B\cdot d\vec l = 0\) on each line element \(d\vec l\text{.}\)
Solution 2. b
Same as (a).
Solution 3. c
Here the two line segments parallel to the \(z\)-axis will give non-zero contributions to the line integral \(\oint\vec B\cdot d\vec l\text{,}\) but their signs will be opposite since while on the left side \(\vec B \parallel d\vec l\text{,}\) on the right side they are antiparallel. The circulation around the entire loop will equal zero.
Solution 4. d
Circulation will be zero. [Can you see why? Think doing the line integral on each segment.]
Solution 5. e
The only non-zero contribution will come from the path at \(x=0\text{.}\) Let \(l_z\) be the length of the path on at \(x=0\text{.}\) We will get circulation = \(B_0\:l_z\text{.}\)
