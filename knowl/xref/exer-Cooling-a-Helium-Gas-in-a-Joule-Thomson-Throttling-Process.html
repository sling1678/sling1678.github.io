Exercise 1. Cooling a Helium Gas in a Joule-Thomson Throttling Process.

In a throttling process \(1.4\) mol of the Helium gas is to be cooled from \(20^{\circ}\)C to \(10^{\circ}\)C by expanding from a \(40\) atm to \(1\) atm. (a) What would be the corresponding volume change? (b) What is the change in the internal energy?
Solution 1. a
This is an exercise in formula manipulations. We use the ideal gas law in combination with the Joule-Thompson throttling equation. The ideal gas law is applicable to each state of an ideal system. Here, if the system before the throttling is same as the system after the throttling, then the ideal gas law will imply that \(\Delta U = \Delta (pV) = nR\Delta T\text{.}\)
\begin{align*} V_2 - V_1 \amp = n R \left(\frac{T_2}{p_2} -\frac{T_1}{p_1} \right) \\ \amp = 1.4\times 0.082\times \left(\frac{10+273.15}{1} -\frac{20+273}{40} \right) = 31.7\ \textrm{L}. \end{align*}
Solution 2. b
\begin{equation*} \Delta U = \Delta (pV) = nR\Delta T = -116\ \textrm{J}. \end{equation*}
