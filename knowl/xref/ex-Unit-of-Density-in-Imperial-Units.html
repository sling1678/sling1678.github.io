Exercise 4. Unit of Density in Imperial Units.
The density of aluminum is 2.7 grams per cubic-centimeter. (a) What is the density in kilogram/cubic meter? (b) What is the density in \(\text{lb/ft}^3\text{.}\)
Hint.
Beware of three factors of \(cm\text{,}\) each needs to be converted!
Answer.
(a) \(2.7\times 10^3\,\text{kg/m}^3\text{,}\) (b) \(168.8\, \text{lb/ft}^3\text{.}\)
Solution 1. (a)
(a)
\begin{equation*} 2.7\,\frac{\text{g}}{\text{cm}^3} = 2.7\,\frac{10^{-3}\text{kg}}{\left(10^{-2}\right)^3\,\text{m}^3} = 2.7\times 10^3\,\text{kg/m}^3. \end{equation*}
Solution 2. (b)
(b)
\begin{align*} 2.7\,\frac{\text{g}}{\text{cm}^3} \amp = 2.7\,\frac{(1/453)\text{lb}}{\left((1/2.54)\times(1/12)\right)^3\,\text{ft}^3} \\ \amp = 168.8\, \text{lb/ft}^3. \end{align*}
