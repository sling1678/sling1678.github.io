Example 32.35. Charges on two Concentric Spherical Metals Connected by a Battery.

Two concentric spherical metal shells separated by nonconducting air have a capacitance of \(300\text{ pF}\text{.}\) What are the charges on the two shells when the potential difference between them is \(1.5\text{ V}\text{?}\)
Answer.
\(\pm 450\:\text{pC} \text{.}\)
Solution.
Using the fundamental capacitor formula we obtain
\begin{equation*} Q = C \Delta \phi = 300\: \text{pF}\times 15\:\text{V} = 450\:\text{pC}. \end{equation*}
