Exercise 5. Motion of Two Pieces in a Two Dimensional Explosion.

Figure 7.88 shows a rocket of mass \(M\) moving in space with speed \(v_0=110\text{ m/s}\) with respect to ground, and then exploding in two pieces. Let the masses of the two pieces A and B be \(0.2M\) and \(0.8M\) respectively. Piece A is seen to move with speed \(u\) in the direction \(\phi=60^\circ\) as seen by a ground-based observer. Larger piece B moves with speed \(V\) in the direction \(\theta=30^\circ\) by the ground-based observer. Find values of \(u\) and \(V\text{.}\)
Hint.
Apply conservation of momentum along \(x\) and \(y\) axis.
Answer.
\(u=550\text{ m/s}\text{,}\) \(V=238\text{ m/s}\text{.}\)
Solution.
Using Figure 7.88 we can write conservation of momentum along \(x\) and \(y\) axis.
\begin{align} \amp 0.8 M V \cos\theta - 0.2 M u \cos\phi = M v_0. \tag{7.81}\\ \amp 0.8 M V \sin\theta - 0.2 M u \sin\phi = 0. \tag{7.82} \end{align}
From the second equation, we get
\begin{equation*} V = \frac{1}{4}\,u\,\frac{\sin\phi}{\sin\theta} = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{4}\,u. \end{equation*}
Using this in Eq. (7.82) gives
\begin{equation*} 0.8\times \frac{\sqrt{3}}{4}\,u\,\cos\theta - 0.2\,\,\cos\phi = v_0. \end{equation*}
Solving this for \(u\text{,}\) we get
\begin{equation*} u = 5 v_0 = 550\text{ m/s}. \end{equation*}
Therefore,
\begin{equation*} V = \frac{5\sqrt{3}}{4}v_0 = 238\text{ m/s}. \end{equation*}
