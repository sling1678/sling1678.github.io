Example 31.19. Electrostatic Energy of Charges at Corners of a Square.

Four charges, \(+q,\ -q,\ +q,\ -q \) alternate on corners of a square of side \(a\text{.}\) What is the electrostatic energy?
Answer.
\(-\dfrac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0}\; \dfrac{q^2}{a}(4-\sqrt{2})\text{.}\)
Solution.
Electrostatic energy has contribution from every pair counted only once. We have pairs \((1,2)\text{,}\) \((1,3)\text{,}\) \((1,4)\text{,}\) \((2,3)\text{,}\) \((2,4)\text{,}\) \((3,4)\text{.}\) The pairs \((1,3)\) and \((2,4)\) have positive energy and the others have negative.
Adding them up, we get
\begin{align*} U \amp = \dfrac{q^2}{4\pi\epsilon_0}\left( -4\times \dfrac{1}{a} + 2\times \dfrac{1}{\sqrt{2}\; a} \right)\\ \amp= -\dfrac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0}\; \dfrac{q^2}{a}(4-\sqrt{2}). \end{align*}
