Example 27.17. Internal Energy of Monatomic and Diatomic Ideal Gasses.

What are the internal energies of \(2\) moles of (a) a monatomic ideal gas and (b) a diatomic ideal gas at \(300\text{K}\) where no vibrational modes are excited, but translational and rotational modes are excited?
Answer.
(a) \(7,479\,\text{J}\text{,}\) (b) \(12,465\,\text{J}\text{.}\)
Solution 1. a
For each mode excited, we have \(U_\text{one mode} = \frac{1}{2}nRT\text{.}\) Letâ€™s comupte this
\begin{equation*} U_\text{one mode} = \frac{1}{2}nRT = \frac{1}{2} \times 2 \times 8.31\times 300 = 2,493\,\text{J}. \end{equation*}
In the monoatomic gas, three translational modes are the only modes. Therefore,
\begin{equation*} U = 3\times U_\text{one mode} = 7,479\,\text{J}. \end{equation*}
Solution 2. b
In the diatomic gas, we have three translational modes plus two rotational modes. Therefore,
\begin{equation*} U = 5\times U_\text{one mode} = 12,465\,\text{J}. \end{equation*}
