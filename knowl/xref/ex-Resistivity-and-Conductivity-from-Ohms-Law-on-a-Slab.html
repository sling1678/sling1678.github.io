Exercise 3. Resistivity and Conductivity from Ohm’s Law on a Slab.

A newly discovered material is made into a slab of width \(3.0 \text{ mm}\text{,}\) length \(2.0 \text{ cm}\text{,}\) and thickness \(1.0 \text{ mm}\text{.}\) In an experiment, it is noticed that \(2.0 \text{ mV}\) applied across its length prodices \(1.0\ \mu\text{A}\) current. (a) Evaluate the resisitivity of the material. (b) What is the conductivity of the material?
Hint.
(a) Use Ohm’s law and then use \(R\) in terms of \(\rho\text{.}\) (b) Use \(\rho= 1/\sigma\text{.}\)
Answer.
(a) \(0.3\ \Omega.\text{m}\text{,}\) (b) \(3.3\ \Omega^{-1}\text{m}^{-1}\text{.}\)
Solution 1. (a)
First we find the resistance from the current/voltage data.
\begin{equation*} R = \frac{\Delta \phi}{I} = \frac{2\text{ mV}}{1.0\ \mu\text{A}} = 2000\ \Omega. \end{equation*}
Using the Ohm’s law formula into the resistance/resistivity/conductivity formula we obtain
\begin{align*} \rho \amp = \frac{A R}{L}\\ \amp = \frac{( (0.001\times 0.003)\:\text{m}^2)\times 2000\ \Omega}{0.02\:\text{m}} = 0.3\:\Omega.\text{m}. \end{align*}
Solution 2. (b)
The resistivity is inverse of conductivity
\begin{equation*} \sigma = \frac{1}{\rho} = 3.3\: \frac{1}{\Omega.\text{m}}. \end{equation*}
