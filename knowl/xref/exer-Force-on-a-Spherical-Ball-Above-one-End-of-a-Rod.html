Exercise 6. Force on a Spherical Ball Above one End of a Rod.

There is a small sphere of mass \(m\) from the edge of a uniform rod of length \(L\) and mass \(M\) as shown in Figure 12.19. Find the gravitational force on the mass m.
Solution.
We follow similar procedure as given in Figure 12.20. We start with a figure in which we define the coordinates we will use.
Here, the force on the sphere will have both \(x\)- and \(y\)-components. The \(x\)- and \(y\)-components of the force from the mass in the element between \(x\) and \(x+dx\) are given by
\begin{align*} \amp dF_x = G_N\frac{m\ dM}{ (L/2 - x)^2 + D^2} \frac{-(L/2 - x)}{\sqrt{(L/2 - x)^2 + D^2}}.\\ \amp dF_y = G_N\frac{m\ dM}{ (L/2 - x)^2 + D^2} \frac{-D}{\sqrt{(L/2 - x)^2 + D^2}}. \end{align*}
We need to integrate these expressions to find the \(F_x\) and \(F_y\) of the force by the entire rod on the sphere.
\begin{align*} \amp F_x = -G_N\frac{mM}{L} \left[\frac{1}{D} - \frac{1}{\sqrt{L^2+D^2}} \right].\\ \amp F_y =- G_N\frac{mM}{ D \sqrt{L^2 + D^2}}. \end{align*}
