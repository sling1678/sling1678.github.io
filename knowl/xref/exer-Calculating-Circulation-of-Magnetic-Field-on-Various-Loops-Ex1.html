Exercise 1. Calculating Circulation of Magnetic Field on Various Loops Ex1.

A magnetic field is uniform with magnitude \(B_0\) above \(z=0\) plane and points towards the positive \(x\)-axis and uniform with with same magnitude \(B_0\) below \(z=0\) axis but points towards the negative \(x\)-axis as shown in FigureÂ 36.41. Find the circulation of the magnetic field for the closed loops (a)-(f). Note the loops have been drawn in different planes.
Solution 1. a
The circulation around this loop will be zero since the contribution of the bottom part of the loop is negative and cancels the contribution from the top. The contributions of the sides are zero since \(\vec B\) is perpendicular to the line elements.
Solution 2. b
Let the path have length \(l_x\) parallel to the \(x\)-axis. The line integral \(\oint\: \vec B\cdot d\vec l\) has zero value on the sides since \(vec B\) is perpendicular to the line elements, positive values for both the top part and the bottom part since in each part the line directed elements have the same direction as the direction of the field. Hence, circulation will be equal to \(2 B_0 l_x\text{.}\)
Solution 3. c,d,e,f
(c), (d), (e), and (f) will all give zero circulation.
