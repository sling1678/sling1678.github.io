Exercise 2. Pressure in a Tank of Water on Moon.

Acceleration due to gravity at moon is \(\dfrac{g}{6}\text{.}\) What is the pressure at a depth of \(2\text{ m}\) in a tank of water at moon?
Hint.
Use the fact that there is no atmosphere at moon.
Answer.
\(3270\text{ Pa}\text{.}\)
Solution.
Since there is no atmosphere, the pressure will come from weight of the fluid particles alone.
\begin{align*} p \amp= \rho g_{\text{moon}} h\\ \amp= 1000\ \text{kg/m}^3 \times \dfrac{9.81\ \text{m/s}^2}{6} \times 2\ \text{m} = 3270\ \text{Pa}. \end{align*}
