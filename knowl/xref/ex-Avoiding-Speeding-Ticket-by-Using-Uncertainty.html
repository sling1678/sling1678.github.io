Exercise 4. Avoiding Speeding Ticket by Using Uncertainty.

Suppose the manufacturer of speedometer of your car specifies that the reading has uncertainty of \(5\%\text{.}\) The speed limit posted on the road is \(70\text{ mph}\text{.}\) (a) If you drive at \(75\text{ mph}\text{,}\) by how much you could be above the speed limit? (b) At what speed on this meter that you could safely be below the speed limit?
Hint.
Work with the fraction rather than percentage notation for the relative uncertainty and pay attention the specific wordings of the questions.
Answer.
(a) \(1.2\,\text{mph}\text{,}\) (b) \(74.0\,\text{mph}\)
Solution 1. (a)
The uncertainty \(\Delta v/v = 5\%\) in fraction notation means
\begin{equation*} \frac{\Delta v}{ v } = \frac{5}{100} = 0.05. \end{equation*}
With \(v = 75\, \text{mph}\text{,}\) we get
\begin{equation*} \Delta v = 0.05\,v = 0.05 \times 75 = 3.75 \rightarrow 3.8\,\text{mph}. \end{equation*}
You could be going at \(75 - 3.8 = 71.2\) mph. That means you could be above the posted speed limit by \(1.2\) mph.
Solution 2. (b)
Let \(x\) be the required speed. From (a) above, we see that we want
\begin{equation*} x - 0.05 x = 70. \end{equation*}
Therefore,
\begin{equation*} x = 73.68 \rightarrow 74.0\,\text{mph}. \end{equation*}
