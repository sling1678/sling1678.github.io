Exercise 2. Contact Angle in Capillary Tube in Water.

Water rises to a height of \(2.5\text{ cm}\) in a capillary tube of radius\(0.50\text{ mm}\text{.}\) If surface tension of water at that temperature is \(72 mN/m\text{,}\) what is the angle of contact?
Hint.
Use capillary action formula.
Answer.
\(31.6^\circ\text{.}\)
Solution.
We will use the capillary action formula derived in this section.
\begin{equation*} h = \frac{2\gamma\,\cos\theta}{ \rho g r}. \end{equation*}
We use \(\rho = 1000\,\text{kg/m}^3\text{.}\) We get
\begin{equation*} \cos\theta = \frac{1000\times 9.81 \times 0.5\times 10^{-3} \times 2.5\times 10^{-2} }{ 2\times 0.5\times 10^{-3} } = 0.85. \end{equation*}
This gives \(\theta = 31.6^\circ\text{.}\)
