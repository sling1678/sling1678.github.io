Exercise 4. A Cubical Box with Open End Floating in Mercury.

A cubical box of side 10 cm made of steel has an open side. With open side of the box up, 90\% of the box floats in mercury. How much of the box will float if 200 gram of load is put inside the box?
Solution.
We already know that that only 1 cm is above the mercury. Now, if we put 200 g more, then the additional buoyancy we need will come from the decrease in this 1 cm. Let the decrease be \(\Delta h\text{.}\) This additional buoyancy should balance the additional weight. Let us denote the area of the base by \(A\text{,}\) the density of the mercury by \(\rho\text{,}\) the additional mass by \(m\text{.}\) Then we have
\begin{equation*} (A \Delta h) \rho g = mg. \end{equation*}
This gives
\begin{equation*} \Delta h = \frac{m}{A \rho} = \frac{0.2\ \textrm{kg}}{0.01\ \textrm{m}^2 \times 13,600\ \textrm{kg/m}^3} = 0.147\ \textrm{cm}. \end{equation*}
With the load the container will have only 1 cm - 0.147 cm = 0.953 cm above mercury.
