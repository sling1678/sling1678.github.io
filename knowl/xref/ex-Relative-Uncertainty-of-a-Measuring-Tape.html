Exercise 3. Relative Uncertainty of a Measuring Tape.

A measuring tape is used to measure a hallway. The result is found to be \(25\text{ m}\text{,}\) but you are sure that this reading may be off on either side by \(5\text{ cm}\text{.}\) What is the relative uncertainty of the measurement in percent notation?
Hint.
Use \(L\) and \(\Delta L\text{.}\)
Answer.
\(20\%\)
Solution.
The measurement given is \(25\,\text{m} \pm 5\,\text{m}\text{.}\) Therefore, relative uncertainty will be
\begin{equation*} \frac{\Delta L}{L} = \frac{5\,\text{m}}{25\,\text{m}} = 0.20 \rightarrow 2.0 \times 10^{-1} = 20\%. \end{equation*}
