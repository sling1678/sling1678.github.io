Exercise 4. Adding Three Velocity Vectors.

Three velocity vectors fall in the \(xy\)-plane of a coordinate system and have the following representations: \(\vec V_1 = (3\ \textrm{m/s})\hat u_x + (2\ \textrm{m/s})\hat u_y\text{,}\) \(\vec V_2 = (9\ \textrm{m/s})\hat u_x + (11\ \textrm{m/s})\hat u_y\text{,}\) and \(\vec V_3 = (-8\ \textrm{m/s})\hat u_y\text{.}\) (a) Find the \(x\) and \(y\)-components of the sum of the three vectors. (b) Determine the magnitude and direction of the sum.
Solution 1. a
Let us denote the sum by symbol \(\vec V\text{.}\) The \(x\)- and \(y\)-components of the sum will be equal to the sum of the \(x\)- and \(y\)-components of the other vectors.
\begin{align*} \amp V_x = (3\ \textrm{m/s}) + (9\ \textrm{m/s}) = 12\ \textrm{m/s}\\ \amp V_y = (2\ \textrm{m/s}) + (11\ \textrm{m/s}) + (-8\ \textrm{m/s}) = 5\ \textrm{m/s}. \end{align*}
Solution 2. b
The magnitude of the sum vector = \(\sqrt{V_x^2 + V_y^2} = 13\ \textrm{m/s}\text{.}\) The direction will be in the first quadrant of the \(xy\)-plane. We can denote the direction here by the angle \(\theta\) that the vector \(\vec V\) makes with respect to the positive \(x\)-axis as we have done before.
\begin{equation*} \theta = \tan^{-1}\left( \frac{V_y}{V_x}\right) = 23^{\circ}. \end{equation*}
