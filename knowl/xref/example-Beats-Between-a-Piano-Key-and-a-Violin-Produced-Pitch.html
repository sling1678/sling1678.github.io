Example 14.39. Beats Between a Piano Key and a Violin Produced Pitch.

A piano key of \(512\, \text{Hz}\) is producing \(500\, \text{Hz}\) sound. (a) What will be the beat frequency if it is checked against a violin producing \(520\, \text{Hz}\text{?}\) (b) If the wire corresponding to the key is \(1.2\) meters long, and has a mass density of \(0.1\, \text{kg/m}\text{,}\) by what percentage should the tension of the wire be adjusted so that the pitch is adjusted to \(512\, \text{Hz}\text{?}\) Assume insignificant change in mass per unit length while tightening.
Answer.
(a) \(20\, \text{Hz}\text{,}\) (b) \(5\%\text{.}\)
Solution 1. a
By the beat frequency formula, we get
\begin{equation*} f_{\text{beat}} = |500 - 520| = 20\, \text{Hz}. \end{equation*}
Solution 2. b
Initially, we have
\begin{align*} \amp f = 500\ \text{Hz}\\ \amp \lambda = 2.4\ \text{m} \end{align*}
Therefore,
\begin{equation*} v = \lambda f = 1200\ \text{m/s}, \end{equation*}
which gives the initial tension to be
\begin{equation*} T = v^2 \mu = (1200\ \text{m/s})^2 \times 0.1\ \text{kg/m} = 1.44\times 10^{5}\ \text{N}. \end{equation*}
The desired frequency parameters are
\begin{equation*} f' = 512\ \text{Hz},\ \ \lambda' = 2.4\ \text{m} \end{equation*}
This means
\begin{equation*} v' = \lambda' f' = 1228.8\ \text{m/s}, \end{equation*}
and
\begin{equation*} T' = v'^2 \mu = 1.51\times 10^5\ \text{N}. \end{equation*}
Therefore, the change in the magnitude of the tension will be
\begin{equation*} T' - T = 0.07\times 10^5 = 7.0\times 10^3\ \text{N}. \end{equation*}
Or, the percentage change
\begin{equation*} \% = \frac{T'-T}{T}\times 100\% = 4.9\%. \end{equation*}
