Exercise 1. Draw Vectors to Scale on a Graph Paper.

In this exercise you will draw two vectors to scale on a graph paper, and then add them graphically. Two forces act on an object: a force of \(3\) Newton(N) in some direction and \(4\) N force in the direction \(90^{\circ}\) from the direction of the \(3\) N force such that the two forces fall in a plane.
  1. Choose a scale for drawing, such as a \(1\ \text{cm}\) line for \(1\ \text{N}\text{,}\) and draw the vectors on the same graph paper using your scale. Make sure that you give your scale with the figure.
  2. Draw or redraw the vectors so that the tail of the second vector is at the tip of the first vector. Use tip-to-tail method to draw the vector that is equal to the sum of the two vectors.
  3. Use the scale for the drawing and convert the length of the sum vector on the graph to determine the magnitude of the sum.
  4. Use a protractor and read off the direction of the sum vector with respect to the force whose magnitude is \(3\ \text{N}\text{.}\)
