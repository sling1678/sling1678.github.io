Exercise 2. Rolling a Ball Inside a Funnel.

A ball of mass \(0.150\text{ kg}\) is going around in a circle of radius \(0.4\text{ m}\) inside a funnel as shown in Figure 6.123 at a constant speed of \(3.0\text{ m/s}\text{.}\) If the speed was any less or any more, the ball will slide. Let coefficient of static friction between ball and the funnel to have the value \(\mu_s=0.5\text{.}\) The half angle of the apex of the cone is \(\theta\text{.}\)
Find angle \(\theta\text{,}\) centripetal acceleration, tangential acceleration and magnitude of the normal force on the ball from the funnel. Assume maximum static friction acting down the incline direction of the funnel.
Answer.
\(51^\circ\text{.}\)
Solution.
From Figure 6.124, we get the following equations along the two axes.
\begin{align*} \amp N\cos\theta + \mu N \sin\theta = m a_c \\ \amp N\sin\theta - \mu_s N \cos\theta - mg = 0 \end{align*}
Send \(mg\) to the right and divide out one equation by another.
\begin{equation*} \frac{\cos\theta + \mu \sin\theta}{\sin\theta - \mu_s \cos\theta } = \frac{a_c}{g}. \end{equation*}
Now, we divide numerator and denominator by \(\cos\theta\text{.}\)
\begin{equation*} \frac{1 + \mu \tan\theta}{\tan\theta - \mu_s } = \frac{a_c}{g}. \end{equation*}
Now, we cross-multiply and solve for \(\tan\theta\text{.}\) I got
\begin{equation*} \tan\theta = \frac{g + \mu a_c }{ a_c - \mu g}. \end{equation*}
Now, we plug in the values \(a_c = v^2/R = 3^2/0.4 = 22.5\,\text{m/s}^2\text{,}\) \(g = 9.8\,\text{m/s}^2\text{,}\) \(\mu = 0.5\text{.}\) I got \(\theta = 51^\circ\text{.}\)
