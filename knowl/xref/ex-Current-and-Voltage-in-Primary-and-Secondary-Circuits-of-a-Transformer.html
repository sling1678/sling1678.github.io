Example 41.56. Current and Voltage in Primary and Secondary Circuits of a Transformer.

The output voltage and input currents of a \(700\text{-W}\) transformer are \(9\text{-V}\) (rms) and \(2\text{ A}\) (rms).
(a) What are the input voltage and output current?
(b) What are the ratio of primary turns to secondary turns?
(c) Is this a step-up or step-down transformer?
Answer.
(a) \(350\,\text{V}\text{,}\) (b) \(39\text{,}\) (c) Step-down.
Solution 1. (a)
Let \(V_\text{in}\) be the rms-voltage at the input. Then equating the product of rms-voltage and rms-current at the input to the given power of the transformer we will get
\begin{equation*} V_{\text{in}}\times 2\:\text{A} = 700\:\text{W}\ \ \Longrightarrow\ \ V_{\text{in}} = 350\:\text{V}. \end{equation*}
Let \(I_{\text{out}}\) be the rms-voltage at the input. Then equating the product of rms-voltage and rms-current at the input to the given power of the transformer we will get
\begin{equation*} I_{\text{out}}\times 9\:\text{V} = 700\:\text{W}\ \ \Longrightarrow\ \ I_{\text{out}} = 77.8\:\text{A}. \end{equation*}
Solution 2. (b)
Let \(N_p\) number of turns in the primary (input) and \(N_s\) number of turns in the secondary (output). Equating the ratio to the ratio of the voltages we get
\begin{equation*} \dfrac{N_p}{N_s} = \dfrac{350\:\text{V}}{9\:\text{V}} = 39. \end{equation*}
Solution 3. (c)
Since \(V_s \lt V_p\text{,}\) the transformer is a step-down transformer.
