Exercise 6. Determine if a Diffraction Grating can Resolve Mercury Yellow-Orange Doublet.

The spectrum of mercury has a pair of yellow orange light at \(576.959\ \text{nm}\) and \(579.065\, \text{nm}\text{.}\) A diffraction grating with \(200\, \text{lines per mm}\) is available in the lab. Determine if this grating will do the job of resolving the two mercury yellow orange lines if the beam diameter is \(4\, \text{mm}\text{.}\) If not, what is the minimum number of lines per mm is needed for this task if it needs to be resolved in the first-order?
Hint.
Use resolving power needed to resolve the two wavelengths and compare that to the resolving power of the available grating.
Answer.
Yes, given grating will be enough.
Solution.
Note that the beam will cover 800 lines. The resolving power needed is
\begin{equation*} R = \dfrac{\Delta \lambda}{\lambda} = 274.5. \end{equation*}
For the order \(m=1\) we have \(R = N\text{.}\) Therefore, we need \(275\) lines of the grating over which the beam is to be incident. Since the beam covers \(800\) lines of the grating, the lines would be resolved in the first order.
