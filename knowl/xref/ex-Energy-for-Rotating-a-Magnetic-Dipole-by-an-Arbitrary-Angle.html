Exercise 6. Energy for Rotating a Magnetic Dipole by an Arbitrary Angle.

Determine the energy needed to flip the dipole of moment \(10^{-3}\text{ A.m}^2\) in a field of \(2\text{ T}\) from being \(160^{\circ}\) direction to the field to \(20^{\circ}\) direction.
Hint.
Use definition.
Answer.
\(-3.8\times 10^{-3}\ \text{J}\text{.}\)
Solution.
This problem uses the dot product nature of energy formula for a dipole.
\begin{equation*} U = -\vec \mu \cdot \vec B. \end{equation*}
Therefore,
\begin{align*} \Delta U \amp = -\mu\:B\:\cos\:\theta_2 + \mu\:B\:\cos\:\theta_1\\ \amp = -2\times 10^{-3}\:( \cos\:20^{\circ} -\cos\:160^{\circ}) = -3.76\:\text{mJ}. \end{align*}
The negative sign means that the dipole will lose the energy in the process.
