Exercise 4. Circular Motion of a Block Supported by a Hanging Mass.

A string is passed through a hole in a table and two blocks are attached to the two ends of the string as shown in FigureÂ 6.125. The block on the table (\(m_1\)) is rotated in a circle of radius \(R\text{.}\) When the angular velocity has a critical value \(\omega=\omega_c\text{,}\) the hanging mass \(m_2\) does not move at all.
(a) Find an expression of the critical value \(\omega_c\) in terms of the given quantities and \(g\text{.}\) Assume table to be frictionless. (b) If \(m_1=1.0\text{ kg}\text{,}\) \(m_2=2.0\text{ kg}\text{,}\) \(R=0.5\text{ m}\text{,}\) what is the speed of the block \(m_1\) when at cirtical angular speed?
Answer Hint: (a) when \(m_1=m_2\text{,}\) \(R=1\text{,}\) \(\omega_c=\sqrt{g}\text{.}\)
Hint.
(1) You need two Free Body diagrams. (2) \(v = \omega r\) is the relation between speed, angular speed, and radius of the circle.
Solution. a
Since the acceleration of the hanging mass \(a=0\text{,}\) we have
\begin{equation} T - m_2 g = 0.\tag{6.55} \end{equation}
For the circularly moving block, accleleration is in the horizontal plane towards the circle. Only tension force has nonzero component in that direction. Thus, we have
\begin{equation} T = m_1\frac{v^2}{R} = m_1 \omega^2 R.\tag{6.56} \end{equation}
From these two equations of motion, it is easy to eliminate \(T\text{.}\)
\begin{equation*} m_1 \omega^2 R = m_2 g. \end{equation*}
Hence,
\begin{equation*} \omega = \sqrt{ \frac{m_2 g}{m_1 R} }. \end{equation*}
