Example 8.36. Spring Potential Energy of a Block.

A block is attached to a spring of spring constant \(1000\text{ N/m}\text{.}\)
(a) What is the spring potential energy of the block if the spring is compressed by \(5\text{ cm}\text{?}\)
(b) What is the spring potential energy of the block if the spring is stretched by \(5\text{ cm}\text{?}\)
(c) The block is oscillating. At one point A the spring is compressed by \(5\text{ cm}\) and at another point B the spring is stretched by \(2\text{ cm}\text{.}\) What is the change in potential energy, \(U_B - U_A\text{?}\)
Answer.
(a) \(1.25\text{ J}\text{,}\) (b) \(1.25\text{ J}\text{,}\) (c) \(-1.05\text{ J}\text{.}\)
Solution 1. a
We use the formula of potential energy due to a spring.
\begin{equation*} U = \dfrac{1}{2}k x^2 = \dfrac{1}{2}\times 1000\times 0.05^2 = 1.25\text{ J}. \end{equation*}
Solution 2. b
We use the formula of potential energy due to a spring. We should get the same value as in (a) since we square the change in length of the spring.
\begin{equation*} U = \dfrac{1}{2}k x^2 = \dfrac{1}{2}\times 1000\times 0.05^2 = 1.25\text{ J}. \end{equation*}
Solution 3. c
We use the formula of potential energy due to a spring.
\begin{align*} U \amp = \dfrac{1}{2}k x_f^2 - \dfrac{1}{2}k x_i^2 \\ \amp = \dfrac{1}{2}\times 1000\times 0.02^2 - \dfrac{1}{2}\times 1000\times 0.05^2\\ \amp = -1.05\text{ J}. \end{align*}
