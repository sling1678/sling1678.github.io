Example 34.16. Resistance from Resitivity, Length, and Thickness.

Find the resistances of \(10\text{ m}\) of (a) 14-gauge copper wire (diameter \(0.0642 \text{ in}\)), (b) 10-gauge copper wire (diameter \(0.102 \text{ in}\)), and (c) 14-gauge nichrome wire.
Data: \(\rho_\text{Cu} = 1.7\times 10^{-8}\:\Omega.\text{m}\text{,}\) \(\rho_\text{nichrome} = 1.0\times 10^{-6}\:\Omega.\text{m}\text{.}\)
Answer.
(a) \(8.1\times 10^{-2}\Omega\text{,}\) (b) \(3.2\times 10^{-2}\Omega\text{,}\) (c) \(4.8\Omega\text{.}\)
Solution.
Let us first convert the diameters in metric unit.
\begin{align*} D_{10} \amp = 0.102\: \text{in} = 2.59\times 10^{-3}\:\text{m}, \\ D_{14} \amp = 0.0642\: \text{in} = 1.63\times 10^{-3}\:\text{m}. \end{align*}
Use the formula of resistance of a wire.
\begin{align*} R_{14} \amp = \rho\:\frac{L}{A} \\ \amp = 1.7\times 10^{-8}\:\Omega.\text{m}\times \frac{10\:\text{m}}{\pi (0.00163/2)^2\:\text{m}^2} = 8.1\times 10^{-2}\:\Omega. \end{align*}
\begin{align*} R_{10} \amp = \rho\:\frac{L}{A}\\ \amp = 1.7\times 10^{-8}\:\Omega.\text{m}\times \frac{10\:\text{m}}{\pi (0.00259/2)^2\:\text{m}^2} = 3.2\times 10^{-2}\:\Omega. \end{align*}
For this part we need the resistivity of nichrome.
\begin{align*} R \amp = \rho\:\frac{L}{A}\\ \amp = 1.0\times 10^{-6}\:\Omega.\text{m}\times \frac{10\:\text{m}}{\pi (0.00259/2)^2\:\text{m}^2} = 4.8 \:\Omega. \end{align*}
