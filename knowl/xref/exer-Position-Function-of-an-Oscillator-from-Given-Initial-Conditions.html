Exercise 3. Position Function of an Oscillator from Given Initial Conditions.

An oscillator of frequency \(20\) cycles per second starts \(3\) cm from the equilibrium so that spring is in stretched state and oscillator has initial velocity of \(40\,\pi\) cm/s pointed towards the equilibrium point. (a) Find \(x(t)\) for the oscillator. (b) What is the amplitude of the motion of the oscillator?
Answer.
(a) \(3\, \text{cm}\,\cos( 40\pi t) + (-1)\,\text{cm}\,\sin(40\pi t)\text{,}\) (b) \(\sqrt{10}\,\text{cm}\text{.}\)
Solution 1. a
We will use a combination of sine and cosine to work with.
\begin{equation} x(t) = A \cos(2\pi f t) + B \sin(2\pi f t),\tag{13.24} \end{equation}
where \(f = 20\) Hz, and we need to find \(A\) and \(B\) from the intial conditions on position and velocity. They are given as
\begin{equation*} x(0) = 3\ \textrm{cm};\ \ v_x(0) = -40\pi\, \textrm{cm/s}. \end{equation*}
Note the minus sign in \(v_x(0)\) to reflect the fact that the block is moving towards the negative \(x\)-axis at this instant. To find \(v_x(t)\) we take the time derivative of \(x(t)\) in Eq. (13.24). The initial conditions give the following conditions on \(A\) and \(B\text{.}\)
\begin{align*} \amp A = 3\ \textrm{cm} \\ \amp2\pi f B = -40\pi\, \textrm{cm/s}. \end{align*}
Therefore, we have the following for \(x(t)\text{.}\)
\begin{equation*} x(t) = (3\ \textrm{cm})\ \cos(40\pi t) - 1\,\text{cm}\, \sin(40\pi t). \end{equation*}
Solution 2. b
To find the amplitude of motion, we combine the amplitudes of cosine and sine terms in the solution as follows.
\begin{equation*} \text{Amplitude } = \sqrt{A_c^2 + A_s^2} = \sqrt{3^2 + 1^2} = \sqrt{10}\,\text{cm}. \end{equation*}
