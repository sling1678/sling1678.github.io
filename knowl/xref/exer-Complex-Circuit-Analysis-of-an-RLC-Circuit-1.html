Exercise 5. Complex Circuit Analysis of an RL Circuit 1.

In the circuit shown in the figure, find the followig (a) the current in the circuit, (b) phase angle between the current in the circuit and the voltage drop across the resistor,
(c) phase angle between the current in the circuit and the voltage drop across the inductor, (d) phase angle between the current in the circuit and the voltage drop across the capacitor, (e) the power factor, (f) real impedance of the circuit, and (g) average power dissipated in the circuit.
Answer.
(a) \((3.2 \times 10^{-7}\,\text{A})\, \cos(120\pi t + \pi/2)\text{.}\)
Solution 1. a
To find the current in the circuit, let us first evaluate the complex impedance of the circuit.
\begin{align*} \amp Z_R = 300\:\Omega,\ \ Z_L = i\omega L = i\:0.754\:\Omega,\\ \amp Z_C = -i/\omega C = -i\:5.3 \times 10^8\:\Omega, \end{align*}
Summing them we get the imegance of the circuit since they are in series.
\begin{align*} Z \amp = Z_R + Z_L + Z_C = \left( 300 -i\:5.3 \times 10^8\right)\:\Omega \\ \amp = \left( 5.3 \times 10^8\:\Omega \right)\angle -90^{\circ}. \end{align*}
Therefore, the current written as \(I_0\cos(\omega t +\phi)\) has
\begin{equation*} I_0 = 318.6\:\textrm{nA},\ \ \phi = \dfrac{\pi}{2}. \end{equation*}
Solution 2. b
Since the impedance of the resistor is real, the voltage across it and the current through it would be in-phase with respect to each other.
Solution 3. c
The \(Z_L\) has a phase constant of \(\pi/2\text{.}\) Therefore, current will be behind the voltage across the inductor in phase by an amount of \(\pi/2\) rad.
Solution 4. d
The \(Z_L\) has a phase constant of \(-\pi/2\text{.}\) Therefore, current will be ahead of the voltage across the capacitor in phase by an amount of \(\pi/2\) rad.
Solution 5. e
Since the phase constant of the impedance is \(\phi \approx \pi/2\text{,}\) the power factor will be \(\cos\phi \approx 0\text{.}\)
Solution 6. f
The real impedance of the circuit refers to the amplitude of the complex impedance, \(|Z|\text{,}\) which is \(5.3 \times 10^8\:\Omega\text{.}\)
Solution 7. g
Average power dissipated is
\begin{equation*} \dfrac{1}{2}\:I_0^2 R = \dfrac{1}{2}\:(318.6\:\textrm{nA})^2 \times 300\:\Omega = 15\:\textrm{pJ}. \end{equation*}
