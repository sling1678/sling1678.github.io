Exercise 1. Heat Transfer Through a Steel Rod.

How much energy is transferred in \(20\) seconds through a cylindrical steel rod of length \(50\) cm and diameter \(4\) cm that connects two heat baths, one at \(580\)K and the other at \(380\)K?
Solution.
This is an example of the use the formula for the rate of conduction. The distance over which the temperature baths are located is \(L=0.5\) m now instead of the width \(w\) of the window pane.
\begin{align*} Q \amp = k \Delta t\frac{|\Delta T|}{L}A \\ \amp = 50\frac{\textrm{J}}{\textrm{s.m.K}} \times 20\ \textrm{s}\frac{200\ \textrm{K}}{0.5\ \textrm{m}}\times \frac{\pi}{4}(0.02\ \textrm{m})^2 = 523\ \textrm{J}. \end{align*}
