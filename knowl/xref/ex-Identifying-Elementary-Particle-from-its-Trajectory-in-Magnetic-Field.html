Example 35.26. Identifying Elementary Particles from Their Trajectories in Magnetic Field.

A particle of charge \(+1\;e\) enters a region of uniform magnetic field of magnitude \(2\text{ T}\) with a velocity of \(9.6 \times 10^5\text{ m/s}\) perpendicular to the magnetic field. It is observed that the particle makes an arc of radius \(5\text{ mm}\) before coming out of the region. Find the mass of the particle.
Answer.
\(1.67\times 10^{-27}\ \text{kg}\text{.}\)
Solution.
The problem states that the particle makes an arc, which is part of a circle. Therefore, we can claim that the particle describes a circular path when in the region of constant magnetic field and use the formula for \(Q/m\) obtained above to write mass in terms of the given quantities.
\begin{equation*} \frac{Q}{m} = \frac{v}{RB_0}\ \ \Longrightarrow \ \ m = \frac{QRB_0}{v} \end{equation*}
Now, we put numbers in to obtain
\begin{align*} m \amp = \frac{1.6\times 10^{-19} \text{C} \times 0.005\ \text{m}\times 2\ \text{T}}{9.6\times 10^{5} \text{m/s}}\\ \amp = 1.67\times 10^{-27}\ \text{kg}. \end{align*}
The particle with charge \(+1\ e\) and mass \(1.67\times 10^{-27}\ \text{kg}\) is proton.
