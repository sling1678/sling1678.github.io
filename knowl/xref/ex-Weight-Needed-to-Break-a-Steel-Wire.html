Exercise 1. Weight Needed to Break a Steel Wire.

How much mass must be hung from a 2-mm diameter steel wire before it breaks?
Data: Breaking Stress of steel = \(400\text{ MPa}\text{.}\)
Hint.
Set Stress to breaking stress and solve for \(M\text{.}\)
Answer.
\(128\text{ kg} \text{.}\)
Solution.
Let \(M \) be the mass that will break the steel wire. Let us set the stress caused by the weight of this mass just before breaking to the breaking stress.
\begin{equation*} \dfrac{Mg}{A} = Y_\text{break}. \end{equation*}
Therefore,
\begin{equation*} M = Y_\text{break} A/g = 400\times 10^{6} \times \pi \times 10^{-6}/9.81 = 128\text{ kg}. \end{equation*}
