Exercise 2. Rolling a Cylinder About Edge.

A cylinder of mass \(M\text{,}\) radius \(R\) and height \(H\) has a tiny hole through the center. When a pin is inserted in the hole, cylinder can rotate about the pin freely as shown in Figure 10.58.
Holding the pin at one end, the cylinder is rolled on a flat surface such that cylinder rolls without sliding with its center moving at speed \(V\text{.}\) Find kinetic energy of the cylinder.
Hint.
No hint.
Answer.
\(\frac{3}{4}MV^2\text{.}\)
Solution.
For the sake of simplification, let us pick a moment shown in Figure 10.59 where the principal axis \(Oz\) is in the direction of fixed axis \(O'x'\text{.}\)
By looking at motion of CM at \(x'=\frac{L}{2},\ y'=0,\ z'=R\) about instantaneous axis or rotation \(O'A\text{,}\) we see that angular velocity pointed towards negative \(z\)-axis with magnitude
\begin{equation*} \omega = V/R. \end{equation*}
Therefore, we have kinetic energy
\begin{align*} K \amp = \frac{1}{2}MV^2 + \frac{1}{2} I_{zz} \omega^2 \\ \amp = \frac{1}{2}MV^2 + \frac{1}{2} \frac{1}{2}MR^2 (V/R)^2 = \frac{3}{4}MV^2. \end{align*}
