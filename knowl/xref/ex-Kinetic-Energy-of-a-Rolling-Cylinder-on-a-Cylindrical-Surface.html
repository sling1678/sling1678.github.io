Exercise 1. Kinetic Energy of a Rolling Cylinder on a Cylindrical Surface.

A cylinder of mass \(M\) and radius \(a\) is rolling without slipping with center moving at speed \(V\) on a cylindrical surface of radius of curvature \(R\) as shown in Figure 10.56. (a) What is its kinetic energy? (b) What is angular momentum about center of the center of the cylindrical surface?
Hint.
For angular velocity, think of rotation about instantaneously at-rest line as axis of rotation.
Answer.
(a) \(\frac{3}{4}MV^2\text{,}\) (b) \(MV\left( R - \frac{a}{2}\right)\) coming out of page.
Solution 1. a
Kinetic energy with respect to the fixed axis will be sum of translational kinetic energy of center of mass and rotation about an axis through the center of mass.
To find angular velocity \(\omega\) in terms of speed of CM \(V\text{,}\) we use instantaneous axis of rotation passing through point P in Figure 10.57.
With velocity \(V\) of the CM about instantaneously at-rest point P at a distance \(a\) from P, angular velocity will be
\begin{equation*} \omega = \frac{V}{a}. \end{equation*}
Therefore, kinetic energy of the cylinder will be
\begin{align*} K \amp = \frac{1}{2}MV^2 + \frac{1}{2} I_0 \omega^2 \\ \amp = \frac{1}{2}MV^2 + \frac{1}{2} \frac{1}{2}Ma^2\omega^2 = \frac{3}{4}MV^2. \end{align*}
Solution 2. b
Both the angular momentum associated with the motion of the CM and the angular momentum about an axis through CM will be coming out of the page in Figure 10.57. Let that axis be \(z\)-axis of body-fixed coordinate system and \(z'\) of the fixed coordinate system. Then, angular momentum about \(O'\) will be
\begin{equation*} \vec L = MV(R-a)\hat k + I_{zz}\omega\hat k. \end{equation*}
With \(I_{zz} = \frac{1}{2}Ma^2\) and \(\omega=V/a\text{,}\) this simlifies to
\begin{equation*} L_z = MV(R-a) + \frac{1}{2}MaV = MV\left( R - \frac{a}{2}\right). \end{equation*}
