Exercise 4. Carnot Cycle with Monatomic Ideal Gas.

Two moles of a monatomic ideal gas performs a Carnot cycle between \(300\text{ K}\) and \(500\text{ K}\text{.}\) The starting volume of the gas before isothermal expansion was \(3\times 10^{-3}\text{ m}^3\text{,}\) and it expands to double the volume in the isothermal step. Find the total amount of work done and heat absorbed from the high temperature reservoir in each cycle.
Hint.
At any point of the cycle, use ideal gas law. For work for processes use \(-pdV\text{.}\) For internal energy use \((f/2)nRT\text{,}\) with \(f=3\) for monatomic gas.
Answer.
\(W = 774\text{ J}\text{,}\) \(Q_{H} = 1935 \text{ J}\text{.}\)
Solution.
First we find the \((p,V)\) at the vertices of the cycle.
\begin{align*} A:\ \ \amp \\ \amp T_A = 500\ \text{K}\\ \amp V_A = 3\times 10^{-3}\ \text{m}^3\\ \amp p_A = 2\times \frac{8.31\times 500}{3\times 10^{-3}} = 2.77\ \text{MPa} \end{align*}
B:
\begin{align*} \amp T_B = 500\ \text{K}\\ \amp V_B = 2\times 3\times 10^{-3}\ \text{m}^3 = 6\times 10^{-3}\ \text{m}^3\\ \amp p_B = \frac{2.77}{2}\ \text{MPa} =1.39 \ \text{MPa} \end{align*}
C:
\begin{align*} \amp T_C = 300\ \text{K}\\ \amp V_C^{\gamma-1} =\frac{T_B}{T_C} V_B^{\gamma-1} \ \ \Longrightarrow\ \ V_C = 12.9\times 10^{-3}\ \text{m}^3\\ \amp p_C = \frac{2 \times 8.31\times 300}{12.9\times 10^{-3}} = 0.386\ \text{MPa} \end{align*}
D:
\begin{align*} \amp T_D = 300\ \text{K}\\ \amp V_D^{\gamma-1} =\frac{T_A}{T_D} V_A^{\gamma-1} \ \ \Longrightarrow\ \ V_D = 6.45\times 10^{-3}\ \text{m}^3\\ \amp p_D V_D = p_C V_C \ \ \Longrightarrow\ \ p_D = 0.773\ \text{MPa} \end{align*}
The calculations for the work by the gas give.
\begin{align*} \amp W_{AB} = n R T_A \ln \frac{V_B}{V_A} = 5760\ \text{J}\\ \amp W_{BC} =\frac{3 n}{2} R (T_B -T_C) = 4986\ \text{J}\\ \amp W_{CD} = n R T_C \ln \frac{V_D}{V_C} = - 3456\ \text{J}\\ \amp W_{DA} =\frac{3 n}{2} R (T_D -T_A) = -4986\ \text{J} \end{align*}
The net work for the entire cycle is obtained by adding the work in each step.
\begin{equation*} W = 2304\ \text{J}. \end{equation*}
The Carnot efficiency of the cycle is given by
\begin{equation*} \eta = 1-\frac{300\ \text{K}}{500\ \text{K}} = 0.4. \end{equation*}
Therefore the cycle abosrbs the following energy from the higher-temperature source.
\begin{equation*} Q_{in} = \frac{W}{\eta} = \frac{2304\ \text{J}}{0.4} = 5760\ \text{J}. \end{equation*}
This is same as the work done in the AB part of the cycle since the change in the internal energy for a constant temperature process of an ideal gas is zero.
