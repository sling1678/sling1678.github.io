Exercise 1. Interference of two Microwave Electromagnetic Waves.

Two coherent microwave sources of wavelength 3 cm are separated by 6 cm. Their electromagnetic waves interfere at a detector 200 cm away. The detector can be moved about in a line parallel to the line joining the two sources. The arrangement is similar to Youngâ€™s double-slit experiment. Find the locations of the detector the constructive interference for order 0, \(\pm 1\) and \(\pm 2\text{.}\)
Answer.
\(y = 0\) ; \(y = 54\, \text{cm}\) and \(y = 115.5\, \text{m}\text{.}\)
Solution.
Let us first find the angles towards which the constructive interferences will occur. We will then use the angle to locate the points on the screen. With \(\lambda = 3\, \text{cm}\) and \(d = 6\, \text{cm}\) we get
\begin{equation*} \sin\theta = \frac{n\lambda}{d} = \frac{n}{2} , \ \ \ (n = 0, \pm1, \pm 2, \dots ) \end{equation*}
This says that \(n \lt 2\) in this case. So, we need to find only \(n=0\text{,}\) and \(n=\pm1\text{.}\) Suppose the origin placed at the center of separation, the \(x\)-axis towards the screen and \(y\)-axis parallel to the screen. Then, to locate the points we will need the tangent of these angles.
\begin{equation*} \tan\theta = \frac{\sin\theta}{\cos\theta} = \frac{\sin\theta}{\sqrt{1-\cos^2\theta}} = \frac{n}{\sqrt{4-n^2}}. \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \tan\theta_{\pm1} =\pm \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}. \end{equation*}
The points on the screen will be
\begin{equation*} y=0,\ \ y = \frac{200}{\sqrt{3}}\ \text{cm}, \text{ and } y = -\frac{200}{\sqrt{3}}\ \text{cm}. \end{equation*}
