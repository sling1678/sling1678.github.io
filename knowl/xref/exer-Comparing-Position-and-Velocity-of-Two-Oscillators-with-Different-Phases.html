Exercise 2. Comparing Position and Velocity of Two Oscillators with Different Phases.

Two oscillators of masses 200 g and 400 g oscillate with the same frequency. They move such that their positions from their corresponding equilibrium points are given by the following two functions (\(t\) in seconds, \(x\) in cm) \(x_1\) and \(x_2\) respectively.
\begin{align*} \amp x_ 1 = 2 \cos(t)\\ \amp x_ 2 = 2 \sin(t) \end{align*}
(a) What are the angular frequencies, amplitudes and phase constants of the two oscillators?
(b) What were their positions and velocities at the initial time \(t=0\text{?}\)
(c) Plot the positions of the two oscillators versus time on the same graph, and interpret which oscillator is ahead, and by how much.
Answer.
(a) ang.freq. = 1 rad/s, ampl. = 2 cm, phase const. = 0 and \(\pi\) radians; (b) \(x_1(0)\) = 2 cm; \(x_2(0)\) = -2 cm; \(v_{1x}(0) = 0 = v_{2x}(0)\text{;}\)
Solution 1. a
Both oscillators have the angular frequency of 1 rad/sec. The frequencies are also same, given by \(1/2\pi = 0.159\) Hz.
For the phase constant, we take one of the oscillators are the reference. Let the phase constant of \(x_1\) be 0. Then, we expressing \(x_2)\) also as cosine will help us compare the two phases.
\begin{equation*} x_2 = \sin(t) = \cos\left( t - \frac{\pi}{2}\right). \end{equation*}
This shows that the phase constant of \(x_2\) is \(-\pi/2\) rad.
Solution 2. b
The positions can be found by just putting in \(t=0\) in the given expressions. To find the velocity, which is the \(x\)-component of the velocity here, we will need to take a derivative before setting the value of \(t\) to zero.
\begin{align*} \amp x_1(0) = 1\\ \amp x_2(0) = 0 \\ \amp v_{1x}(0) = \left.\frac{dx_1}{dt}\right|_{t=0} = -\sin(0) = 0.\\ \amp v_{2x}(0) = \left.\frac{dx_2}{dt}\right|_{t=0} = \cos(0) = 1. \end{align*}
