Exercise 2. Velocity and Acceleration of a Car Moving in a Circular Track from Position.

A car moves on a circular track. A coordinate system is chosen whose origin coincides with the center of the circle and the circle falls in the \(xy\)-plane. The position of the car with time varies according to the following functions of time: \(x(t) = 2 \cos\left(\frac{\pi}{100} t\right)\text{,}\) \(y(t) = 2 \sin\left(\frac{\pi}{100} t\right)\text{,}\) \(z=0\text{.}\) Determine the velocity and acceleration of the car at (i) \(t =0\text{,}\) (ii) \(t = 50\ \textrm{sec}\text{,}\) (iii) \(t = 100\ \textrm{sec}\text{,}\) (iii) \(t=150\ \textrm{sec}\text{,}\) and (iv) \(t=200\ \textrm{sec}\text{.}\)
Solution.
In this solution I will give only the components of the vectors and you can practice in constructing the magnitude and direction. The general formula for the components of the velocity and acceleration obtained by performing the time derivatives are
\begin{align*} \amp v_x = -2\frac{\pi}{100}\sin\left(\frac{\pi}{100}\ t \right);\ v_y = 2\frac{\pi}{100}\cos\left(\frac{\pi}{100}\ t \right);\ v_z = 0\ \ \textrm{[Unit: m/s]};\\ \amp a_x = -2\left(\frac{\pi}{100}\right)^2\cos\left(\frac{\pi}{100}\ t \right) \ \ [\textrm{Unit: m/s}^2]\\ \amp a_y = -2\left(\frac{\pi}{100}\right)^2\sin\left(\frac{\pi}{100}\ t \right) ;\ a_z = 0\ \ [\textrm{Unit: m/s}^2] \end{align*}
The instantaneous values at particular instants are obtained by substituting the value of \(t\text{.}\) For instance, at \(t=0\text{,}\) we find that \(v_x = 0\text{,}\) \(v_y = 2\pi/100\ \textrm{m/s}\text{,}\) \(v_z = 0\text{,}\) \(a_x = -2\left( \pi/100\right)^2\) m/s\(^2\text{,}\) \(a_y = 0\text{,}\) and \(a_z = 0\text{.}\)
