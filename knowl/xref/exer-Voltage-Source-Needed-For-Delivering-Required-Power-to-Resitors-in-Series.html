Exercise 2. Voltage Source Needed For Delivering Required Power to Resitors in Series.

In the voltage divider circuit in FigureÂ 34.34, power \(P\) delivered to the resistor \(R_1\) is given. Find the voltage sources needed in each case. (a) \(R_1 = 8 \Omega\text{,}\) \(R_2 = 800 \Omega\text{,}\) \(P= 60\, \text{W}\text{;}\) (b) \(R_1 = 800 \Omega\text{,}\) \(R_2 = 8 \Omega\text{,}\) \(P= 60\, \text{W}\text{;}\) (c) \(R_1\text{,}\) \(R_2\text{,}\) \(P\) (find the general formula in this case).
Answer.
(a) \(2213\,\text{V}\text{,}\) (b) \(221\,\text{V}\text{.}\)
Solution 1. a
Resistors are in series. Therefore same current \(I\) will flow through the two resistors.
\begin{equation*} I = \dfrac{V}{808\:\Omega}. \end{equation*}
The power used by resistor \(R_1\) will be \(P_1 = I^2R_1\text{,}\) which is 60 W. Therefore, we must have
\begin{equation*} \left[\dfrac{V}{808\:\Omega}\right]^2\times 8\:\Omega = 60\:\textrm{W},\ \ \Longrightarrow\ \ V = 2,213\:\textrm{V}. \end{equation*}
Solution 2. b
Same procedure as in (a) leads to \(V = 221\:\textrm{V}\text{.}\)
Solution 3. c
Same procedure as in (a) leads to
\begin{equation*} V = (R_1 + R_2)\sqrt{\dfrac{P}{R_1}}. \end{equation*}
