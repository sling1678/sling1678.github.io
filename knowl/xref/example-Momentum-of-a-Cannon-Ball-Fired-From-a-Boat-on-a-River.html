Example 7.3. Momentum of a Cannon Ball Fired From a Boat on a River.

A cannon ball of mass \(20\text{ kg}\) is fired from a boat on a river at a speed of \(40\text{ m/s}\) with respect to the boat at an angle \(30^\circ\) from the ground towards the East direction. The boat itself is going at a speed of \(10\text{ m/s}\) with respect to the shore towards North on the river. (a) What is the momentum of the cannon ball with respect to the boat? (b) What is the momentum of the cannon ball with respect to the shore?
Answer.
(a) \(800\text{ kg.m/s}\) at \(30^\circ\) above horizon towards East. (b) \(825\text{ kg.m/s}\) at angles to be figured out by the student.
Solution 1. a
The velocity with respect to the boat in components is
\begin{equation*} \vec v = 40\,\cos\,30^\circ \hat i + 40\,\sin\,30^\circ \hat k. \end{equation*}
Therefore, momentum of the ball with respect to the boat is (units \(\text{kg.m/s}\))
\begin{equation*} m\vec v = 800\,\cos\,30^\circ \hat i + 800\,\sin\,30^\circ \hat k. \end{equation*}
The magnitude is \(800\text{ kg.m/s}\text{,}\) and direction same as direction of this velocity.
Solution 2. b
In FigureÂ 7.4 we have \(y\)-axis to the North. Therefore, the velocity of the boat with respect to the shore is
\begin{equation*} \vec V = 10\,\hat j. \end{equation*}
Now, we add this to the velocity of the ball with respect to the boat to get the velocity of the ball with respet to the shore. Let us denote this velocity with \(\vec w\text{.}\)
\begin{equation*} \vec w = \vec V + \vec v = 40\,\cos\,30^\circ \hat i + 10\,\hat j + 40\,\sin\,30^\circ \hat k. \end{equation*}
From this we get momentum of the cannon ball with respect to the shore to be
\begin{equation*} m \vec w = 800\,\cos\,30^\circ \hat i + 200\,\hat j + 800\,\sin\,30^\circ \hat k. \end{equation*}
You can get the magnitude by
\begin{equation*} \sqrt{800^2\cos^2\,30^\circ + 200^2 + 800^2\sin^2\,30^\circ} = 825\text{ kg.m/s}. \end{equation*}
For direction, you can figure out the spherical coordinate angle. That is left as an exercise.
