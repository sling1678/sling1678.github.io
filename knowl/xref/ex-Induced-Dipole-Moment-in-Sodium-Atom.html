Exercise 2. Induced Dipole Moment in Sodium Atom.

Evaluate the magnitude of the dipole moment of a sodium atom induced when the atom is between two large parallel aluminum plates separated by \(3\text{ mm}\) where a potential difference of \(100\text{ V}\) is maintained.
Data: \(\alpha_\text{Na} = 27\times 10^{-40}\:\text{C.m}^2/\text{V}\text{.}\)
Hint.
Use \(E=\phi/d\) to get the field.
Answer.
\(9\times 10^{-34}\:\text{C.m}\text{.}\)
Solution.
The magnitude of the electric field between the plates will be
\begin{equation*} E = \dfrac{V}{d} = \frac{100\:\text{V}}{0.003\:\text{m}} = \dfrac{1}{3}\times 10^{5}\:\text{V/m}. \end{equation*}
Therefore, the dipole moment of sodium atom will be
\begin{align*} p \amp = \alpha E\\ \amp = 27\times 10^{-40}\:\text{C.m}^2/\text{V} \times \dfrac{1}{3}\times 10^{5}\:\text{V/m}\\ \amp = 9\times 10^{-34}\:\text{C.m}. \end{align*}
