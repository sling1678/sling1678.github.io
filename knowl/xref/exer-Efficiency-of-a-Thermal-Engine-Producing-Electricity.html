Exercise 2. Efficiency of a Thermal Engine Producing Electricity.

A thermal engine produces \(4\) MJ of electrical energy while operating between two thermal baths of different temperatures. The working substance of the engine rejects \(5\) MJ of heat to the cold temperature bath. What is the efficiency of the engine?
Answer.
\(44.4\%\text{.}\)
Solution.
Given
\begin{equation*} W_{by} = 4\ \textrm{MJ}\ \ \textrm{and}\ \ Q_{out} = 5\ \textrm{MJ}. \end{equation*}
This gives
\begin{equation*} \ Q_{in} = W_{by} + Q_{out} = 9\ \textrm{MJ}. \end{equation*}
Therefore
\begin{equation*} \textrm{Efficiency} = \frac{W_{by}}{Q_{in}} = \frac{4\ \textrm{MJ}}{9\ \textrm{MJ}} = 0.444\ \ \textrm{or}\ \ 44.4\%. \end{equation*}
