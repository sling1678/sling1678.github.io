Exercise 2. Electric Field of a Uniformly Charged Rod Surrounded by an Oppositely Charged Cylindrical Shell.

A long rod of radius \(R_1\) is uniformly charged with volume charge density \(\rho_0\text{.}\) Surrounding the rod is a shell of radius \(R_2\) that is also charged uniformly, but of the opposite type and has a surface charge density \(-\sigma_0\text{.}\) The two charge densities are such that for any length the rod and the shell are balanced in total charges. Find electric field in (a) \(s \le R_1\text{,}\) (b) \(R_1 \lt s \lt R_2\text{,}\) (c) \(s \gt R_2\text{.}\)
Hint.
Apply cylindrical symmetry.
Answer.
(a) \(\frac{\rho }{2\epsilon_0}\ s\text{,}\) (b) \(\frac{\lambda_0}{2\pi \epsilon_0}\ \frac{1}{s}\) with \(\lambda_0 = \rho_0 \pi R_1^2\text{,}\) (c) \(0\text{.}\)
Solution 1. a
Assuming the rod and shell are long enough that we can assume cylindrical symmetry, we can use immediately use th results of Gauss’s law in this section. For (a), only the charge in the inner cylinder matters and the case is that of an inside point. This gives
\begin{equation*} 2\pi s L E_a = \frac{\rho \pi s^2 L}{\epsilon_0}. \end{equation*}
Therefore,
\begin{equation*} E_a = \frac{\rho }{2\epsilon_0}\ s. \end{equation*}
Solution 2. b
This point is an outside point of the inner cylinder, but inside a shell. Hence, only inside cylinder matters. This gives
\begin{equation*} E_b = \frac{\lambda_0}{2\pi \epsilon_0}\ \frac{1}{s}, \end{equation*}
where \(\lambda_0 = \rho_0 \pi R_1^2\text{.}\)
Solution 3. c
Here, Gauss’s equation for a Gaussian surrounding both cylinder and shell will give
\begin{equation*} 2\pi s L E_c = 0. \end{equation*}
Therefore, \(E_c=0\text{.}\)
