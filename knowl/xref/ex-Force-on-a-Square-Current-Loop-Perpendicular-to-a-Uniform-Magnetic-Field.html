Exercise 3. Force on a Square Current Loop Perpendicular to a Uniform Magnetic Field.

Current flows in a loop shaped as a square of side \(a\text{.}\) The loop of wire is in a plane perpendicular to a magnetic field of magnitude \(B\) as shown in the figure. What is the magnetic force on the full loop?
Hint.
Look at the direction of forces on the four straight segments.
Answer.
\(0\text{.}\)
Solution.
Based on the given orientation of current and magnetic field, the forces on the four straight segments have equal magnitude, \(F_i = I a B\text{,}\) \(i = 1,\ 2\, 3\, 4\text{.}\) But their directions are such that \(\vec F_1\) cancels \(\vec F_3\) and \(\vec F_2\) cancels \(\vec F_4\text{.}\) Therefore, net force on the loop is zero.
