Example 38.45. (Calculus) Induced Electric Field Outside from Varying Current in a Solenoid.

The current in a long solenoid of radius \(3\, \text{cm}\) and \(25\, \text{turns/cm}\) is varied with time at a rate of \(2\, \text{A/s}\text{.}\) Find the electric field at a distance \(4\,\text{cm}\) from the center of the solenoid.
Answer.
\(3.33\times 10^{5} \ \textrm{N/C}\text{.}\)
Solution.
From Faraday law, we have
\begin{equation*} \oint\:\vec E\cdot d\vec l = \mathcal{E} = \dfrac{d\Phi}{dt}. \end{equation*}
Here \(\dfrac{d\Phi}{dt}\) has a cylindrical symmetry. Hence, we can use “Ampere’s Law” type of arguments to assert that the electric field will circulate around the solenoid. Hence the electric field at a distance \(r\) from the axis of the solenoid will obey
\begin{equation*} 2\pi r\times E = \mathcal{E}\ \ \Longrightarrow\ \ E = \dfrac{\mathcal{E}}{2\pi r}. \end{equation*}
Putting in the expression for \(\Phi\) for a solenoid n we find
\begin{align*} E \amp = \dfrac{\mu_0\:n\:\Delta I/\Delta t}{2\pi r} \\ \amp = \dfrac{4\pi\times 10^{-7}\times 2500\times 2}{2\pi \times 0.03} = 3.33\times 10^5\:\textrm{N/C}. \end{align*}
