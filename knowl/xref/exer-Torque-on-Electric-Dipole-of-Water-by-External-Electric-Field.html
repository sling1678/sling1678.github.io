Exercise 1. Torque on Electric Dipole of Water by External Electric Field.

The dipole moment of a water molecule between two large copper plates separated by a distance of \(5\, \text{mm}\) in the north-south direction is pointed due east. Find the torque on the molecule, when a voltage of \(150\, \text{V}\) is applied across the plates so that electric field is pointed due north. (Use only the permanent dipole moment of water molecule.)
Answer.
\(1.8\times 10^{-25}\, \text{N.m}\text{.}\)
Solution.
The angle between the dipole moment vector and the electric field is \(90^{\circ}|\text{.}\) Therefore, the magnitude of torque on the water molecule will be
\begin{align*} \tau \amp = p\:E\: \sin\theta = 6.1\times 10^{-30}\:\textrm{C.m}\times 150\:\dfrac{\textrm{V}}{\textrm{m}}\:\sin 90^{\circ}\\ \amp = 1.83\times 10^{-25}\:\textrm{N.m} \end{align*}
