Exercise 1. Low Temperature Specific Heat Varying with Temperature.

At very low temperatures the molar specific heats of metals vary as \(T^3\text{.}\)
\begin{equation*} \frac{C_P}{3R} = 77.9\left(\frac{T}{\Theta} \right)^3, \end{equation*}
where \(\Theta\) is called the Debye temperature. Here \(R\) is the universal gas constant and \(C_P\) is the molar specific heat for constant pressure. The Debye temperature of aluminum is \(420\)K. How much heat will be involved when the temperature of a \(3\)-kg aluminum block is raised from \(4\)K to \(20\)K?
Answer.
\(0.116\) J.
Solution.
Since \(C_P\) depends on the temperature \(T\) we integrate to obtain the heat exchanged. The calculation uses the value of \(R\) which is simpler in moles. Therefore, we will use moles for the quantity. There \(27\) g in each mole of Aluminum. Therefore the number of moles \(n\) in the given sample is
\begin{equation*} n = \frac{3000\ \textrm{g}}{27\ \textrm{g/mol}} = 111.11\ \textrm{mol}. \end{equation*}
\begin{align*} \Delta Q \amp = \int n C_P dT = 3\times (77.9/420^3)\times111.11 \times 8.31\int_{4}^{20} T^3 dT\\ \amp = 116\ \textrm{J}. \end{align*}
