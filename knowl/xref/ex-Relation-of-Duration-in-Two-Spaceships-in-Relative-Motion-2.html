Checkpoint 49.33. Relation of Duration and Distance in Two Spaceships in Relative Motion.

Same two observers as in Exercise CheckpointÂ 49.23. But, now we look at two events occurring in spaceship A. A photon arrives at the origin of A at its time \(t=0\) and another photon arrives at \((x =1\: \textrm{m}, 0,0)\) at \(t=0\) in the frame of ship A. (a) Find the coordinates and times of the two events as observed by frame B. (b) In which frame, the two events are simultaneous and in which frame they are not simultaneous?
Hint.
Answer.
(a) \(1.92 \times 10^{-9}\text{ sec}\text{,}\) \(1.155\text{ m}\text{,}\) (b) Simultaneous in A.
Solution. (a)
(a) Now, the events are in frame A, which is moving towards positive \(x\) axis when observed from frame B. The event of photon arriving at origin at \(t_A=0\) will be also origin of B at \(t_B=0\) since the two frames were set that way. The other event of photon at \(x_A = 1\text{ m}\) at \(t_A=0\) will have \(t_B\) and \(x_B\) as follows.
\begin{align*} t_B \amp = \gamma\left( t_A + x_A V/c^2\right)\\ \amp = \sqrt{2}{\sqrt{3}}\times \frac{1}{2\times 3 \times 10^{8}} = 1.92 \times 10^{-9}\text{ sec}. \end{align*}
\begin{align*} x_B \amp = \gamma\left( x_A + V t_A\right) = \sqrt{2}{\sqrt{3}}\times 1 = 1.155\text{ m}. \end{align*}
