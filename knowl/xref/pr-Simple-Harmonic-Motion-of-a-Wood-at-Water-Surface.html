Exercise 39. (Calculus) Simple Harmonic Motion of a Wood at Water Surface.
A piece of wood of uniform cross-sectional area floats over water with a height \(h_0\) submerged below water level. It is pushed sligltly into the water, and let go from rest. Show that the subsequent motion of the block is a simple harmonic oscillation, and find the frequency of oscillations.
Hint.
Follow the motion of the center of mass of the wooden piece.
Answer.
\(\frac{1}{2\pi}\;\sqrt{ \frac{g }{h_0} }\text{.}\)
Solution.
For simplicity consider a rectangular shaped box as shown in Figure 17.45.
We need to analyze two situations, one at the equilibrum Fig. (a) and another at an arbitrary instant during the motion of the block, Fig (b). As shown in the figure, with positive \(y\) axis pointed down we get the following equaiton from the quilibrium situation.
\begin{equation} mg - A h_0 \rho_w g = 0,\tag{17.27} \end{equation}
which gives \(m=A h_0 \rho_w\text{.}\) From the situation at arbitrary instant, we get
\begin{equation} mg - A (h_0+y) \rho_w g = m a_y = m \frac{d^2y}{dt^2}.\tag{17.28} \end{equation}
From these equations, we can get
\begin{equation} \frac{d^2y}{dt^2} = -\frac{A y\rho_w g }{m}y.\tag{17.29} \end{equation}
Using expresson of \(m\) here gives
\begin{equation} \frac{d^2y}{dt^2} = -\frac{g }{h_0}y.\tag{17.30} \end{equation}
This is an equation of a simple harmonic motion with angular frequency, \(\omega\) given by
\begin{equation*} \omega = \sqrt{ \frac{g }{h_0} }. \end{equation*}
Frequency will be this divided by \(2\pi\text{.}\)
\begin{equation*} f = \frac{1}{2\pi}\;\sqrt{ \frac{g }{h_0} }. \end{equation*}
