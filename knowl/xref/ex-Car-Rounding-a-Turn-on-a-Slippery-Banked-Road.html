Example 6.115. Car Rounding a Turn on a Slippery Banked Road.

Figure 6.116 shows a car rounding a bend in the road of radius of curvature \(R=200\text{ m}\) that has a banking angle of \(15^\circ\text{.}\) Ordinarily, the coefficient of static friction between tires and road is \(\mu_s = 0.3\) but on a rainy night the road became slick and coefficient of friction dropped to \(\mu_s = 0.05\text{.}\)
(a) What is the safest speed the car can round the bend without sliding? (b) If car’s mass is \(3,000\text{ kg}\text{,}\) what is the magnitude of normal force on the car?
Answer.
\(N = 28,332\text{ N},\ \ v = 24.1\text{ m/s}\text{.}\)
Solution.
Notice the following aspects of this problem. (1) Normal is not straight up, but perpendicular to the surface of the road. (2) Both normal force and static friction force provide the force for centripetal acceleration.
Figure 6.117 shows free-body diagram of three forces on the car, its weight, normal which is perpendicular to the road surface, and static friction that is pointed down the road.
There are also rolling friction force in the forward direction and air drag which are not shown since we are only interested in the forces that would give us the conditions associated with the circular motion.
We will assume uniform circular motion at unknown speed \(v\text{.}\) Since we are looking for maximum speed, we will be using maximum static friction, i.e., \(F_s = F_s^\text{max} = \mu_s N\text{.}\) Applying \(\vec F=m\vec a\) along \(x\) and \(y\) axes gives us
\begin{align*} \amp N\sin\theta + \mu_s N \cos\theta = m \frac{v^2}{R}. \\ \amp N\cos\theta - \mu_s N \sin\theta - m g = 0. \end{align*}
Solving these for \(N\text{,}\) we get
\begin{equation*} N = \frac{mg}{\cos\theta - \mu_s \sin\theta}. \end{equation*}
For \(v\) we get
\begin{equation*} v = \sqrt{\frac{gR( \sin\theta + \mu_s \cos\theta )}{\cos\theta - \mu_s \sin\theta}}. \end{equation*}
Since we are examining the slick road condition, we will use \(\mu_s = 0.05\text{.}\) With given values of \(\theta\) and \(R\) and using \(g=9.81\text{ m/s}^2\text{,}\) I got the following values.
\begin{equation*} N = 28,332\text{ N},\ \ v = 24.1\text{ m/s}. \end{equation*}
