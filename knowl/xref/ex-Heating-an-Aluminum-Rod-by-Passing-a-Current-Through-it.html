Exercise 3. Heating an Aluminum Rod by Passing a Current Through it.

An aluminum rod of length \(25 \text{ cm}\) and diameter \(2 \text{ m}\) is insulated. When a current is passed through the rod, its temperature rises at the rate of \(20 \) degrees Celsius per minute. Find the current.
Data: Density of aluminum \(2.7 \text{ g/cc}\text{,}\) specific heat aluminum \(900 \text{ J/kg.K}\text{.}\)
Hint.
Use conservation of energy, \(m\:c_p\:\frac{\Delta T}{\Delta t} = P = I^2 R\text{.}\)
Answer.
\(17.2 \text{ A}\text{.}\)
Solution.
This problem is similar to the one solved above. The governing equation being the balancing of energy per unit time.
\begin{equation*} m\:c_p\:\frac{\Delta T}{\Delta t} = P = I^2 R. \end{equation*}
This gives
\begin{equation*} I = \sqrt{\frac{m\:c_p}{R}\:\frac{\Delta T}{\Delta t}}. \end{equation*}
Putting the expression for R in terms of resistivity, and mass by the product of volume \((AL)\) and density \((D)\text{.}\)
\begin{equation*} I = \sqrt{\frac{ALD\:c_pA}{\rho L}\:\frac{\Delta T}{\Delta t}} = \sqrt{\frac{A^2D\:c_p}{\rho}\:\frac{\Delta T}{\Delta t}}.\ \ \ \ (1) \end{equation*}
The numerical values given in the problem are
\begin{align*} A \amp = \pi\times (0.001\:\text{m})^2 \\ \amp = 3.14\times 10^{-6}\;\text{m}^2,\ \ D = 2700\:\text{kg/m}^3,\\ c_p \amp = 900\:\text{J/kg.K},\ \rho \\ \amp = 2.7\times 10^{-8}\:\Omega.\text{m},\ \ \frac{\Delta T}{\Delta t} = \frac{1}{3}\:\text{deg/s}. \end{align*}
Putting these numerical values in (1) gives the current, \(I = 17.2\) Amps.
