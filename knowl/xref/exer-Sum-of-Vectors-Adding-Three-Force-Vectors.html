Exercise 5. Adding Three Force Vectors.

Three force vectors fall in the \(yz\)-plane of a coordinate system and have the following representations: \(\vec V_1 = (100\ \textrm{N})\hat u_y + (200\ \textrm{N})\hat u_z\text{,}\) \(\vec V_2 = (-500\ \textrm{N})\hat u_y + (100\ \textrm{N})\hat u_z\text{,}\) and \(\vec V_3 = (-400\ \textrm{N})\hat u_y+ (500\ \textrm{N})\hat u_z\text{.}\) (a) Find the \(y\) and \(z\)-components of the sum of the three vectors. (b) Determine the magnitude and direction of the sum.
Solution 1. a
Let us denote the sum vector here by the symbol \(\vec F\text{.}\) The \(y\)- and \(z\)-components of the sum will be equal to the sum of the \(y\)- and \(z\)-components of the other vectors.
\begin{align*} \amp F_y = (100\ \textrm{N}) + (-500\ \textrm{N}) + (-400\ \textrm{N}) = (-800\ \textrm{N})\\ \amp F_z = (200\ \textrm{N}) + (100\ \textrm{N}) + (500\ \textrm{N}) =(800\ \textrm{N}). \end{align*}
Solution 2. b
The magnitude of the sum vector = \(\sqrt{F_y^2 + F_z^2} = 800\sqrt{2}\ \textrm{N}\text{.}\)
Here we can choose to describe the direction by the angle a vector makes with the positive \(y\) axis in a counterclockwise sense as we look at the \(yz\) plane from positive \(x\) axis direction. The direction will be in the second quadrant of the \(yz\)-plane. We can denote the direction here by the angle \(\theta\) that the vector \(\vec F\) makes with respect to the positive \(y\)-axis.
\begin{equation*} \theta = \tan^{-1}\left( \frac{F_z}{F_y}\right) + 180^{\circ}= 135^{\circ}. \end{equation*}
Note that I have added \(180^{\circ}\) to convert the angle I obtained from the arctangent so that the angle refers to the angle with respect to the positive \(y\)-axis.
