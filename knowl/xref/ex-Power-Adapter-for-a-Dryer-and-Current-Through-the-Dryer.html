Exercise 1. Power Adapter for a Dryer and Current Through the Dryer.

A hairdryer is rated to work in a \(220\text{-V}\) AC line (i.e. \(V_\text{rms} = 220\text{ V}\)). It operates at \(1000\text{ W}\) power. To connect the hair dryer to \(120\text{-V}\) AC line, it has to be connected through a step-up transformer that will convert the \(120\text{-V}\) line to yield a \(220\text{-V}\) output to which the hair-dryer will be connected.
(a) What must be the turn ratio between the primary and the secondary? Which one must have higher turns?
(b) Determine the current that the hair dryer will draw from the \(120\text{-V}\) line.
Hint.
(a) Definition, (b) \(I_1V_1= I_2V_2\text{.}\)
Answer.
(a) \(2:1\text{,}\) (b) \(8.33\:\text{A}\text{.}\)
Solution 1. (a)
Since we need to to step-up the voltage we will need \(N_s:N_p = 2:1\text{.}\)
Solution 2. (b)
\(I_p\times V_p = 1000\:\text{W}\) with \(V_p = 120\:\text{V}\text{.}\) Therefore, \(I_p = 1000\:\text{W}/120\:\text{V} = 8.33\:\text{A}\text{.}\)
