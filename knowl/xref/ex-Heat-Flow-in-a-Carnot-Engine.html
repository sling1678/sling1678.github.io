Example 25.5. Heat Flow in a Carnot Engine.

A Carnot engine operating in a Carnot cycle between \(400^{\circ}\text{C}\) and an unknown lower temperature has an efficiency of \(0.3\text{.}\) How much heat must be rejected to the cold bath for every kJ of heat absorbed from the hot bath.
Answer.
\(0.7\,\text{kJ}\text{.}\)
Solution.
The efficiency of the Carnot engine can be expressed in terms of \(Q_C\) and \(Q_H\) alone.
\begin{equation*} \eta_C = \frac{Q_H-Q_C}{Q_H}\ \ \Longrightarrow\ \ Q_C = (1-\eta_C) Q_H. \end{equation*}
Therefore, if \(Q_H = 1\text{ kJ}\text{,}\) then
\begin{equation*} Q_C = (1-0.3) \times 1\ \text{kJ} = 0.7\ \text{kJ}. \end{equation*}
