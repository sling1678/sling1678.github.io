Exercise 6. Reflectivity of Mirrors of Fabry-Perot Cavity Required to Separate Mercury Yellow-Orange Doublet.

What should be the reflectivity of the Fabry-Perot etalon so that the yellow-orange doublet of mercury, which correspond to wavelengths \(576.959\text{ nm}\) and \(579.065\text{ nm}\text{,}\) are just resolvable by Raleight criterion in \(m=100\) order?
Hint.
Set the difference in wavelengths to equal half width of peaks.
Answer.
\(0.336\text{.}\)
Solution.
The resolving of two wavelengths will occur if the peaks of constructive interference corresponding to the two wavelengths fall outside of half-width of the peaks. This give the condition of resolution in the \(m^\text{th}\) order to be
\begin{equation*} \dfrac{\Delta \lambda}{\lambda_{\textrm{ave}}} = \dfrac{1-R}{m\pi\sqrt{R}}, \end{equation*}
where I used \(2 n d \cos\theta = m \lambda\) for the \(m^\text{th}\) order. For the given lines we need the following \(R\) in \(m=100\text{.}\)
\begin{equation*} \dfrac{2.106}{578.012} = 0.00364352 = \dfrac{1-R}{100\pi\sqrt{R}}. \end{equation*}
Squaring both sides and rearranging we get
\begin{equation*} R^2 - 3.31 R + 1 = 0,\ \ \Longrightarrow\ \ R = 0.336. \end{equation*}
