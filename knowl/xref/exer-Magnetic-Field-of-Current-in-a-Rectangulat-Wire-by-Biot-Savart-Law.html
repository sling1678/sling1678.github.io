Exercise 6. Magnetic Field of Current in a Rectangulat Wire by Biot Savart Law.

An electric circuit contains a rectangular wire of dimensions \(a \times b\) carrying a current \(I\) as in FigureÂ 36.28. Find magnetic field at an arbitrary point P by the current in the rectangular wire in the area enclosed.
Solution.
Note that the magnetic field from all four straight segments are in the negative z-axis. So, they will all add together. The magnitude from each segment is given by an adaptation of the formula in exercise above. Let \((x,y)\) be the coordinates of the field point P. Then, using the answer from the last exercise we get
\begin{align*} \vec B_P = \amp - \hat u_z\: \dfrac{2\mu_0\: I}{4\pi}\:\left[ \dfrac{b-y}{s\sqrt{s^2 + (b-y)^2}} + \dfrac{y}{s\sqrt{s^2 + y^2}} \right. \\ \amp \ \ \ \ \ + \left. \dfrac{a-x}{s\sqrt{s^2 + (a-x)^2}} + \dfrac{x}{s\sqrt{s^2 + x^2}} \right]. \end{align*}
