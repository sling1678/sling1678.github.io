Exercise 3. Torque on a Magnetic Dipole and Energy Required to Flip its Orientation.

A magnetic dipole of magnitude \(9.27 \times 10^{-24}\text{ A.m}^2\) is placed in a magnetic field of magnitude \(3\text{ T}\text{.}\) The dipole makes an angle of \(30^{\circ}\) with the magnetic field. Determine (a) the torque on the dipole and (b) the energy to flip the direction of the dipole by \(180^{\circ}\text{.}\)
Hint.
Use definitions.
Answer.
(a) \(1.39\times 10^{-23}\:\text{N.m} \text{,}\) (b) \(4.82\times 10^{-23}\:\text{J}\text{.}\)
Solution 1. (a)
(a) The magnitude of the torque on a magnetic dipole moment \(\vec \mu\) in a magnetic field \(\vec B\) is given by
\begin{align*} \tau \amp = |\vec \mu\times \vec B| = \mu\:B\:\sin\:\theta \\ \amp = 9.27 \times 10^{-24}\: \text{A.m}^2\times 3\: \text{T}\times \sin\:30^{\circ}\\ \amp = 1.39\times 10^{-23}\:\text{N.m}. \end{align*}
Solution 2. (b)
(b) The energy to flip a magnetic dipole moment is given by
\begin{align*} \Delta U \amp = U_f - U_i \\ \amp = -\mu\:B\:\cos\:(30^{\circ}+180^{\circ}) - [-\mu\:B\:\cos\:30^{\circ}]= 2\mu\: B\:\cos\:30^{\circ}\\ \amp = 2\times 9.27 \times 10^{-24}\: \text{A.m}^2\times3\: \text{T}\cos\:30^{\circ} = 4.82\times 10^{-23}\:\text{J}. \end{align*}
