Example 22.2. (Calculus) Low Temperature Study of Aluminum.

The specific heat of aluminum at constant pressure is found to vary with temperature as \(c_P(T) = \left(7.0\times 10^{-4} \text{J/g.K}^4 \right)T^3 \text{,}\) where \(T\) is the temperature expressed in the kelvin scale. How much heat is needed to raise the temperature of 50 grams of aluminum from \(5\text{K}\) to \(9\text{K}\text{?}\)
Answer.
\(52\ \text{J}\text{.}\)
Solution.
Note that the specific heat is temperature dependent. Therefore, we would have to integrate the specific heat to find heat required.
\begin{align*} Q \amp = m\int_{T_1}^{T_2} c_P(T) dT \\ \amp = 50\text{g}\times \left(7.0\times 10^{-4} \text{J/g.K}^4 \right)\int_{T_1}^{T_2} T^3 dT\\ \amp = \frac{3.5\times 10^{-2}}{4}(\text{J/K}^4 )\left(T_2^4 - T_1^4 \right) = 52\ \text{J}. \end{align*}
