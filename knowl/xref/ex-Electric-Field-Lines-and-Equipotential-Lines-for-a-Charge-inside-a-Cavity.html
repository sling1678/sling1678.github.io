Example 32.18. Electric Field Lines and Equipotential Lines for a Charge inside a Cavity.

A negative charge \(-q\) is fixed inside the cavity of a neutral spherical conductor as shown in Figure 32.19. (a) Show all induced charge distributions schematically, and draw electric field lines. (b) Draw equipotential lines on the same diagram using either a different color ink or as dashed lines.
Answer.
See solution.
Solution.
Answers for both (a) and (b) are shown in Figure 32.20. We use the rules given in the hint. Induced charges on the external surface are uniformly spread while the distribution at the inside surface of the cavity is not uniform. There is higher charge density at the cavity surface closer to the negative point charge located inside the cavity.
