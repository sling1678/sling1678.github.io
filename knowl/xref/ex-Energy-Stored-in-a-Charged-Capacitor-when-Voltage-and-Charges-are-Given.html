Example 32.36. Energy Stored in a Charged Capacitor when Voltage and Charges are Given.

A capacitor once charged to \(100\text{ V}\) stores \(\pm 10\ \mu\text{C}\) of charges on its plates. How much electrostatic energy is stored?
Answer.
\(500\ \mu\text{J}\text{.}\)
Solution.
The electrostatic energy stored in a charged capacitor is
\begin{align*} U \amp = \frac{1}{2}\:Q\:\Delta\phi\\ \amp = \frac{1}{2}\times 10\: \mu\text{C} \times 100\: \text{V} = 500\: \mu\text{J}. \end{align*}
