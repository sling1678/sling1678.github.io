Exercise 3. Heat Required by a Carnot Engine to Power a Motor.

An engine working in a Carnot cycle between two heat baths of temperatures \(600\)K and \(273\)K runs an electric motor rated at \(60\) W of power (assume running the motor costs \(60\) W). If each cycle is completed in \(10\) seconds, how much heat does the engine absorb from the higher-temperature bath in each cycle?
Answer.
\(1101\ \textrm{J}\text{.}\)
Solution.
The efficiency
\begin{equation*} \eta = \frac{T_H - T_C}{T_H} = \frac{327}{600} = 0.545. \end{equation*}
The work output in each cycle is
\begin{equation*} W = P\Delta t = 60\ \textrm{W}\times 10\ \textrm{s} = 600\ \textrm{J}. \end{equation*}
Therefore
\begin{equation*} Q_{in} = \frac{W}{\eta} = \frac{600\ \textrm{J}}{0.545} = 1101\ \textrm{J}. \end{equation*}
