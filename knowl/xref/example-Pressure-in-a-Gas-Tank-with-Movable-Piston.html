Example 17.7. Pressure in a Gas Tank with Movable Piston.

A cylindrical gas tank has an air-tight piston of area \(3\,\text{cm}^2\) area that can slide freely. There is a \(50\,\text{kg}\) block sitting on the piston to balance the pressure in the gas. The mass of the piston is \(10\,\text{kg}\text{.}\) What is the pressure in the gas?
Solution.
Let \(p\) be the pressure inside the tank. Then, force on the piston by the gas pointing out from the tank will be \(pA\) while the force pointing in towards the gas will come from atmospher and the weight of the block sitting on the piston and te weight of the pistin itself. This will give us the following balances of forces equation.
\begin{equation*} p\,A = p_0\,A + m_\text{piston}\, g + M_\text{block}\,g. \end{equation*}
Therefore, (covert the are to \(m^2\) by multiplying with \(10^{-4}\text{.}\))
\begin{align*} p \amp = p_0 + \frac{(m_p + m_b)\,g}{A}\\ \amp = 1.0\times 10^{5} + \frac{60\times 10}{3\times 10^{-4}} = 2.1\times 10^{6} \text{Pa}. \end{align*}
That is approx. \(21\,\text{atm}\text{.}\)
