Example 9.15. Angular Velocity from Angle versus Time Plot - Linear Segments.

Figure 9.16 shows a plot of angle rotated versus time. Find angular velocity at (a) \(10\text{ sec}\text{,}\) (b) \(15\text{ sec}\text{,}\) (c) \(30\text{ sec}\text{,}\) and (d) \(50\text{ sec}\text{.}\)
Answer.
(a) and (b) \(15\text{ rad/sec}\text{,}\) (c) and (d) \(-8\text{ rad/sec}\text{,}\)
Solution 1. (a) and (b)
(a) The slope of the line through the point on the graph corresponding to the instant \(t = 10\text{ sec} \) gives the angular velocity.
\begin{equation*} \omega = \dfrac{400-100}{20-0} = 15\text{ rad/sec}. \end{equation*}
This is counterclockwise rotation.
(b) The slope here is same as (a) since it is the same line.
Solution 2. (c) and (d)
(c) The slope of the line through the point on the graph corresponding to the instant \(t = 30\text{ sec} \) gives the angular velocity.
\begin{equation*} \omega = \dfrac{400-0}{20-70} = -8\text{ rad/sec}. \end{equation*}
This part is clockwise rotation.
(c) The slope here is same as (c) since it is the same line.
