Exercise 1. Wavelength from Direction of First-order Peak in a Diffraction Grating.

A light beam of an unknown wavelength is incident over \(5\, \text{mm}\) of a diffraction grating that has \(400\ \text{lines per mm}\text{,}\) and the first-order peak is observed at an angle of \(20^{\circ}\text{.}\) Find the wavelength in \(\mu\text{m}\text{.}\)
Hint.
Use the principal peaks condition for \(m=1\text{.}\)
Answer.
\(0.855\:\mu\textrm{m}\text{.}\)
Solution.
From the data number of grating lines upon which the light is incident, \(N = 2000\text{,}\) and separation of lines, \(a = 2.5\:\mu\textrm{m}\text{.}\) From the codition of \(m=1\) peak,
\begin{equation*} \lambda = a\:\sin\:\theta = 0.855\:\mu\textrm{m}. \end{equation*}
