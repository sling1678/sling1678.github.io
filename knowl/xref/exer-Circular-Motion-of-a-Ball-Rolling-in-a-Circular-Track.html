Exercise 5. Motion of a Ball Rolling in a Circular Track.

A ball is rolling in a circular track of radius \(R\) which is in the \(xy\)-plane but the center of the track is not at the origin of the Cartesian coordinate system in use. Instead, the center of the track is at \((x_0,y_0)\text{.}\) The ball has a constant speed of \(v_s\text{.}\) (a) Give an expression of the position vector as a function of time \(\vec r(t)\) in terms of the unit vectors \(\{\hat u_x, \hat u_y\}\) of the Cartesian coordinates. (b) Use your expression for \(\vec r(t)\) to find an expression for the velocity of the particle at an arbitrary time \(t\text{.}\) (c) Use your expression for \(\vec r(t)\) to find an expression of the acceleration of the particle at an arbitrary instant \(t\text{.}\)
Solution 1. a
The circle of motion and the coordinates are shown in the figure.
Supposing that the ball is at the largest positive point at time \(t=0\text{.}\) Then, we can write the \(x\) and \(y\)-coordinates of the ball at an arbitrary time as
\begin{align*} \amp x(t) = x_0 + R \cos(\omega t),\\ \amp y(t) = y_0 + R \sin(\omega t), \end{align*}
where \(\omega = v_s/R\) is the angular speed of the ball.
The position vector \(\vec r(t)\) will be
\begin{equation*} vec r(t) = \left[ x_0 + R \cos(\omega t) \right] \hat u_x + \left[y_0 + R \sin(\omega t)\right] \hat u_y. \end{equation*}
Solution 2. b
Taking the time derivative of \(\vec r\) will give the velocity of the ball.
\begin{equation*} \vec v(t) = \frac{d\vec r}{dt} = \left[ - R\omega \sin(\omega t) \right] \hat u_x + \left[ R\omega \cos(\omega t)\right] \hat u_y. \end{equation*}
Solution 3. c
Taking the time derivative of \(\vec v\) will give the acceleration of the ball.
\begin{equation*} \vec a(t) = \frac{d\vec v}{dt} = - R\omega^2 \left[ \cos(\omega t) \hat u_x + \sin(\omega t) \hat u_y \right]. \end{equation*}
Note that \(\cos(\omega t) \hat u_x + \sin(\omega t) \hat u_y\) is the unit vector pointed from the center of the circle towards the ball. Therefore, if a ball moves uniformly in a circle, the acceleration has the magnitude \(R\omega^2\) and the direction is pointed towards the center of the circle as expected.
