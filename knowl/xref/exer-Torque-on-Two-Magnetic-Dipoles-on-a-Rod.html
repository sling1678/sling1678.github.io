Exercise 4. Torque on Two Magnetic Dipoles on a Rod.

Two identical magnetic dipoles, of magnetic moment \(\mu\) each, are attached to a light rod of length \(L\) pivoted in the middle. The rod is then placed in a magnetic field \(B\) perpendicular to the rod as shown in FigureÂ 35.46. If the moment of inertia of the rods about the center is \(I_0\text{,}\) find the angular acceleration in each case.
Answer.
(c) \(2 \mu B/I_0\text{.}\)
Solution.
The torque on each dipole will tend to rotate the dipole. Since the rod is pivoted at the center only in the situation (c) we will get any net torque about the pivot point.
(c) The torques on the two dipoles add here, giving \(\tau_\text{net} = 2 \mu B\text{.}\) Therefore, the angular acceleration has the magnitude \(\alpha = \tau_\text{net}/I_0 = 2 \mu B/I_0\text{.}\)
