Exercise 6. Complex Circuit Analysis of an RL Circuit 2.

Determine current in each branch, power dissipated in the resistor, and power produced by the source for the circuit given in Figure 41.52.
Solution.
As the hint suggests, we replace the resistance, the capacitance, and the inductance by their complex impedances and treat the circuit as a ``purely resistive’’ circuit. The net impedance of the resistor and capacitor in parallel is
\begin{equation*} Z_{\parallel} = \dfrac{-iR/\omega C}{R -i/\omega C} = \dfrac{-iR }{\omega RC -i}= |Z_{\parallel}|e^{i\phi_{\parallel}}, \end{equation*}
with
\begin{equation*} |Z_{\parallel}| = \dfrac{R}{\sqrt{1+\omega^2R^2C^2}},\ \ \phi_{\parallel} = -\tan^{-1} (\omega R C). \end{equation*}
We need to add the impedance of the inductor to obtain the impedance across the source.
\begin{align*} Z \amp = Z_L + Z_{\parallel} = i\omega L + \dfrac{-iR }{\omega RC -i} \\ \amp = (63.6 + i 1224)\:\Omega = 1226\:\Omega\angle 1.51\:\textrm{rad}. \end{align*}
Therefore, the current \(I\) written as \(I_0\:\cos(\omega t + \phi)\) will have
\begin{equation*} I_0 = \dfrac{170\:\textrm{V}}{1226\:\Omega} = 0.139\:\textrm{A},\ \ \phi = -1.51\:\textrm{rad}. \end{equation*}
To find the currents \(I_1\) and \(I_2\) we can use the current divider formula. For the complex currents we get
\begin{equation*} I_1 = \dfrac{Z_C}{Z_R + Z_C} I,\ \ I_1 = \dfrac{Z_R}{Z_R + Z_C} I. \end{equation*}
The calculation for the amplitude and phase of \(I_1\) will go as follows.
\begin{equation*} I_1 = \dfrac{-i/\omega C}{R -i/\omega C}\times I_0\angle \phi\:\textrm{rad}. \end{equation*}
Now, we can rewrite the multiplier of the current on the right side as an amplitude and an angle
\begin{equation*} \dfrac{-i/\omega C}{R -i/\omega C} = A\:\angle \theta. \end{equation*}
Then, the amplitude and the phase of the current \(I_1\) will be
\begin{equation*} I_1 = A\times I_0\:\angle (\phi+\theta). \end{equation*}
Other current can be determined similarly. From the currents and the corresponding impedances we can deduce the EMF across each element. A student can use these to calculate the power produced by the source and the power dissipated in the resistor.
