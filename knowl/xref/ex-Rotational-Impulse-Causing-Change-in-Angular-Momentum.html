Example 9.114. Rotational Impulse Causing Change in Angular Momentum.

The plot in Figure 9.115 shows net torque (with countercloclwise positive) on a disk of moment of inertia \(5.0\text{ kg.m}^2\) as a function of time. At \(t=0\text{,}\) the disk is rotating at angular velocity \(2.0\text{ rad/s}\) in the clockwise direction. What is the magnitude and sense of rotation of the angular velocity at \(t = 5.0\text{ sec}\text{?}\)
Answer.
\(1.0\text{ rad/s}\) in the clockwise direction.
Solution.
We will use \(J_\text{rot}^\text{net} = \Delta L\) with \(J_\text{rot}^\text{net} = \) area under the curve, \(\Delta = (I\omega)_f - (I\omega)_i \) to find \(\omega_f\text{.}\) The area above zero torque give positive impulse and that below give negative impulse.
\begin{equation*} J_\text{rot}^\text{net} = \dfrac{1}{2}\times 10 \times 3 + (-5\times 2) = 15-10 = 5\text{ N.m.s}. \end{equation*}
Now, note that \(\omega_i = -2\text{ rad/s}\) since it is clockwise and we are using counterclockwise sense as positive. Therefore, rotational impulse/change in angular momentum gives
\begin{equation*} 5\,\omega_f - 5 \times (-2) - 5. \end{equation*}
Therefore,
\begin{equation*} \omega_f = -1.0\text{ rad/s}. \end{equation*}
Therefore, the final angular velocity is \(1.0\text{ rad/s}\) in the clockwise direction.
