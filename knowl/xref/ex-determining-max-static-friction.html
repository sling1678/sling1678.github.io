Example 6.47. Maximum Static Friction.

A book of mass \(1.5\text{ kg}\) is at rest on a horizontal table. The book is then pushed with a force at an angle of \(30^{\circ}\) below the horizontal direction.
When magnitude of the force is increased, we find that, once the magnitude reaches \(25\text{ N}\text{,}\) the book starts to slide on the table.
(a) What are the magnitudes of normal and static friction forces between the book and the table?
(b) What is the value of the coefficient of static friction force between the book and the table?
Answer.
(a) \(F_N = 27.22\text{ N},\ \ F_s = 21.65\text{ N}\text{,}\) (b) 0.8.
Solution 1. a
We follow exact same steps explained in ExampleÂ 6.43. They lead to the following equations of motion. You should work out the detials.
\begin{equation*} F_N = 27.22\text{ N},\ \ F_s = 21.65\text{\text N}. \end{equation*}
Solution 2. b
This \(F_s\) is actually \(F_s^{\text{max}} \) since the book will slide if the applied force was any greater.
\begin{equation*} F_s^{\text{max}} = 21.65\text{\text N}. \end{equation*}
From \(F_s^{\text{max}} \) and \(F_N\) we get \(\mu_s \text{.}\)
\begin{equation*} \mu_s = \dfrac{F_s^{\text{max}}}{F_N} = \dfrac{21.65}{27.22} = 0.8. \end{equation*}
