Exercise 5. Average Distance Between Sun and Mercury.
An astronomical unit (AU) is equal to the average distance between Earth and Sun. It is approximately equal to 150 million kilometers. Evaluate the average distances (a) from Sun to Mercury, (b) from Sun to Mars, and (c) Sun to Jupiter in AU if they are \(5.79 \times 10^{10}\ \text{m}\text{,}\) \(2.28 \times 10^{11}\ \text{m}\) and \(7.78 \times 10^{11}\ \text{m}\) respectively.
Hint.
In AU, the distance from the Sun to Earth is \(1.0\, \text{AU}\text{.}\)
Answer.
(a) \(d_\text{S-Mer}=0.386\, AU\text{,}\) (b) \(d_\text{S-Mar}=1.52\ AU\text{,}\) (c) \(d_\text{S-J}=5.19\ AU\) .
Solution 1. (a)
\begin{equation*} d_\text{S-Mer} = 5.79 \times 10^{10}\ \text{m}\times \frac{1\, \text{AU}}{1.50\times 10^{11}\,\text{m} } = 0.386\,\text{AU}. \end{equation*}
Solution 2. (b)
\begin{equation*} d_\text{S-Mar} = 2.28 \times 10^{11}\ \text{m}\times \frac{1\, \text{AU}}{1.50\times 10^{11}\,\text{m} } = 1.52\,\text{AU}. \end{equation*}
Solution 3. (c)
\begin{equation*} d_\text{S-J} = 7.78 \times 10^{11}\ \text{m}\times \frac{1\, \text{AU}}{1.50\times 10^{11}\,\text{m} } = 5.19\,\text{AU}. \end{equation*}
