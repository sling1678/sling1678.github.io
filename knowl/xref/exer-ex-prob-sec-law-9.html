Exercise 2. Varying Tension in a Hanging Chain.

A long chain consists of links of mass \(m\) each. The chain is hung from a tower. What is the tension in the chain at the \(n^{\text{th}}\) link from the bottom?
Answer.
\(n m g\text{.}\)
Solution.
There are two forces on the \(n^{\text{th}}\) link, the weight of \(n\) links below, which is pointed down and the tension \(T_n\) at the site pointed up.
\begin{equation*} T_n = n m g\ \ \ ( n = 1, 2,\cdots, N ). \end{equation*}
