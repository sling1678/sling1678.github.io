Exercise 3. U-Tube with Two Different Fluids.

An open U-tube has uniform cross-sectional area of 3 square cm and same height on both ends. Mercury is poured in it till the height from the top is 50-cm. How much water must be poured on one side so that the level of the mercury rises by 1 cm on the other side?
Answer.
\(81.6\, \text{cm}^3\text{.}\)
Solution.
The figure shows the situation after water has been poured in on the left side of the tube so that mercury levels are no longer same on the two sides. Let \(h_1\) be the height of the water in the tube and \(h_2\) be the height of the mercury with respect to its height on the other side of the U-tube. Let us denote the densities of water and mercury by \(\rho_1\) and \(\rho_2\) respectively.
Now we apply Pascalâ€™s principle to the mercury. The pressure at any level in either side of the tube will be the same as long as the two levels are in the same fluid. Refering to the figure we see that the pressure of the level marked \(p_{\textrm{ref}}\) has two expressions, one on the left side using the water height above this reference level and another expression on the right side using the mercury height. Denoting the atmospheric pressure by \(p_0\) we have
\begin{align*} \amp p_{\textrm{ref}} = p_{0} + \rho_1 g h_1,\\ \amp p_{\textrm{ref}} = p_{0} + \rho_2 g h_2. \end{align*}
Therefore,
\begin{equation*} \rho_1 h_1 = \rho_2 h_2 \end{equation*}
Solving for \(h_1\) we obtain
\begin{equation*} h_1 = \left( \frac{ \rho_2}{\rho_1} \right) h_2. \end{equation*}
In the problem statement \(h_2\) is \(2\times 1\) cm since mercury is supposed to rise 1 cm from the instance when the levels were equal on the two sides. Therefore, the height of the water column will be
\begin{equation*} h_1 = \left( \frac{ 13.6\ \textrm{g/cc}}{1.0\ \textrm{g/cc}} \right)2\ \textrm{cm} = 27.2 \ \textrm{cm}. \end{equation*}
Now, the levels on the top on the two sides are 50 cm + 1 cm - 27.2 cm = 23.8 cm on the water-side and 50 cm - 1 cm = 49 cm on the mercury side. The volume of the water poured in will be \(27.2\times 3 = 81.6 \text{cm}^3\text{.}\)
