Exercise 3. Power of Magnifying Glass from Observed Magnification.

You perceive an angular magnification of a magnifying glass to be \(10\times\text{.}\) Suppose your near point is \(25\text{ cm}\text{.}\) What is the power in unit \(\text{diopter}\) of the magnifying glass?
Hint.
Use Eq. (45.11) and convert \(1/f\) to power.
Answer.
\(45\text{ diopters}\text{.}\)
Solution.
Letâ€™s write Eq. (45.11) in terms of \(1/f\) with \(f\) in meter. With near point in meter, we get
\begin{equation*} M = 1 + \frac{0.25\text{ m}}{f} = 1 + .25\, P. \end{equation*}
Therefore,
\begin{equation*} P = \frac{M-1}{0.25} = \frac{10-1}{0.25} = 45\text{ diopters}. \end{equation*}
You can see for higher magnification, \(M \approx n_p\, P\text{,}\) where \(n_p\) is the near point in \(m\text{.}\)
