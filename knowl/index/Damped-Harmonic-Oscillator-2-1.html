Any frictional force will damp the motion, but viscous drag is a particularly easier damping force to work with analytically.
\begin{equation} \vec F_{visc} = -b\ \vec v\ \ \ (b \ge 0), \tag{13.44} \end{equation}
where \(b\) is the proportionality constant that depends on the viscosity of the medium and the geometry of the moving object. We have a minus sign in Eq. (13.44) since drag opposes velocity.
