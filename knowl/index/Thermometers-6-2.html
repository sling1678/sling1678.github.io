A Thermometer is sometimes calibrated to operate over a small range of temperatures by picking two reproducible thermal conditions of known temperatures. Readily available thermal conditions are the conditions that exist in which a material can coexist between two different material phases, such as solid and liquid, or three different phases simultaneously, such as the triple point.
