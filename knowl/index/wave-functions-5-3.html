Real waves, such as water waves, a pulse traveling on a string, or sound of a drum are not sinusoidal waves. They can be of any shape. For instance, suppose a very long taut string is given a snap at one end once while the end is held fixed in one place to provide tension in the string (FigureÂ 14.22). This results in a bump in the string, which moves towards the other end. This is also a wave, although it does not look like the sine or cosine function. We call such a wave a pulse.
