We will denote the rolling friction force by \(\vec F_r \) and the magnitude by the same symbol without the arrow. Since, rolling friction is a static friction, we have a limiting value, which will be denoted by \(F_r^{\text{max}}\text{.}\) A coefficient of rolling friction, \(\mu_r \text{,}\) can be defined similarly to static and kinetic friction coefficients.
\begin{equation} \mu_r = \dfrac{F_r^{\text{max}}}{F_N}.\tag{6.21} \end{equation}
We will study examples of rolling force when we study rolling motion in the chapter on rotation.
