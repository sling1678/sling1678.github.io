The product of dielectric constant and permittivity of vacuum, i.e., \(\epsilon_r\epsilon_0\text{,}\) is called the permittivity of the dielectric, just as \(\epsilon_0\) is called the permittivity of vacuum or free space. Permittivity is denoted by the same symbol \(\epsilon\) without the subscript zero.
\begin{equation} \epsilon = \epsilon_0\, \epsilon_r.\tag{33.16} \end{equation}
You can read this definition as saying that dielectric constant is permittivity of the medium relative to the permittivity of vacuum.
