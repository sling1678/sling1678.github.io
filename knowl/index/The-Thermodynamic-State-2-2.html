Whenever you place your system in a new environment, thermodynamic properties take time to stabilize. During the change, it is often difficult to state thermodynamic properties with any degree of accuracy. That is, we are unable to specify the thermodynamic state unless the thermodynamic properties of the system have stopped changing. When values of thermodynamic properties have stabilized, we say that the system has reached a thermodynamic equilibrium and the state corresponding to these conditions are called equilibrium states.
