This experimental situation presents the following conditions on the ends of the string, called boundary conditions. With \(y\) for the displacement, \(x\) axis horizontal with origin at the node, the condition on the vibration function will be
\begin{align} \amp y(x,t)|_{x=-\epsilon} = A_0\ cos(\omega t),\tag{14.38}\\ \amp y(x,t)|_{x=L} = 0.\tag{14.39} \end{align}
Here \(\epsilon \lt\lt \lambda\) and you can think of vibrator being “at the node”. When frequency \(\omega\) of the driver is near the frequency of a normal mode, the amplitude \(A\) is much larger than \(A_0\text{,}\) which is the amplitude of the tip of the vibrator, due to resonance.
