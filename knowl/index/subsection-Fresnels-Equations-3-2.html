The amplitudes of reflected and transmitted waves depend on the direction of the electric field. This direction is given with respect to the plane containing the directions of the incident, reflected, and transmitted waves as shown in FigureÂ 46.9. This plane is called plane of incidence. If electric field is perpendicular to the plane of incidence, we say the wave is S polarized or transverse electric or TE. If the direction of electric field is in the plane of incidence, then the direction of magnetic field will be perpendicular to the plane of incidence - we call these waves P poarized or transverse magnetic or TM.
