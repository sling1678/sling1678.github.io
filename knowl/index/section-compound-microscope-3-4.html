Multiplying these two sources of magnification gives us the magnification of the microscope, which will be the angular magnification.
\begin{equation} M_\text{microscope} = m_\text{obj} \times M_\text{eyepiece/eye} = -\frac{q_\text{o}\left(f_\text{e} + n_p \right)}{ f_\text{o} f_\text{e}}.\tag{45.13} \end{equation}
Thus, if you have a \(50\text{X}\) objective and a \(10\text{X}\) eyepiece, you will get a microscope that has \(500\text{X}\) magnification. The magnification of the microscope is sometimes referred to as the magnifying power.
