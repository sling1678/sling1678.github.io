The rate of heating of a resistor can be calculated by using the principle of conservation of energy. Suppose current \(I\) flows through a cylindrical wire across which a voltage difference \(\Delta \phi=\phi_1-\phi_2\) is maintained (FigureÂ 34.22). Let \(n\) be the number of conduction electrons per unit volume and \(v\) their average drift speed. Let \(A\) be the area of cross-section and \(R\) the resistance of the wire. As a result of the current in the wire, electrons in the wire will move a distance \(v \Delta t\) in time \(\Delta t\text{.}\)
