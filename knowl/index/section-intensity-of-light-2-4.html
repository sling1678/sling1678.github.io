Dividing Eq. (46.16) by \(A\Delta t\text{,}\) will give us power flow per unit corss-sectional area. This is called power flux and denoted by \(S\text{.}\) Using various equations above, we can express it as
\begin{align} S \amp = \frac{ u A c\Delta t }{A \Delta t} = u c, \tag{46.17}\\ \amp = c \epsilon_0 E_0^2 \cos^2 (k x - \omega t + \phi). \tag{46.18}\\ \amp = \frac{1}{2}c \epsilon_0 E_0^2 \left[ 1 + \cos (2k x - 2\omega t + 2\phi)\right]. \tag{46.19} \end{align}
