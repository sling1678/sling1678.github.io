Rumford also calculated the amount of heat produced in the boring of a cannon and related it to the mechanical energy put in for boring thereby finding the first reported value of the mechanical equivalent of heat. His value expressed in the unit of heat (calories) and the British unit of mechanical energy (Foot-pound) would be \(3.15 \) foot-pound per calorie, which is approximately \(1.5\%\) higher than the presently accepted value. Count Rumford also invented many other useful devices, such as the kitchen range, the double boiler, the pressure-cooker and the drip coffee maker.
