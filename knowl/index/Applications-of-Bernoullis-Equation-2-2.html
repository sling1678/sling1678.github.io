\begin{equation} p_1 + \rho g h_1 = p_2 + \rho g h_2. \ \ \text{(Static)}\tag{18.16} \end{equation}
If the area of pipe at the entrance and exit points are same, the speed of flow will be same, canceling the kinetic energy terms. This results in the same equation.
\begin{equation} p_1 + \rho g h_1 = p_2 + \rho g h_2. \ \ \text{(Same Pipe Size at Ends)}\tag{18.17} \end{equation}
This says that work by the pressure difference between two points in a fluid at rest of having same cross-sections, provides the difference in the potential energy.
\begin{equation} p_1 -p_1 = \rho g \left( h_2 - h_1 \right). \tag{18.18} \end{equation}
