An easier way to observe the phase difference is through the use of a polar plot, called the phasor diagram. Note that a sinusoidal function can be represented by components of a vector, the \(x\)-component for cosine and the \(y\)-component for the sine. To compare two sinusoidal functions in their vector representations, first you must convert all the sines and cosines to either all sines or all cosines. In our present study we will convert them all into cosines. Then, the \(x\)-component of these vectors will contain the physical information.
