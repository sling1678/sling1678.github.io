When the rays of light from a single point of the object interact with an optical device, such as a mirror or lens, new rays emerge from the optical device due to reflection and refraction effects as we have seen in the last chapter. These new rays whose origin is same point of the object will either converge to a point in space or appear to diverge away from a point in space. This point in space is the image point of the point on the object.
