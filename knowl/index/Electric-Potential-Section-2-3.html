To get a feel for the concept, consider moving a charge \(+Q\) near another charge \(+q\text{,}\) which remains fixed in its place, as shown in FigureÂ 31.1. Suppose you move \(+Q\) so that your force on \(+Q\) is only an infinitesimally greater than needed to balance the electric force on \(+Q\text{;}\) that is, we maintain net force on \(+Q\) zero and hence its speed will not change. Then, by conservation of energy, work done by you will go towards changing the potential energy of \(Q\text{.}\) Since, we worked against electric force only, this potential energy is called electric potential energy (EPE).
\begin{equation} \text{Change in EPE} = \text{Work done against electric force}.\tag{31.1} \end{equation}
