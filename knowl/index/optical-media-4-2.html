The refractive index \(n\text{,}\) or equivalently, the dielectric constant \(\epsilon_r\text{,}\) is often a function of wavelength \(\lambda\) of light. For instance, the refractive index of flint glass for red light is 1.62 while for a violet light it is 1.67. This difference is responsible for the separation of colors when sun light passes through a prism. The dependence of refractive index on frequency is called dispersion of light.
