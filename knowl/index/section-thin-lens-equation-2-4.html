Locating the Final Image Graphically: Locating the image graphically as a result of refraction through a lens is straightforward. As usual we draw two rays of light \(\text{PV}_1\text{V}_2\text{Q}_1\) and \(\text{PXYQ}\) tracing their paths through the lens making sure to take into account the refractions at the interfaces according to the Snell’s law. We find that the rays intersect at point \(\text{Q}\) for the case shown in Figure 44.56. The crossing of real rays at point Q generate a real image there.
