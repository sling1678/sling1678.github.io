The thermodynamic state of a system can change as a result of interaction with the environment. We call the process of change a thermodynamic process. For instance, you can change pressure, temperature, and/or volume of a gas by heating or cooling the gas. The state of the system may change gradually and in a well-defined way or change very abruptly and unknown way. In this section we will study some commonly used processes for studying thermodynamics of gases.
