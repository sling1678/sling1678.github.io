Imagine shining a monochromatic light through a narrow hole of width a little larger than the wavelength. Rather than a simple shadow behind the hole, you will find that there is an interference pattern even though there is aparently only one source of light. This is illustrated in the diagram given in Figure 48.1. How’s that possible? The wavefront emerging behind the hole must consist of many waves for them to produce interference pattern.
