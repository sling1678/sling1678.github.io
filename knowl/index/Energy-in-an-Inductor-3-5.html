If magnetic field is not uniform, then the energy in the magnetic field in a particular space volume will be given by an integral over the volume.
\begin{equation*} U_B = \frac{1}{2\mu_0} \int_{\text{Volume}} B^2 dV\ \ \ \text{(Energy in magnetic field)} \end{equation*}
The formula for the energy in the magnetic field is similar to that of energy in the electric field.
\begin{equation*} U_E = \frac{1}{2} \epsilon_0\int_{\text{Volume}} E^2 dV. \ \ \ \text{(Energy in electric field)} \end{equation*}
The sum of energy in electric and magnetic field is called the electromagnetic energy, or energy in the electromagnetic field, \(U_{em}\text{.}\)
\begin{equation*} U_{em} = U_E + U_B. \end{equation*}
