Dividing by \(\Delta t\) we obtain the correct equation of motion for a rocket, which is sometimes called rocket equation.
\begin{equation*} m \frac{\Delta v_x}{\Delta t} = u \frac{|\Delta m|}{\Delta t}. \end{equation*}
Now, when we take \(\Delta t \rightarrow 0\) limit, we obtain the equation at instant \(t\) as a differential equation. Here, we need to observe that \(|\Delta m|/\Delta t\) should be positive but mass is decreasing. So, \(|\Delta m|/\Delta t\) will go over to \(-dm/dt\) rather than \(dm/dt\text{.}\)
\begin{equation} m \frac{d v_x}{d t} = - u \frac{d m}{d t}.\tag{7.66} \end{equation}
