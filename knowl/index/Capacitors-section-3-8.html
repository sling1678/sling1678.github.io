Below, we will show that the energy required to charge a capacitor from neutral to a final charge \(\pm Q_f\) with final potential difference between plates \(V_f\) is given by
\begin{equation} U = \frac{1}{2}\ Q_f V_f.\tag{32.7} \end{equation}
This is the amount of electrostatic energy stored in the capacitor. We can write this in couple of other forms by using the capacitor formula, \(Q=CV\text{.}\)
\begin{equation} U = \frac{1}{2}\ C V_f^2 = \frac{1}{2}\frac{Q_f^2}{C}.\tag{32.8} \end{equation}
