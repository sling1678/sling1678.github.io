At the interface of two media some or the entire incident light manages to enter the second medium as shown in Figure 43.22. The light ray in the second medium is called the refracted ray. The direction of the refracted is given by the angle (\(\theta_2\)) the refracted ray makes with the normal as shown in the figure. The angle of refraction \(\theta_2\) is related to the angle of incidence and the refractive indices \(n_1\) and \(n_2\) of the two media by the law of refraction, also called the Snell’s law.
\begin{equation} n_1\ \sin\theta_1 = n_2\ \sin\theta_2.\tag{43.25} \end{equation}
According to Snell’s Law, if \(n_2 \lt n_1\) then \(\theta_2 \gt \theta_1\) and if \(n_2 \gt n_1\) then \(\theta_2 \lt \theta_1\) as shown in Figure 43.23. Remember: Higher \(n\) smaller \(\theta\). Thus, if a ray of light in air (\(n_1 = 1.0\)) is incident on water (\(n_2 = 1.33\)), it will bend towards the normal, but if a ray of light enter water from its path in glass (\(n_1 = 1.5\)), it will bend away from the normal.
