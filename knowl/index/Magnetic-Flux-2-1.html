Before we look more closely upon Faraday’s law, let us study magnetic flux, which will be used to state the law. Similar to the electric field flux, magnetic flux through a surface is a measure of number magnetic field lines that pass through that surface. You may recall from your study of electric flux that relative orientation of the surface and direction of the field is important for flux. For instance, if a field is parallel to a surface, there will be no flux, and if a field is perpendicular to the surface, it will have maximum flux as shown in Figure 38.5. At other angles, the flux will be in-between.
