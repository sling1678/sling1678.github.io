Turn on the faucet and collect water in a bucket. Suppose it takes \(15\text{ sec}\) to fill a \(4\text{-liter}\) bucket. Dividing the volume by the time, i.e., \(16\text{ liter/min} \text{,}\) gives us an important measure of flow, called the volume rate of flow. This is denoted by letter \(Q\text{.}\)
\begin{equation} Q = \dfrac{\Delta V}{\Delta t}\tag{18.1} \end{equation}
