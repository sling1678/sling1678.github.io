The unit of kilogram defined by an artefact has some intrinsic problems. For instance, the prototype may be damaged or corrode due to oxidation or other wear and tear. As a result of these problems, the prototype is said to be gaining approximately 1 micro-gram per year. Therefore, it is hard to keep the standard kilogram constant to a very high degree of precision. Presently, several new methods for defining kilogram are being investigated. A particularly attractive possibility is to define the kilogram based on the mass of a fixed number of molecules of a substance that can be made with high purity.
