Sign Conventions for lenses:
  1. \(p\) positive if left of the lens otherwise negative.
  2. \(q\) positive if image to the right of the lens otherwise negative.
  3. \(f\) positive for convex lens and negative for concave lens.
By using a finite size object on the axis you can prove that the magnification of image defined as the ratio of the image height to the object height is equal to the negative of the image distance to object distance \(-q/p\text{.}\)
