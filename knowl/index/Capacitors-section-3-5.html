The unit of capacitance in SI system of units is Coulomb/Volt or Farad, named after the English physicist Michael Faraday.
\begin{equation*} 1\ \text{Farad} = 1\ \frac{\text{Coulomb}}{\text{Volt}}\ \Leftrightarrow\ 1\ \text{F} = 1\ \frac{\text{C}}{\text{V}}. \end{equation*}
It turns out that 1 Farad is too big a unit for laboratory use. Usually one uses micro-farad (\(\mu\text{F}\)) or pico-farad (\(\text{pF}\)) in the lab.
