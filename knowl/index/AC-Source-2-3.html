In this chapter we will study AC circuits where the current in a circuit is driven by an oscillating electromotive force, which we will refer to as voltage. To be consistent with tradition, we are going to use symbol \(V\) instead of symbol \(\mathcal{E}\) for EMF and voltage.
\begin{equation*} V(t) = V_0 \cos(2\pi f t + \phi), \end{equation*}
where \(V_0\) is the amplitude of the oscillating voltage, \(f\) the frequency of oscillations, and \(\phi\) the phase constant. Amplitude \(V_0\) is also called peak voltage of the source. The argument of cosine, here \((2\pi f t + \phi)\) is called the phase of the source, which obviously deends on time \(t\text{.}\) Beware, sometimes the phase constant \(\phi\) itself is called the phase and that is very confusing.
