We are often interested in power in a particular direction (FigureÂ 43.10). The amount of power emitted per unit solid angle of a light source is called its radiant intensity \(I_R\text{.}\) The SI unit of radiant intensity is Watts per steradian (\(\text{W/sr}\)). Let \(\Delta P\) be the power of light in the solid angle \(\Delta \Omega\text{,}\) then the radiant intensity is given by
\begin{equation} I_R = \frac{\Delta P}{\Delta \Omega}\ \ \ \text{(Radiant Intensity)}\tag{43.7} \end{equation}
If the source is isotropic, i.e., emits same power in all directions, then the total optical energy produced per second will be \(4\pi I_R\text{.}\)
