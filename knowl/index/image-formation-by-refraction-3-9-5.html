FigureÂ 44.42 shows that there exists another special point \(\text{F}_2\) in medium \(n_2\) such that if you place the object very far away such that the rays from the object striking the convex surface can be considered to be parallel, e.g., source can be sunlight from the Sun. Then, we find that these rays, pass through point \(\text{F}_2\) and continue. This means the image of a such a source at infinity \(p=\infty\text{,}\) is at the second focal point, i.e., \(q=f_2\text{.}\) Point \(\text{F}_2\) is called second focal point and distance of \(\text{F}_2\) from the vertex is called the second focal length.
