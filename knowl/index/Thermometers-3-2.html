Since 1954 the triple point of water, a state in which a mixture of ice, liquid water and vapor coexist in equilibrium, has been chosen as the standard reference which is arbitrarily assigned a value of \(273.16\text{K}\text{.}\) This fixes the value of constant \(a \) in Eq. (19.12) in terms of the assigned value of the temperature of triple point and the value of \(X\) at \(X=X_{\text{TP}}\) of the thermometric property when the thermometer is in equilibrium at the triple point of water.
\begin{equation} 273.16\text{K} = a\, X_{\text{TP}} \label{}\tag{19.13} \end{equation}
Eliminating \(a \) from Eqs. (19.12) and (19.13), we are able to immediately determine temperature \(t \) when the value of the thermometric property \(X \) is known.
\begin{equation} t(X) = \left(\frac{273.16\text{K}}{X_{\text{TP}}} \right)\ X \tag{19.14} \end{equation}
