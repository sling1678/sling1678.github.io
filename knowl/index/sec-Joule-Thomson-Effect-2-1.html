Imagine pumping a gas through a thermally insulated tube that has a porous plug such that the pressure on the two sides of the plug is different. Let \(p_1\) be the pressure on one side of the plug and a different pressure \(p_2\) on the other side as shown in FigureÂ 24.24. We will take \(p_1 \gt p_2\) for the discussion below. Let the pressure on each side be maintained steady by a pump and the entire system be thermally insulated. The process of pumping a gas this way is called Joule-Thomson process or Joule-Thomson throttling.
