As shown in FigureÂ 43.22, the directions of incident and reflected rays are given by angles with respect to the normal to the interface. Thus, incidence angle \(\theta_1\) made by the incident ray with the normal is called the angle of incidence, and angle \(\theta_1^{\prime}\) made by the reflected ray is called the angle of reflection. The law of reflection asserts that the two angles are equal to each other.
\begin{equation} \theta_1^{\prime} = \theta_1.\tag{43.24} \end{equation}
We will try to use the common convention - add a prime to designate a reflected angle for the corresponding incident angle.
