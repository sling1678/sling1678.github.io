Amount of heat needed to change temperature depends on other properties in addition to amount of material, the temperature, and the material itself. For instance, heat to raise the temperature of a gas is different if you keep the volume constant versus if you keep the volume constant. Therefore, we have specific heat at constant volume, denoted by \(c_V\) and specific heat at constant pressure, denoted by \(c_P\text{.}\)
