Soon people began to wonder whether the reverse was also true, i.e., a magnetic field would produce an electric current as well. Most of experiments to test this reverse process didn’t work out. For instance, strong magnets next to a wire failed to produce any detectable current in the wire. Even the magnetic field produced by large steady currents in a wire failed to cause any current in a loop of wire next to the first wire. We call these experiments null experiments since the result is zero. They are illustrated in Figure 38.1. The key was using the changing magnetic field as we will see below.
