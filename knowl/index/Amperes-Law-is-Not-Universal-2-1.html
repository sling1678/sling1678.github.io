The four basic laws of electricity and magnetism were discovered experimentally. While studying them James Clerk Maxwell(1831-1879) discovered some logical inconsistencies. The source of inconsistencies turned out to be the incompleteness of Ampere’s law. Recall that according to Ampere’s law the circulation of magnetic field around a loop C is proportional to the current passing through any surface (S) whose boundary is the loop C itself.
\begin{equation} \oint_C \vec B\cdot d\vec l = \mu_0 I_{\text{enc}} \ \ \text{(independent of surface)}.\tag{42.1} \end{equation}
Although, there are infinitely many surfaces that can be attached to any loop, it is important to realize that Ampere’s law stated in Eq. (42.1) is independent of the choice of surface as illustrated in Figure 42.1.
