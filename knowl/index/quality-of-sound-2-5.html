The reason you are not able to find a pitch in a noise is that it usually consists of a continuous sound spectrum unlike sound of a musical note which consists of a particular frequency and its overtones. If you plot the intensity versus frequency in a noise, you will get a continuous graph, rather than spikes at the fundamental and overtones in the case of musical notes (see FigureÂ 15.7). The plot of the intensity versus frequency is also called the power spectrum.
