Resistors, capacitors, and inductors are called passive elements of a circuit while EMF source is called the active element. In the absence of external magnetic field through the surface of a circuit, Faraday’s law simplifies to a rule similar to Kirchhoff’s voltage rule, which we might call pseudo-Kirchhoff’s voltage law, as long as we take care of back-emf voltage drop due to inductance of the circuit as we will see below. If you place the AC circuit in an external dynamic magnetic field whose flux may be varying, then you better include the rate of magnetic flux also as required by Farady’s law.
