FigureÂ 43.21 shows two types of reflection - specular and diffuse. When the interface is smooth the reflected ray is sharp whose direction is symmetrical with respect to the incident ray. Such a reflection is called specular reflection, from the word Speculum for mirror in Latin. This is what happens when a visible light ray is reflected from a mirror. When the reflection is not sharp it is called a diffuse reflection, e.g. reflection from a ground glass. You are reading this book from diffuse reflected light from the paper, which makes it possible to read the same letter from different angles.
