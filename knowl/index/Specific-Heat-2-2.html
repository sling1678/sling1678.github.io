Specific heat of a material is defined as the heat capacity of a material per unit mass. Dividing heat capacity in Eq. (22.1) by mass \(M \) of the sample will give the specific heat. Specific heat is usually denoted by the small letter \(c\text{.}\)
\begin{equation} \text{Specific Heat, }c = \dfrac{1}{M}\left( \dfrac{Q}{\Delta T}\right).\tag{22.2} \end{equation}
