The vector rotates counterclockwise in time in keeping with the argument \(\omega t\) of cosine. The vector is called a phasor. When phasors corresponding to the driving EMF and the steady state voltage across the capacitor are plotted in the same diagram, the two phasors point in different directions, although both rotate at the same rate. If angle \(\phi \gt 0\text{,}\) then the phasor for \(V_S\) is said to be ahead of the phasor for \(V\text{,}\) while the opposite is the case when \(\phi \lt 0\text{.}\) In FigureÂ 40.37, you can examine a situation in which phasor \(V_S\) is ahead of phasor V.
