Example 8.27. Kinetic Energy In Different Frames.

A train is moving at speed \(3\text{ m/s}\) towards West. Inside the train, a toy car of mass \(0.2\text{ kg}\) is moving with speed \(4\text{ m/s}\)with respect to the train due North. (a) What is the kinetic energy of the toy with respect to the train? (b) What is the kinetic energy of the toy with respect to the ground?
Answer.
(a) \(1.6\text{ J}\text{,}\) (b) \(2.5\text{ J}\text{.}\)
Solution 1. (a)
Since speed with respect to train is already given in the problem, its easy.
\begin{equation*} K = \frac{1}{2}\times 0.2 \times 4^2 = 1.6\text{ J}. \end{equation*}
Solution 2. (b)
We need to find the speed with respect to the ground from the addition of the two velocities.
\begin{equation*} \vec v_\text{of car wrt ground} = \vec v_\text{of train wrt ground} + \vec v_\text{of car wrt train}. \end{equation*}
The magnitude of this velocity is needed for th eKE formula. This is a vector equation. Let West be positive \(x\) and North be positive \(y\) directions. Then, we get
\begin{equation*} \vec v_\text{of car wrt ground} = 3\,\hat i + 4\, \hat j. \end{equation*}
Therefore, speed of interest will be
\begin{equation*} v = \sqrt{3^2 + 4^2} = 5\text{ m/s}. \end{equation*}
Therefore, kinetic energy of the toy with respect to ground will be
\begin{equation*} K = \frac{1}{2}\times 0.2 \times 5^2 = 2.5\text{ J}. \end{equation*}
