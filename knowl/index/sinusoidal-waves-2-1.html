When a string is shaken sinusoidally, i.e., it is vibrated such that the oscillations are sine or cosine function of time, the wave propagated in the string also has sinusoidal shape as illustrated in FigureÂ 14.3. The period of the wave in space is called its wavelength, and it is usually denoted by the Greek letter \(\lambda\) (lambda). The absolute value of the displacement of the disturbance on either side of the equilibrium is called amplitude, usually denoted by letter \(A\text{.}\) Note that amplitude is not the entire range of displacement but half of that.
