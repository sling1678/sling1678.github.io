In many accelerator experiments, it is common practice to accelerate charged particles by placing the particle in an electric field. If you place a particle of charge \(q\) in electric field \(\vec E\text{,}\) the force on the particle will be given by
\begin{equation} \vec F_\text{on q} = q\:\vec E.\tag{29.28} \end{equation}
In this section we will work out examples of motion of particles when electric force is the only force on the particle. Later on, when we discuss magnetic force, we will look at another way we can change the motion of a particle based on its charge.
