Transverse Magnification \(M_T\) The size and orientation of the image with respect to the image is given by the magnification \(m\) defined as the ratio of image height \((h_i)\) to the object height \((h_o)\text{.}\)
\begin{equation} M_T \equiv \frac{h_i}{h_o}.\tag{44.36} \end{equation}
If magnification is negative then image is inverted with respect to the object. By using the geometry of the rays you can show that the ratio of the heights are related to the ratio of the image distance and the object distances.
\begin{equation} M_T = \frac{h_i}{h_o} = - \frac{q}{p}.\tag{44.37} \end{equation}
For the sake of brevity, we will often write \(m\) for \(M_T\text{.}\)
\begin{equation*} M_T \equiv m. \end{equation*}
