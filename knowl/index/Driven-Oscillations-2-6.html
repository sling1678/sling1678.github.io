Current in the circuit varies between \(+I_0\) and \(-I_0\text{.}\) We call \(I_0\) current amplitude. From Eq. (40.30), we conclude that current amplitude varies with the driving frequency, \(\omega\text{.}\) As you vary \(\omega\) of the EMF source, you will change \(I_0\) as illustrated in FigureÂ 40.24. The driving frequency at which you get the largest amplitude current occurs when
\begin{equation} \omega =\omega_0 = \dfrac{1}{\sqrt{LC}} \equiv \omega_R.\tag{40.33} \end{equation}
The observation that a frequency exists at which current fluctuates with the largest amplitude is called resonance of current and the special frequency, \(\omega_R\text{,}\) its resonance frequency. When you drive the circuit at the resonance frequency, current makes the largest oscillations about zero. We will see below that at this frequency, source is also able to deliver maximum power into the circuit by examining the resonance of average power.
