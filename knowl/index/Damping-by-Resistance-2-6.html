  1. Underdamped Case. If \(R/2L \lt 1/\sqrt{LC}\text{,}\) then current in the circuit oscillates about the equilibrium value of zero, successively damping out each cycle.
  2. Over-damped Case. If \(R/2L \gt 1/\sqrt{LC}\text{,}\) then current in the circuit does not oscillate; current just damps out to zero.
  3. Critically Damped Case. If \(R/2L = 1/\sqrt{LC}\text{,}\) then current in the circuit does not oscillate; current just damps out to zero rather quickly.
