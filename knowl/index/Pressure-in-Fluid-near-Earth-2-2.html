The atmosphere is invisible to the human eye, but it consists of gases and dust particles. The atmospheric pressure on your head may be assumed to come from the weight of all the gases above your head. Suppose \(W_\text{air}\) be the weight of all the air and \(A\) be the area of cross-section of your head, then the pressure on air on you rhead will be
\begin{equation*} p_{\text{head}} = \dfrac{W_{\text{air above}}}{A}. \end{equation*}
Of course, this will be same on any object, not just your head. This is atmospheric pressure.
