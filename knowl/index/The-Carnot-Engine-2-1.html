In a Carnot engine a fixed amount of ideal gas is heated and cooled in a particular cyclic process called the Carnot cycle. The Carnot cycle consists of four steps shown in FigureÂ 25.3.
  1. Isothermal expansion at high temperature \(T_H\text{.}\)
  2. Adiabatic expansion lowering temperature from \(T_H\) to \(T_C\text{.}\)
  3. Isothermal compression at lower temperature \(T_C\text{.}\)
  4. Adiabatic compression raising temperature from \(T_C\) to \(T_H\text{.}\)
The states A, B, C, and D are chosen so that at the end of these four steps, the system returns to state A.
