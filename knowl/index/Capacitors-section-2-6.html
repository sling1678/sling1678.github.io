Leiden jars were instrumental in early experiments on electricity. FigureÂ 32.31 shows a pair of Leiden jars used in a Wilmhurst mill for storing charges. Leiden jars can be charged to fairly high voltages, e.g., in a Wilmhurst mill. When wires connecting the two plates of a charged Leiden jar are brought near each other spectacular sparks from one wire to the other are often visible. Leiden jars are sometimes also called condensers.
