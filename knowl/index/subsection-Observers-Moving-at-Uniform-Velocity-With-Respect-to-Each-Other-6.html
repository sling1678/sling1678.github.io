The velocity vectors \(\vec v = (v_x, v_y, v_z)\) and \(\vec v' = (v'_x, v'_y, v'_z)\) are called relative velocity of P with respect to O and O’, respectively. Taking derivative of velocity components gives relations among the components of acceleration of P. Since relative speed \(V\) is constant, its derivative is zero. Hence, we get
\begin{align} \amp a_x = a'_x,\ \ (\text{since } \frac{dV}{dt} = 0.)\tag{4.64}\\ \amp a_y =a'_y, \tag{4.65}\\ \amp a_z = a'_z. \tag{4.66} \end{align}
The acceleration vectors \(\vec a = (a_x, a_y, a_z)\) and \(\vec a' = (a'_x, a'_y, a'_z)\) are called acceleraions of P in S and S’ respectively.
