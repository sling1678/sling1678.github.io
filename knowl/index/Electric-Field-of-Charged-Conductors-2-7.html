We find electric potential at a point P by integrating negative of the dot product of electric field with displacement from \(r=\infty\) (reference point) to the \(r=r\text{.}\) For a point P outside the conductor we will get
\begin{equation*} \phi(r_\text{out}) = -\int_\infty^{r_\text{out}}\, \frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0}\, \frac{dr}{r^2} = \frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0}\,\frac{q}{r_\text{out}} \end{equation*}
For a point P inside the sphere, the integral is nonzero upto the point \(r=R\) and zero when \(r \lt R\text{.}\) Therefore, we will get
\begin{equation*} \phi(r_\text{in}) = \frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0}\,\frac{q}{R}. \end{equation*}
