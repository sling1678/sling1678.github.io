Most things expand when heated and contract when cooled. When a solid bar is heated its length wil change if either of its ends is free to expand or contract. In almost all materials, the fractional change in length \(\Delta L / L\text{,}\) i.e., the strain, is proportional to the change in temperature \(\Delta T \text{.}\)
\begin{equation} \dfrac{\Delta L}{L} \propto \Delta T, \implies \dfrac{\Delta L}{L} = \alpha\, \Delta T,\tag{20.1} \end{equation}
