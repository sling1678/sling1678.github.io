If an object is considerable distance from the optical axis, light from it will be incident on the lens with varying obliqueness depending on the direction. The more oblique rays focus nearer the lens than the less oblique rays. As a result the image is blurred. This effect is called astigmatism. To understand astigmatism in a convex lens consider an object at an off-axis point, and define a reference plane called the meridional plane consisting of the chief ray (i.e. ray from the point object that passes through the center of the lens) and the optical axis (Fig. \ref="aberration-astigmatism"}).
